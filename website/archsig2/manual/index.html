Reading ArchSig output

From summary to evidence.

ArchSig output is structured so an agent can start with a compact summary, then open packet detail only where the review needs evidence, coverage, or exact measurement boundaries.

Artifacts

ArchSig reads observed architecture evidence with a selected interpretation profile, then emits a packet and summary for review.

  • archmap-observation-map-v0Source-grounded Atom observation evidence: atom observations, molecule observations, semantic observations, concern hints, gaps, provenance, and non-conclusions.
  • law-policy-v0The selected LawUniverse, witness rules, molecule patterns, obstruction definitions, signature axes, coverage requirements, and exactness assumptions.
  • archsig-analysis-packet-v0The full detail store: molecule readings, obstruction circuits, signature axes, flatness, repair candidates, coverage gaps, measurement families, detail refs, and non-conclusions.
  • analysis-summaryThe compact first reading surface for Skills and human reviewers.

Read summary first

The summary is designed to keep the review focused. It answers what was measured before the reader opens raw packet detail.

  1. verdictThe measured conclusion for the supplied ArchMap and LawPolicy.
  2. qualityMeasurementCounts for nonzero axes, hotspots, recurrent pressure, holes, holonomy loops, workflow risk, transfer bridges, and coverage gaps.
  3. trendDiagnosisWhere pressure concentrates and which cross-axis insights appear.
  4. dominantFindingsThe compact list of findings worth reading before full packet traversal.
  5. actionQueueThe prioritized review queue with detail refs.
  6. measurementBasisInput refs, validation results, coverage gaps, measured boundaries, and selected policy refs.

Open packet detail

Open archsig-analysis-packet-v0 when a finding needs source refs, witness detail, missing evidence, or an exact packet path.

  • detailIndexShows packet sections and packet:<json-pointer> refs for deeper reading.
  • sourceRefsPoint the agent back to files, symbols, docs, tests, traces, or other source evidence.
  • missingEvidence and coverage gapsKeep unknown evidence visible instead of treating absence as zero.
  • nonConclusionsState what the packet is not claiming.

Measurement families

Families are different views over the same ArchMap plus LawPolicy analysis.

FamilyRead it for
signatureAxesSelected law-axis pressure and zero / nonzero readings.
workflowRiskReadingsPermission, authority, state/effect, LLM mediation, and domain-cohesion pressure.
architectureSpectrumReportHotspots, witness clusters, recurrent obstruction support, coverage gaps, and review focus.
transferBridgeReadingsBridge pressure, overlap paths, split readiness, and boundary preparation.
architectureHomotopyReportFilled loops, unfilled holes, nonzero holonomy loops, local curvature cells, filler evidence, and coverage gaps.
Observation-axis readingsProjection fidelity, atom origin closure, effect relation pressure, synthesis blockage, operation preconditions, and path multiplicity loss.
Repair / operation readingsRepair candidates, operation deltas, transferred obstructions, side-effect axes, and readiness.

Measurement basis

Every reading is relative to the supplied evidence and selected policy. Before acting on a finding, check what was measured.

  • Observed source stateWhich ArchMap was read, which source refs were included, and which observations were source-backed.
  • Selected policyWhich LawPolicy selected the laws, witness rules, axes, and coverage expectations.
  • Validation stateWhether ArchMap, LawPolicy, and analysis validation passed, warned, or failed.
  • Coverage boundaryWhich gaps block a zero reading or require more evidence before repair planning.

Reading statuses

  • actionableThe bounded reading has enough evidence for review action.
  • needsReviewHuman or source review is required before implementation decisions.
  • blockedByCoverageGapMissing evidence must stay visible as an unknown remainder.
  • nonConclusionArchSig explicitly refuses a conclusion on that surface.

Measurement scope

Read every result as relative to the supplied ArchMap, selected LawPolicy, validation state, and coverage boundary. If the evidence or policy changes, the reading can change too.
