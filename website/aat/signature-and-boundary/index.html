Signature and Theorem Boundary

Architecture Signature is a multi-axis diagnostic of selected obstruction families. Theorem boundaries state what a package can conclude and, just as importantly, what it does not conclude.

Reading model

AAT does not say

"This design has quality score 92." A signature is not a scalar rank, a global cleanliness certificate, or a way to turn missing evidence into a zero measurement.

AAT says

Under this selected universe, with these measured axes, coverage assumptions, exactness assumptions, and non-conclusions, these obstruction coordinates are zero, nonzero, or unmeasured.

The chapter is a boundary discipline for reading reports and theorem packages. It separates the general mathematical vocabulary from the concrete Lean anchors that currently carry proved finite static structural claims.

Architecture Signature

Plain reading. A signature is a typed diagnostic vector over selected obstruction families, not a single score for architecture quality.

ArchitectureSignature records coordinates of selected obstruction families. Static, boundary, abstraction, projection or LSP, runtime, semantic, operation, extension, and analytic axes may all appear, but they do not collapse into one score or one total order.

Definition 5.1. An ArchitectureSignature is a family of typed coordinates indexed by selected obstruction axes. A coordinate is meaningful only together with its measurement status, evidence universe, claim level, and non-conclusions.

Lean status defined / proved bridge General vocabulary is documented in Chapter 5. The current concrete Lean schema is ArchitectureSignatureV1, ArchitectureSignatureV1Axis, axisValue, and axisMeasurementClass; selected required-law bridges are proved where listed below.

Proposition 5.2. In the current finite static theorem package, selected required signature axes are exactly the axes that participate in the required zero-law bridge. Runtime, empirical, matrix, and analytic axes may be useful diagnostics, but they are not silently added to RequiredSignatureAxesZero.

Lean status proved anchor Anchored by selectedRequiredLawAxes, mem_selectedRequiredLawAxes_iff, RequiredSignatureAxesZero, and architectureLawful_iff_requiredSignatureAxesZero.

Proof idea. Lean fixes the selected required-axis list and proves that the lawfulness package is equivalent to those selected axes being available and zero. Matrix diagnostics are then derived by matrixDiagnosticCorollaries_of_requiredSignatureAxesZero, so they remain consequences of the package rather than extra required zero axes.

Measurement status

Plain reading. Unknown is not zero. Missing or private evidence changes the claim boundary instead of contributing a numeric value.

Every axis carries a status. measured_zero, measured_nonzero(value), unmeasured, out_of_scope, private_unavailable, and not_applicable are different claim states. unmeasured is not simply a worse numeric value than measured_zero; it is a boundary on what can be compared.

Definition 5.3. A measurement status classifies how an axis value was obtained: measured zero, measured nonzero, unmeasured, out of scope, private or unavailable, or not applicable.

Lean status defined only Anchored by MeasurementBoundary, ArchitectureClaim, ArchitectureSignatureV1.axisMeasurementClass, and Chapter 11 analytic-axis boundary vocabulary.

Proposition 5.4. An unmeasured boundary is not measured-zero evidence. In particular, an optional signature axis with value none may satisfy a weak no-nonzero predicate, but it does not discharge an available-and-zero theorem precondition.

Lean status proved anchors See MeasurementBoundary.unmeasured_not_measuredZero, ArchitectureClaim.unmeasured_not_measuredZero, ArchitectureSignatureV1.not_axisAvailableAndZero_of_axisValue_none, and AnalyticAxisBoundary.not_canDischargeZeroReflectingClaim_of_unmeasured.

Proof. Lean keeps two predicates apart. The weak axisMeasuredZero predicate can hold for none, because no positive value is present. The stronger axisAvailableAndZero predicate requires an available some 0 value, and not_axisAvailableAndZero_of_axisValue_none proves that none does not satisfy it. The report-facing claim boundary mirrors this separation through MeasurementBoundary.unmeasured_not_measuredZero and ArchitectureClaim.unmeasured_not_measuredZero.

  • Structural coordinates Static, boundary, abstraction, projection, and operation-level obstruction readings.
  • Semantic and runtime coordinates Observation and runtime exposure readings that require their own evidence.
  • Empirical readings Cost and product outcomes are not required zero axes inside AAT.

Signature record

A signature coordinate is a typed claim, not just a number in a dashboard. The coordinate records the axis, the measured or unmeasured status, the claim level, the universe in which it was produced, the evidence that supports it, and the claims that are explicitly not being made.

Definition 5.5. A signature coordinate is the tuple consisting of an axis, a measurement status, a value when measured, a claim level, a universe or coverage boundary, evidence references, and recorded non-conclusions.

Lean status defined / tooling boundary Concrete Lean anchors include ArchitectureSignatureV1.axisValue, ArchitectureClaim, ToolingTheoremPackageMetadata, and AnalyticAxisBoundary. Tool artifacts mirror this shape in docs/tool/signature_artifacts.md.

This is why two reports cannot be compared merely because they both contain a value. The coordinate must be read together with its boundary and its axis-specific order.

Theorem boundary

Plain reading. A theorem package says what was proved, under which assumptions, and which tempting stronger readings remain outside the proof.

A theorem boundary packages selected universe, required laws, invariant family, witness universe, coverage assumptions, exactness assumptions, operation preconditions, conclusion, and non-conclusions. It prevents a bounded formal result from being read as a global architecture claim.

Definition 6.1. A TheoremBoundary is the package of selected universe, required laws, invariant family, witness universe, coverage assumptions, exactness assumptions, operation preconditions, conclusion, and non-conclusions that licenses a formal or report-side claim.

Lean status defined only The exact schematic name is docs vocabulary. Lean encodes this boundary through ProofObligation, ArchitectureTheoremPackage, ArchitectureClaim, ArchitectureCore.SelectedClaimBoundary, and ToolingTheoremPackageMetadata.

Proposition 6.2. A theorem-boundary-backed claim does not automatically become a global architecture claim. The non-conclusion field is part of the package, not commentary after the fact.

Lean status proved accessors Anchored by ArchitectureClaim.records_nonConclusions_iff, ToolingTheoremPackageMetadata.records_nonConclusions_iff, measuredWitness_not_formalProvedClaim, and not_formalProvedClaim_of_missingPreconditions.

Proof idea. The Lean records expose the non-conclusion clause as a field and prove accessor theorems showing that the predicate read by clients is exactly that field. Tooling metadata also proves that a measured witness is not a formal proved claim and that missing preconditions block formal proved status. The boundary therefore travels with the claim object.

Static split does not imply runtime or semantic flatness, and absence of a selected witness does not imply global absence.

Claim levels

A signature coordinate is not only a number. It also carries a claim level and a measurement boundary. A formal coordinate is backed by a theorem boundary; a tooling coordinate is produced by an extractor or validator; an empirical coordinate belongs to a dataset or calibration protocol; a hypothesis is a future research claim.

Definition 6.3. A claim level classifies the evidence basis for a coordinate or report claim as formal, tooling, empirical, or hypothesis.

Lean status defined only Anchored by ClaimLevel, ArchitectureClaim, ClaimClassification, and ToolingTheoremPackageMetadata.

  • formal A Lean-backed or theorem-boundary-backed statement under explicit assumptions.
  • tooling An artifact produced by a scanner, validator, report, or workflow with its own evidence boundary.
  • empirical / hypothesis A repository, operation, or outcome claim that is not promoted to theorem status.

Comparison rules

Plain reading. Compare only like with like: same axis, compatible boundary, measured on both sides, and an axis-specific order.

Signature comparison is axis-local. Two reports are comparable only where the same axis is present on both sides, measured under compatible assumptions, and equipped with a meaningful order. Missing evidence, private evidence, or a changed measurement universe breaks that comparison rather than contributing a numeric zero.

Proposition 6.4. Signature comparison is valid only on selected compatible measured axes with an axis-specific order. Missing evidence, private evidence, or a changed measurement universe breaks the comparison for that axis.

Lean status design boundary The status and claim-boundary vocabulary is defined and partially proved; AAT does not currently expose a general total-order theorem over full signatures.

Proof idea. The rule follows from the boundary vocabulary rather than from a global ordering theorem. Because unmeasured is not measured_zero, and because each coordinate is read relative to a selected universe and axis order, a full scalar comparison would require assumptions not present in the signature record.

Non-conclusions

ArchitectureSignature does not rank architectures by one scalar quality value. It does not make unmeasured axes safe, turn private evidence into measured zero, infer runtime or semantic flatness from a static split, or claim empirical cost reduction from a formal zero-axis theorem.

Non-conclusion 6.5. A signature report, theorem package, or website summary does not by itself prove repository-wide quality, complete extraction, complete telemetry, global semantic flatness, runtime safety, product outcome improvement, or a single quality ranking. Those require separate evidence and separate theorem or empirical boundaries.

Lean status boundary anchors Tracked by non-conclusion fields in ArchitectureClaim, ArchitectureCore.SelectedClaimBoundary, ToolingTheoremPackageMetadata, and related theorem-package records.

The public site treats Signature as a multi-axis diagnostic. Any product, cost, or workflow reading must preserve the theorem boundary and the artifact boundary that produced the coordinate.
