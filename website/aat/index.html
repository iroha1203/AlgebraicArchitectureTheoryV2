Thesis

Software architecture is usually described through components, layers, services, diagrams, design principles, reviews, and operational symptoms. AAT gives those readings a smaller mathematical root. It begins with typed architectural facts, then studies how facts assemble, which laws evaluate them, and where failures become minimal witnesses.

The central progression is: Atom, AtomAxiomSystem, Molecule, ArchitectureObject, DesignLaw, ObstructionCircuit, ZeroCurvaturePackage, ArchitectureSignature, and ArchitectureOperation. Each step adds structure without turning architecture into a single metric or a catalog of informal smells.

Reading order

Read AAT in four layers. Part I gives the foundation: atoms, architecture objects, and laws. Part II develops the central concepts: obstruction circuits, zero curvature, signatures, design-principle layers, and operations. Part III connects the theory to geometry, computable representations, and worked readings. References collect related work, Lean status, and formal anchors.

What this gives

AAT changes the shape of architectural reasoning. A design principle becomes a law over a selected configuration. A failure becomes an obstruction circuit when it is minimal relative to that law and universe. A repair becomes an operation whose preservation and improvement claims can be stated explicitly.

This makes architecture review more precise without pretending that every architectural fact is globally observed or every design principle is universal. The theory is useful because it keeps the selected facts, laws, witnesses, signatures, and operations visible in the same grammar.
