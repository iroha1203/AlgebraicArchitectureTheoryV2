Checked surface

AAT keeps the mathematical exposition, the Lean declaration map, and future work ledger in separate files. This page gives the public reading order for those surfaces.

  • Mathematical theory docs/aat/mathematical_theory.md is the research text for definitions, theorem candidates, examples, and design boundaries.
  • Lean theorem index Declaration names, file paths, current meanings, and status labels for the implemented Lean surface.
  • Proof obligations Future theorem work, empirical hypotheses, and open boundaries that sit outside the current proved core.
  • Formal Anchors Audit Chapter-by-chapter public bridge from website claims to Lean declarations, theorem-index links, and boundary notes.

Claim status

The website uses four status labels. They keep Lean proof, formal carriers, planned mathematics, and empirical work in separate lanes.

Status Reading Typical artifact
proved A Lean declaration establishes the claim under its recorded assumptions. Theorem, lemma, bridge theorem, or checked example.
defined only A Lean carrier exists for precise later statements. Structure, predicate, executable metric, or schema.
future proof obligation A mathematical claim is part of the planned proof program. Proof-obligation entry and theorem candidate.
empirical hypothesis A repository, operation, dataset, or outcome claim belongs to empirical evaluation. Protocol, dataset, telemetry artifact, or calibration study.
