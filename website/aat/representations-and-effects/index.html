Can algebraic architecture objects generate graph, matrix, analytic, and effect representations?

Graph and matrix

On a finite unweighted universe, acyclicity, closed-walk zero, and nilpotence of the adjacency matrix can express the same static structure. Finite propagation becomes a further reading once a walk universe and propagation model are selected.

Definition 13.1. The graph / matrix bridge is the selected finite structural reading that compares an unweighted dependency graph, its finite walk evidence, and its adjacency matrix. Its input is a proof-carrying finite component universe.

Proposition 13.2. For a finite selected unweighted structural universe, the graph and matrix readings are theorem-connected: acyclicity, closed-walk zero, and adjacency nilpotence are the same structural zero package, with the spectral-radius reading living on the corresponding finite complex matrix.

Proof idea. Powers of the finite adjacency matrix enumerate selected finite walks. If the graph is acyclic, sufficiently long selected walks have zero count, so a positive matrix power is zero. Conversely, a nonempty closed walk repeats past any nilpotence cutoff and refutes matrix nilpotence. The spectral statement is then read through the finite complex adjacency matrix.

Non-conclusion 13.3. Decomposable remains StrictLayered. Acyclicity, finite propagation, nilpotence, and spectral conditions appear as structural or analytic representation readings connected to that base definition by theorem packages.

Analytic representation

AnalyticRepresentation maps a structural architecture object into an analytic domain. The representation can be zero-preserving, zero-reflecting, obstruction-preserving, or obstruction-reflecting. These are different strengths; reflecting directions carry richer evidence packages.

Definition 13.4. An AnalyticRepresentation consists of a representation map together with focused predicates for preserving and reflecting selected zeros and obstructions. The reflecting predicates carry coverage, witness completeness, semantic contract coverage, and non-conclusion fields.

  • ZeroPreserving Structural zero implies analytic zero.
  • ZeroReflecting Analytic zero plus assumptions implies structural zero.
  • ObstructionPreserving Structural obstruction implies analytic obstruction.
  • ObstructionReflecting An analytic obstruction can be read back as structural through explicit assumptions.

Representation pattern

A representation theorem has two directions with different obligations. Preserving sends a structural zero or witness into the representation. Reflecting reads a represented zero or obstruction back into the structural world through stronger assumptions.

This pattern gives dashboards and numerical summaries their theorem shape: an aggregate can guide attention, and a reflecting package turns the represented value into structural evidence.

Proposition 13.5. Preserving and reflecting are independent theorem packages. A preserving representation can transport a structural zero into an analytic zero; a reflecting package additionally carries the recorded assumptions that authorize reading the analytic result back as a selected structural statement.

Proof idea. The Lean API keeps the four predicates distinct. The preserving theorems use the corresponding preserving field; the reflecting theorems use the reflecting field and its assumptions. A report with a represented value still needs the reflecting package to discharge the structural theorem.

Valuation surface

ObstructionValuation maps selected witnesses to numeric or ordered values. Aggregates can be useful for dashboards; an aggregate zero reflects witness-level zero through a zero-reflecting value domain and aggregation under the selected assumptions.

This is the same representation discipline as matrix and analytic readings: preserving zero transports evidence forward, while reflecting zero carries coverage and semantic contract assumptions back into the structural world.

Proposition 13.6. A zero valuation discharges selected witness zero when its zero-reflecting premise is available. Optional omitted values, unavailable axes, and unmeasured report fields have their own measurement status.

State transitions and effects

The state-transition layer treats commands, events, retries, compensations, snapshots, CRUD overwrites, and migrations as generators, with laws expressed as relations. Event Sourcing, Saga, CRUD, retry safety, snapshot consistency, and migration naturality are projections of this algebra.

The effect law surface captures obstruction visible through behavior: cross-surface effects, implicit authority, handler gaps, and non-commuting effect pairs.

Definition 14.1. A state-transition or effect law package is a selected finite family of replay, roundtrip, compensation, projection, migration, or effect relations read against a carrier and observation semantics. Lawfulness is a package statement over those selected relations.

  • Event Sourcing Replay and projection laws are selected finite law cases that read event histories through observation.
  • Saga Compensation and weak recovery are selected law packages with explicit recovery relations.
  • CRUD Overwrite behavior is read against the selected state and observation relation.
  • Circuit Breaker Runtime protection is read through selected localization and fault-model assumptions.
  • Replicated Log Convergence travels with selected quorum, ordering, fault model, and entries.

Effect examples

A dependency graph may show checkout through a payment interface, while the effect layer reveals a hidden authority surface: a non-idempotent retry command, a compensation gap, a snapshot with an omitted required event, or a non-commuting migration against the old projection.

  • Replay law Projection after appending events agrees with replaying the prior projection through those events.
  • Compensation law A compensation sequence is observationally equivalent to the relevant identity behavior.
  • Migration naturality Old and new projections commute with the migration relation under stated assumptions.
  • Effect obstruction Handler gaps, implicit authority, or non-commuting effect pairs can be selected obstruction witnesses.

Proposition 14.2. State-transition and effect lawfulness can be connected to selected obstruction zero for the law family and observation semantics supplied to the package.

Proof idea. The law family packages expose the selected laws and their obstruction predicates in the same universe. The bridge theorem states that selected family lawfulness is equivalent to zero selected family obstruction. Runtime traces, hidden effects, and real-code extraction travel through their own evidence packages.

Chapter summary

Representations let AAT move from algebraic architecture objects into graphs, matrices, signature axes, and state-transition law families. Preservation carries selected structure forward; reflection reads represented evidence back through the needed assumptions.

The effect layer extends the same discipline to commands, events, replay, compensation, projection, and migration. A static dependency view can therefore be connected to semantic and effect readings while each representation keeps its own assumptions.

Terms

representation
A map from selected structural evidence into another domain such as a graph, matrix, signature axis, or state-transition law family.
preserving direction
A structural zero or obstruction is sent into a represented zero or obstruction.
reflecting direction
A represented zero or obstruction is read back as structural through explicit assumptions.
measured zero
The selected axis was measured and its in-scope value is zero.
unmeasured axis
The axis has its own measurement status and is distinct from measured zero.
state-transition algebra
The command, event, replay, compensation, projection, and migration laws read as selected finite law cases.
effect law surface
The selected law surface for effects that enrich the static dependency graph.
