Representations and Effects

Graph, matrix, analytic, and state-transition views are different representations of selected architecture structure. The strength of a representation depends on whether it preserves or reflects zeros and obstructions under stated assumptions.

Reader model

AAT does not say

"The metric is zero, therefore the architecture is flat." A matrix, spectral value, dashboard axis, or event log is a representation of selected evidence, not the whole structure by itself.

AAT says

This representation preserves or reflects this selected zero or obstruction under these coverage, completeness, and semantic-contract assumptions; everything else stays outside the theorem boundary.

representation
A map from selected structural evidence into another domain such as a graph, matrix, signature axis, or state-transition law family.
preserving direction
A structural zero or obstruction is sent into a represented zero or obstruction.
reflecting direction
A represented zero or obstruction is read back as structural only with explicit assumptions.
measured zero
The selected axis was measured and its in-scope value is zero.
unmeasured axis
The axis is not available in the selected evidence; it is not a zero.
state-transition algebra
The command, event, replay, compensation, projection, and migration laws read as selected finite law cases.
effect boundary
The selected law surface for effects that a static dependency graph may not see.

Graph and matrix

Plain reading. The matrix bridge is a finite structural representation theorem. It is not the definition of Decomposable, and it is not a theorem about runtime propagation, latency, or cost.

On a finite unweighted universe, acyclicity, absence of closed walks, and nilpotence of the adjacency matrix can express the same static structure. Finite propagation is not baked into that structural theorem; it depends on a walk universe and a propagation model.

Definition 13.1. The graph / matrix bridge is the selected finite structural reading that compares an unweighted dependency graph, its finite walk evidence, and its adjacency matrix. Its input is a proof-carrying finite component universe, not an ambient repository.

Lean status defined only / proved bridges Anchored by ComponentUniverse, adjacencyMatrix, indexedAdjacencyMatrix, and finite matrix bridge theorems.

Proposition 13.2. For a finite selected unweighted structural universe, the graph and matrix readings are theorem-connected: acyclicity, absence of nonempty closed walks, and adjacency nilpotence are the same structural zero package, with the spectral-radius reading living on the corresponding finite complex matrix.

Lean status proved anchors See the Matrix Bridge index entries for adjacency powers, nilpotence, closed-walk obstruction, and spectral-radius zero / positivity.

Proof idea. Powers of the finite adjacency matrix enumerate selected finite walks. If the graph is acyclic, sufficiently long selected walks cannot exist, so a positive matrix power is zero. Conversely, a nonempty closed walk can be repeated past any nilpotence cutoff and refutes matrix nilpotence. The spectral statement is then read through the finite complex adjacency matrix; it does not attach cost or runtime meaning to the radius.

Analytic representation

AnalyticRepresentation maps a structural architecture object into an analytic domain. The representation can be zero-preserving, zero-reflecting, obstruction-preserving, or obstruction-reflecting. These are different strengths, and the reflecting directions are the ones that need stronger evidence.

Definition 13.4. An AnalyticRepresentation consists of a representation map together with separate predicates for preserving and reflecting selected zeros and obstructions. The reflecting predicates carry coverage, witness completeness, semantic contract coverage, and non-conclusion fields.

Lean status defined only / proved accessors Anchored by AnalyticRepresentation, ZeroPreserving, ZeroReflecting, ObstructionPreserving, ObstructionReflecting, and RecordsNonConclusions.

  • ZeroPreserving Structural zero implies analytic zero.
  • ZeroReflecting Analytic zero plus assumptions implies structural zero.
  • ObstructionPreserving Structural obstruction implies analytic obstruction.
  • ObstructionReflecting An analytic obstruction can be read back as structural only with explicit assumptions.

Representation pattern

A representation theorem has two directions that must not be conflated. Preserving sends a structural zero or witness into the representation. Reflecting reads a represented zero or obstruction back into the structural world, and therefore needs stronger assumptions.

This pattern also explains why dashboards and numerical summaries are useful but insufficient: an aggregate can guide attention without by itself proving structural flatness.

Proposition 13.5. Preserving and reflecting are independent theorem boundaries. A preserving representation can transport a structural zero into an analytic zero; a reflecting claim additionally needs the recorded assumptions that authorize reading the analytic result back as a selected structural claim.

Lean status proved accessor anchors See AnalyticRepresentation.analyticZero_of_structuralZero, structuralZero_of_analyticZero, analyticObstruction_of_structuralObstruction, and structuralObstruction_of_analyticObstruction.

Proof idea. The Lean API does not identify the four predicates. The preserving theorems apply only when the corresponding preserving field is supplied. The reflecting theorems require the reflecting field and its assumptions. Therefore a report that has only a represented value has not discharged the structural theorem package.

Valuation boundary

ObstructionValuation maps selected witnesses to numeric or ordered values. Aggregates can be useful for dashboards, but an aggregate zero reflects witness-level zero only when the value domain and aggregation are zero-reflecting under the selected assumptions.

This is the same boundary discipline as matrix and analytic representations: preserving zero is weaker than reflecting zero, and reflecting an obstruction requires coverage and semantic contract assumptions.

Proposition 13.6. A zero valuation discharges only selected witness absence when its zero-reflecting premise is available. An optional missing value, an unavailable axis, or an unmeasured report field is not a theorem-level zero.

Lean status proved accessor anchors Anchored by ObstructionValuation.no_obstruction_of_value_zero, noSelectedObstruction_of_zeroReflectingSum, AnalyticAxisBoundary.some_zero_ne_unmeasured, and not_canDischargeZeroReflectingClaim_of_unmeasured.

State transitions and effects

Plain reading. Event Sourcing, Saga, CRUD, Circuit Breaker, and Replicated Log are not one quality score. They are different operation and law packages with different selected assumptions and non-conclusions.

The state-transition layer treats commands, events, retries, compensations, snapshots, CRUD overwrites, and migrations as generators, with laws expressed as relations. Event Sourcing, Saga, CRUD, retry safety, snapshot consistency, and migration naturality are projections of this algebra.

The effect boundary captures obstruction that a dependency graph alone may miss: cross-boundary effects, implicit authority, missing handlers, and non-commuting effect pairs.

Definition 14.1. A state-transition or effect-boundary law package is a selected finite family of replay, roundtrip, compensation, projection, migration, or effect relations read against a carrier and observation semantics. Lawfulness is a selected package claim, not a statement about all implementation behavior.

Lean status defined only / proved law bridges Anchored by StateTransitionCarrier, StateTransitionLawFamilyLawful, EffectBoundaryLawFamilyLawful, and their obstruction-absence bridge theorems.

  • Event Sourcing Replay and projection laws are selected finite law cases, not proof of complete real-code event logs.
  • Saga Compensation and weak recovery are selected law packages, not a general inverse for every failure mode.
  • CRUD Overwrite behavior is read against the selected state and observation relation; it is not a universal theorem that CRUD is simple or bad.
  • Circuit Breaker Runtime protection is relative to selected localization and failure-model assumptions.
  • Replicated Log Convergence is conditional on selected quorum, ordering, failure model, and entries.

Effect examples

A dependency graph may show that checkout depends only on a payment interface, while the effect layer reveals a hidden authority boundary: a retry command is not idempotent, a compensation does not undo the action, a snapshot omits a required event, or a migration fails to commute with the old projection.

  • Replay law Projection after appending events agrees with replaying the prior projection through those events.
  • Compensation law A compensation sequence is observationally equivalent to the relevant identity behavior.
  • Migration naturality Old and new projections commute with the migration relation under stated assumptions.
  • Effect obstruction Missing handlers, implicit authority, or non-commuting effect pairs can be selected obstruction witnesses.

Proposition 14.2. State-transition and effect-boundary lawfulness can be connected to selected obstruction absence, but only for the law family and observation semantics supplied to the package.

Lean status proved bridges See stateTransitionLawFamilyLawful_iff_noStateTransitionLawFamilyObstruction and effectBoundaryLawFamilyLawful_iff_noEffectBoundaryLawFamilyObstruction.

Proof idea. The law family packages expose the selected laws and their obstruction predicates in the same bounded universe. The bridge theorem states that selected family lawfulness is equivalent to absence of the selected family obstruction. It does not add missing runtime traces, hidden effects, or real-code extractor completeness.
