Can architectural quality be read as a multi-axis signature with explicit proof support?

Architecture Signature

The signature records coordinates of obstruction families. Static structure, boundary policy, abstraction, projection, LSP, runtime behavior, semantic observation, operation, extension, and analytic readings can each become a typed axis in the diagnostic.

Definition 5.1. An ArchitectureSignature is a family of typed coordinates indexed by obstruction axes. A coordinate is read together with its measurement status, evidence universe, claim level, proof support, and recorded non-conclusions.

Proposition 5.2. In the current finite static theorem package, selected required signature axes are exactly the axes that participate in the required zero-law bridge. Runtime, empirical, matrix, and analytic axes may be useful diagnostics. The required-zero bridge names the axes that participate in that reading.

Proof idea. Lean fixes the selected required-axis list and proves that the lawfulness package is equivalent to those selected axes being available and zero. Matrix diagnostics are then derived by matrixDiagnosticCorollaries_of_requiredSignatureAxesZero, so they remain consequences of the package rather than extra required zero axes.

Measurement status

Every axis carries a status. measured_zero, measured_nonzero(value), unmeasured, out_of_scope, private_unavailable, and not_applicable are different claim states. The status is part of the coordinate: it tells a reader how the evidence can participate in theorem use, reports, and comparison.

Definition 5.3. A measurement status classifies how an axis value was obtained: measured zero, measured nonzero, unmeasured, out of scope, private or unavailable, or not applicable.

Proposition 5.4. An unmeasured status is not measured-zero evidence. In particular, an optional signature axis with value none may satisfy a weak no-nonzero predicate, but it does not discharge an available-and-zero theorem precondition.

Proof. Lean keeps two predicates apart. The weak axisMeasuredZero predicate can hold for none, because no positive value is present. The stronger axisAvailableAndZero predicate requires an available some 0 value, and not_axisAvailableAndZero_of_axisValue_none proves that none does not satisfy it. The report-facing report-facing claim support mirrors this separation through MeasurementBoundary.unmeasured_not_measuredZero and ArchitectureClaim.unmeasured_not_measuredZero.

Signature record

A signature coordinate is a typed claim. The coordinate records the axis, measurement status, claim level, universe, evidence references, proof support, and conclusion. This turns a report value into a reusable mathematical object.

Definition 5.5. A signature coordinate is the tuple consisting of an axis, a measurement status, a value when measured, a claim level, a universe or coverage support, evidence references, theorem support or claim support, and recorded non-conclusions.

Report comparison reads the coordinate together with its support and axis-specific order. The coordinate supplies the value together with the assumptions that make comparison meaningful.

Theorem boundary

A TheoremBoundary packages selected universe, required laws, invariant family, witness universe, coverage assumptions, exactness assumptions, operation preconditions, conclusion, and non-conclusions. It is the proof-carrying envelope around a formal or report-side result.

Definition 6.1. A TheoremBoundary is the package of selected universe, required laws, invariant family, witness universe, coverage assumptions, exactness assumptions, operation preconditions, conclusion, and non-conclusions that licenses a formal or report-side claim.

Proposition 6.2. A theorem-boundary-backed claim carries its conclusion and its non-conclusions together. The non-conclusion field is part of the package and travels with the conclusion.

Proof idea. The Lean records expose the non-conclusion clause as a field and prove accessor theorems showing that the predicate read by clients is exactly that field. Tooling metadata also proves that a measured witness is not a formal proved claim and that missing preconditions block formal proved status. The proof support therefore travels with the claim object.

Claim levels

A signature coordinate is more than a number. It also carries a claim level and measurement status. A formal coordinate is backed by theorem support; a tooling coordinate is produced by an extractor or validator; an empirical coordinate belongs to a dataset or calibration protocol; a hypothesis is a future research claim.

Definition 6.3. A claim level classifies the evidence basis for a coordinate or report claim as formal, tooling, empirical, or hypothesis.

  • formal A Lean-backed statement under explicit assumptions.
  • tooling An artifact produced by a scanner, validator, report, or workflow with its own evidence basis.
  • empirical / hypothesis A repository, operation, or outcome claim read through a dataset, calibration protocol, or future research program.

Comparison rules

Signature comparison is axis-local. Two reports are comparable where the same axis is present on both sides, measured under compatible assumptions, and equipped with a meaningful order. Unmeasured evidence, private evidence, and changed measurement universes are recorded as coordinate status, so comparison stays attached to the selected axes.

Proposition 6.4. Signature comparison is valid only on selected compatible measured axes with an axis-specific order. Missing evidence, private evidence, or a changed measurement universe breaks the comparison for that axis.

Proof idea. The rule follows from the coordinate vocabulary rather than from a global ordering theorem. Because unmeasured is not measured_zero, and because each coordinate is read relative to a selected universe and axis order, a full scalar comparison would require assumptions not present in the signature record.

Chapter summary

ArchitectureSignature gives AAT a multi-axis way to read architecture quality. Each coordinate carries its measurement status, evidence basis, proof support, and comparison rule. A report can place static, runtime, semantic, empirical, and product readings in one structured diagnostic.

The chapter's main contribution is a disciplined reading of architectural evidence. A signature records what is measured, how a coordinate can be compared, and which theorem or empirical support carries the reading. Later chapters use that record to track repairs, examples, and representations.

Terms

signature axis
A selected coordinate of architecture evidence, such as a static, semantic, runtime, empirical, or product reading.
measurement status
The record that distinguishes measured zero, measured obstruction, unmeasured evidence, and out-of-scope evidence.
theorem boundary
The assumptions and proof scope under which a signature coordinate supports a mathematical claim.
axis-local comparison
A comparison made only where the same axis is measured under compatible assumptions and has a meaningful order.
