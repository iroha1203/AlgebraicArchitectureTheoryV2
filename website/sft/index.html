Abstract

SFT is not a point-prediction theory of the future. It studies bounded reachable architecture futures under explicit field models, support relations, policies, horizons, observations, and calibration boundaries.

AAT supplies the local algebra of architectural change. ArchSig supplies bounded observation and review artifacts. SFT uses those inputs to discuss how artifacts and governance reshape the supported paths of software evolution.

Three-part index
