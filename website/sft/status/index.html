Boundary note

A claim level names the evidence and semantics currently available. It does not upgrade the claim to a stronger level.

Status vocabulary

SFT uses status labels to keep theory exposition, bounded schemas, tool reports, empirical studies, and deployed governance systems distinct. The label belongs to a specific statement under a stated model and observation boundary.

  • Conceptual A vocabulary or interpretation for field-shaped software evolution. It is not evidence of reachability, prediction, or safety.
  • Trace-grounded A diagnosis or reconstruction supported by named artifacts, review records, incident records, or signature observations.
  • Set-valued schema A theorem-shaped statement over explicit support semantics, horizon, step relation, and observation boundary.
  • Transition-kernel model A probabilistic or stochastic model after transition semantics have been supplied. It is not implied by set-valued reachability.
  • Calibrated empirical forecast A forecast claim evaluated against trace or dataset evidence with calibration criteria and held-out checks.
  • Deployed governance An operational workflow that uses reports, thresholds, feedback, and policy. It remains separate from formal theorem status.

Claim level map

Level Readable claim Required boundary Not concluded
L0 Conceptual or diagnostic interpretation. Named vocabulary, non-conclusions, and scope. Reachability, proof, or prediction.
L1 Trace-grounded field diagnosis. Artifact, trace, measurement, and missing-data boundary. Causal uniqueness or global completeness.
L2 Set-valued theorem schema. Support relation, step semantics, horizon, and observation axes. Probability, accuracy, or deployed safety.
L3 Transition-kernel or stochastic model claim. Transition kernel, assumptions, and inference rule. Dataset-calibrated correctness.
L4 Calibrated empirical forecast. Dataset, calibration protocol, held-out evidence, and failure modes. Lean theorem status or causal proof.
Ops Deployed governance or review workflow. Policy, threshold, feedback loop, ownership, and audit record. Universal safety or market outcome prediction.

ForecastCone and ConsequenceEnvelope

A ForecastCone is a set of reachable field paths under selected support, policy, observation, and horizon. It is not a point prediction and does not become an empirical forecast without calibration.

A ConsequenceEnvelope is the report form that summarizes selected cones, path classes, affected signature axes, witness candidates, missing boundaries, and recommended review or CI interventions. It is not a causal proof and not a Lean theorem proof.

Forecast output must preserve unknown or unmodeled remainder. A narrower cone only means narrower under the stated support semantics and selected axes.

ArchSig-SFT tooling boundary

ArchSig-SFT can turn bounded inputs such as Markdown PRDs, specs, issues, and AI proposal text into artifact descriptors, operation-support estimates, forecast-cone skeletons, and consequence-envelope reports. Those artifacts are useful review aids, not proof objects.

  • Current capability Generate bounded forecast artifacts with source references, measured axes, missing axes, theorem-boundary items, and non-conclusions.
  • Remaining gaps GitHub Issue JSON adapters, AI proposal JSON adapters, real dataset calibration, and framework semantics adapters remain outside the current claim.
  • Reader rule Tool output may inform an SFT field estimate; it does not strengthen an AAT theorem or prove forecast correctness.

Promotion rules

Moving a statement to a stronger level requires new evidence or semantics. Rewording a page, adding a diagram, or producing a report does not promote the statement.

  • From L0 to L1 Name the artifacts, traces, observations, and missing-data boundary.
  • From L1 to L2 Define the support relation, step semantics, selected horizon, and set-valued conclusion.
  • From L2 to L3 Add transition-kernel semantics and inference assumptions.
  • From L3 to L4 Run dataset calibration and report failures, residuals, and held-out checks.
  • From any level to Lean theorem Add formal definitions and a checked proof in Lean, with theorem boundaries and non-conclusions preserved.
