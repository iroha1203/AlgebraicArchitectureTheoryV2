How can a large, living development field become a mathematical object without losing its force?

The move

The computational move in SFT is not to shrink the theory until it becomes a static metric. It is to keep the large object in view, then choose a bounded slice that can be inspected. The field remains rich; the computation states exactly which part of that richness has been selected.

A SoftwareFieldEstimate records selected evidence, unavailable evidence, and unknown remainder. AAT then supplies the local algebra for the architecture projection. ArchSig supplies bounded observations. SFT adds support, policy, step semantics, horizons, path sets, envelope projections, and feedback hooks.

This is how "software evolution" becomes a computable object. We do not compute the future as a point. We compute a bounded space of paths: which architecture futures remain reachable when this field, this support relation, this policy, this observation boundary, and this horizon are selected.

Definition 2.0. A computable SFT problem is a selected field estimate together with observation axes, admissible operation families, support and policy assumptions, a horizon, and an explicit boundary for unknown or unmodeled evidence.

The SFT machine

The machinery is best read as a sequence of translations. Each stage turns a living development situation into a more precise object while keeping enough context to remain useful for engineering judgment.

The decisive concept is OperationSupport. It asks what the field makes available, natural, cheap, reviewable, or selected. A supported operation is not automatically safe, but it is part of the future shape of the system. Once support is explicit, StepRelation turns supported operations into field transitions, and bounded paths become mathematical objects.

This is where SFT becomes stronger than a metaphor. "The PRD pushes the system toward shortcut paths" can be sharpened into a question about candidate operation families, affected architecture regions, comparable signature axes, obstruction witnesses, and bounded reachable paths.

Computation habit. Do not ask whether the future is known. Ask which reachable paths are supported under the stated field model, and what evidence would change that support.

Cone and envelope

The theoretical object is the ForecastCone: the bounded set of reachable field paths from a selected starting field under selected support and horizon. The practical object is the ConsequenceEnvelope: a readable projection of one or more cones into affected regions, path classes, signature axes, obstruction candidates, missing invariants, and review questions.

This distinction is what gives SFT its engineering shape. A theorist can reason about cones and simulations. A reviewer can reason about envelopes. A tool can generate skeleton artifacts. A later calibration protocol can compare forecast items to observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome references.

Lean-backed discipline

The formal core protects the ambitious language. Lean-facing SFT does not need to formalize every organization, PRD, or human judgment. It isolates the theorem-bearing fragment: fields, supported operations, step relations, bounded paths, cone projection, envelope projection, support safety, and update records.
