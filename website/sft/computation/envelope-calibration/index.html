How does a formal cone become a useful review artifact without losing its evidence boundary?

Envelope

The envelope is a loss-aware projection. It does not preserve every path detail. It preserves the facts reviewers need: reachable path classes, affected architecture regions, comparable signature axes, expected axis delta ranges, obstruction candidates, missing boundaries, and unknown remainder.

Vocabulary

ConsequenceEnvelope

A reviewer-facing projection of one or more ForecastCones into path classes, affected regions, signature axes, obstruction candidates, missing boundaries, and unknown remainder.

Audience
Reviewers, issue authors, CI designers, and tool reports.
Shape
Loss-aware report projection, not a reconstruction of the full cone family.

Proposal accounting

A proposal is not yet a transition. SFT separates raw proposal pressure from accepted, rejected, delayed, unresolved, and review-mediated outcomes. That accounting matters because rejected and delayed paths still teach the field.

Vocabulary

Proposal accounting

The bookkeeping that separates generated proposal pressure from accepted, rejected, delayed, unresolved, and review-mediated transitions.

Why
Rejected and delayed proposals still reveal support, policy, and governance pressure.
Use
Feeds review mediation, feedback update, and later calibration records.

Calibration

Definition 2-3. Calibration is the empirical linkage between bounded forecast artifacts and later observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome references under an explicit benchmark protocol.

Vocabulary

Calibration

The empirical linkage between forecast items and later observed PR, review, CI, incident, rollback, or outcome records.

Categories
Matched, unmatched, unavailable, private, not comparable, inconclusive.
Purpose
Turns SFT from review artifact generation into an empirically testable research program.

Workbench habit. The envelope is useful before calibration as a review artifact. Calibration begins when the same forecast items are joined to observed outcomes.

Proposition 2-3. A ConsequenceEnvelope is a loss-aware projection from one or more ForecastCones into reviewer-facing regions, axes, witnesses, missing boundaries, and recommendations.

Proof idea. The projection selects visible path classes, affected regions, comparable axes, obstruction candidates, and boundary items from the cone family under the observation boundary. It does not invert the projection, preserve path counts, assign probability, or prove causal correctness. The preserved object is the review surface plus its unknown remainder.

Example 2-3. A coupon envelope can report a lawful policy insertion path, a hidden PaymentAdapter dependency path, a rounding semantic obstruction path, and an unknown remainder. Reviewers can turn that report into issue decomposition and invariant questions before the PR is written.
