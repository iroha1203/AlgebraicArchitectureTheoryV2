Simulator and Calibration

A PRD-to-ConsequenceEnvelope simulator is one realization of computable SFT. Before calibration, its output is a bounded review artifact. Calibration requires observed outcome links and benchmark discipline.

Question

When does a bounded review report become calibrated empirical evidence?

Maturity levels

  • Level 0Input artifact only.
  • Level 1ArtifactDescriptor identifies kind, scope, refs, and missing evidence.
  • Level 2OperationSupportEstimate records candidate operation families and policy constraints.
  • Level 3ForecastConeSkeleton gives bounded path classes and support refs.
  • Level 4ConsequenceEnvelope reports axes, obstructions, missing invariants, and questions.
  • Level 5ForecastCalibrationHook links forecast item refs to expected observed refs.
  • Level 6Observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome refs are compared under a benchmark protocol.

Calibration

Calibration is not a flag inside one report. It is the practice of linking forecast item refs to observed outcome refs, classifying matched and unmatched evidence, preserving unavailable or private data boundaries, and comparing results over a held-out or prospective dataset.

Definition 2.23. SFT calibration is the empirical linkage between bounded forecast artifacts and later observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome references under an explicit benchmark protocol.

SFT statusempirical protocolSource: docs/tool/sft_calibration_benchmark.md.

Boundary

Non-conclusion 2.24. Without calibration, a ConsequenceEnvelope is a bounded review artifact. With calibration, it can become empirical evidence relative to a dataset and protocol, not a Lean theorem or causal proof.
