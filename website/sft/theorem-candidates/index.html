Reader contract

The candidate list is not a progress ledger copied from GitHub Issues. It is the web-facing reading of the checked SFT formal surface. A row may contain proved accessor theorems while the larger research statement remains a theorem candidate.

A Lean proved accessor means the named record, projection, or bounded witness preserves a stated boundary. It does not imply probability, calibrated forecast correctness, extractor completeness, governance effectiveness, or global future trajectory safety.

Status legend

  • Definition candidate A stable SFT concept or record shape. Lean may define the carrier while leaving substantive bridge theorems separate.
  • Theorem candidate A theorem-shaped SFT statement whose bounded reading is tracked by the checked formal surface.
  • Lean proved A checked theorem exists for the named bounded accessor, bridge, or counterexample wrapper.
  • Future proof obligation A stronger or more general claim is intentionally left outside the current Lean package.
  • Schema only Expository theory schema only. It is not being presented as a Lean theorem.

Counterexample anchors

The counterexample package is part of the theorem surface because it names forbidden readings directly. These are finite Lean witnesses, not empirical claims.

First reading points: Part I introduces the endpoint/path anchor, and Part IV connects support safety and proposal accounting to the counterexample anchors.

Lean proved wrapper

Endpoint safety is not path safety

proved

SFTCounterexamples.endpoint_safe_zero_delta_not_path_safe records that endpoint delta zero and safe endpoints do not imply that the selected trajectory stayed inside the safe region.

Lean proved wrapper

Accepted preservation is not support preservation

proved

SFTCounterexamples.accepted_preservation_not_support_preservation separates accepted-step evidence from a theorem that every operation in finite support preserves the selected safe region.

Lean proved wrapper

Same current signature is not same future trajectory

proved

SFTCounterexamples.same_observed_signature_different_future_trajectory keeps current observation separate from future operation support and future signature trajectory.
