Candidate families

Cone inclusion from support inclusion

If every step supported by policy A is simulated by policy B under the same bounded field and horizon, the forecast cone for A projects into the forecast cone for B.

Status
formal target
Boundary
Requires explicit horizon, support relation, and projection map; does not assert empirical correctness.

Safe-region preservation under guarded support

When all selected steps preserve a safe region, every bounded path generated by those steps stays inside that region.

Status
formal target
Boundary
The safe region is a chosen predicate, not a claim that the real system has no unmeasured failure mode.

Envelope projection monotonicity

A projection that forgets path detail can preserve affected-axis inclusion while losing causal and count information.

Status
formal target
Boundary
Useful for report shape; not a proof that an observed incident was caused by one forecast item.

Counterexample anchors

  • Endpoint without path safety.The same endpoint can hide different paths, so endpoint equality does not justify safety of the route taken.
  • Metric movement without invariant preservation.A better-looking signature coordinate may coexist with a broken local premise or missing invariant.
  • Observation without completeness.A bounded extractor may see enough for review while still missing field components relevant to prediction.

Lean status

Current SFT theorem candidates are primarily formal targets and proof obligations. AAT provides proved and defined anchors for the architecture layer, but SFT field reconstruction, calibration, and forecast quality remain outside Lean unless a bounded mathematical statement is explicitly introduced.

The relevant tracking documents are the AAT theorem index and proof obligations. This page summarizes their SFT-facing implications without changing those source documents.
