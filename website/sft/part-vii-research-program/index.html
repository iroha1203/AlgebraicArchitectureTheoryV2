Reader model

Part VII is the exit chapter for SFT. It tells a reader how to use the theory without turning it into a universal explanation of software, an automatic forecasting system, or a substitute for local proof and empirical validation.

SFT does not say

A field vocabulary, a forecast cone, or a workbench report proves the future behavior of a software organization.

SFT says

Software evolution can be modeled as bounded operations, observable trajectories, feedback-mediated field updates, and boundary-aware consequence reports.

Broad scope raises the standard for boundaries: every SFT claim must say whether it is vocabulary, theorem-shaped, tool-supported, empirically calibrated, or operationally deployed.

Positioning

SFT is not a static architecture metric, software process model, DevOps maturity model, technical debt taxonomy, AI coding benchmark, or replacement for formal verification.

Its difference is that software evolution itself becomes the computational object: artifacts and feedback reshape support, governance changes selection policy, observation determines signature trajectories, and consequence envelopes describe reachable architecture futures.

Static metric

Usual emphasis
A snapshot score or selected architecture coordinate.
What SFT adds
Trajectory, support, missing axes, and field updates across changes.
Blocked reading
A single number is not architecture quality.

Process model

Usual emphasis
Stages, roles, handoffs, and delivery practice.
What SFT adds
The effect of artifacts and feedback on reachable architecture futures.
Blocked reading
Process maturity is not field lawfulness.

DevOps maturity model

Usual emphasis
Operational capability, automation, deployment, and team practice maturity.
What SFT adds
How governance and feedback change operation support, policy, and reachable architecture futures.
Blocked reading
Delivery maturity is not a theorem about field safety.

Socio-technical metaphor

Usual emphasis
Useful language for people, teams, incentives, and systems.
What SFT adds
A bounded computational object: support, policy, observation boundary, cone, envelope, and field update.
Blocked reading
A metaphor alone is not a computation or claim level.

Technical debt taxonomy

Usual emphasis
Named debt classes and remediation categories.
What SFT adds
Obstruction witnesses, affected axes, support constraints, and consequence envelopes.
Blocked reading
A taxonomy label is not a causal proof.

AI benchmark

Usual emphasis
Model performance on coding or review tasks.
What SFT adds
Proposal support, shortcut witnesses, policy boundaries, and review / CI feedback updates.
Blocked reading
AI policy compliance is not architecture lawfulness.

Formal verification

Usual emphasis
Checked local statements under explicit assumptions.
What SFT adds
A way to carry those theorem boundaries into forecast and governance reports.
Blocked reading
Local theorem status is not future trajectory safety.

What SFT does not claim

These are not caveats after the fact; they are part of the theory surface. A report that drops them is not preserving the SFT claim boundary.

  1. No mechanical future determinism SFT does not claim that future software behavior is mechanically determined.
  2. No complete organizational model Not every organizational effect, incentive, or coordination failure is formalized by the field model.
  3. No automatic empirical forecast A ForecastCone does not become an empirical prediction without calibration.
  4. No trajectory safety from AAT alone AAT theorem status does not imply future field trajectory safety.
  5. No safety from unmeasured axes Observed zero and unmeasured are different statuses.
  6. No ground truth extractor claim ArchSig extraction is not a complete ComponentUniverse or ground truth architecture object.
  7. No causal uniqueness from observed deltas An observed signature delta does not uniquely identify the artifact action that caused it.
  8. No policy-to-lawfulness implication AI policy compliance or review acceptance does not imply architecture lawfulness.
  9. No global risk reduction from cone narrowing A narrower modeled cone can still hide unmeasured axes, missing evidence, and external runtime effects.
  10. No human or market prediction SFT does not predict human intention, organizational success, or market success.

Open problems

The research program is split so that formalization, tooling, calibration, governance, and lifecycle questions can move independently. The list below restates the open problems as executable research tasks rather than slogans.

Open problems grouped by work track
Track Research tasks Output boundary
Formalization ForecastCone, support simulation, and the relation between ConsequenceEnvelope and one or more cones. Lean definitions and theorem schemas, not calibrated forecasts.
Tooling Artifact adapters, theorem-boundary integration, report projection, and workbench artifact flow. Evidence-bearing reports, not ground-truth architecture objects.
Calibration PRD-to-envelope simulator evaluation, held-out traces, false positive / false negative categories, and real dataset protocols. Empirical forecast-quality evidence, not causal theorem status.
Governance Review mediation, AI shortcut detection, allowed operation support, proposal constraints, and posterior field updates. Governance design artifacts, not general AI safety claims.
Lifecycle Codebase-as-field-memory studies, migration trajectories, contraction, deletion, and end-of-life decisions. Decision boundaries over selected inputs, not market or staffing predictions.
  • Canonical open-problem list ForecastCone and support simulation; ConsequenceEnvelope-to-cone relation; trace-grounded field reconstruction; PRD-to-envelope calibration; review mediation and AI shortcut benchmarks; theorem-boundary integration; codebase field-memory studies; AI-mediated governance; lifecycle and end-of-life models; deployed closed-loop workbench.
  • Next formal step Define the minimal core for reachable field paths, support inclusion, horizon, step simulation, and cone projection.
  • Next empirical step Record which traces are available, private, unavailable, or unknown before calling an estimate reconstructed.
  • Next tooling step Keep artifact source references, missing evidence, theorem boundaries, and non-conclusions attached through the pipeline.

Workbench direction

The SFT workbench should take PRDs, design memos, issue plans, codebase state, architecture signatures, review and CI history, incident history, and AI policy. It should return a consequence envelope, affected axes, witness families, missing boundaries, risky default paths, issue decomposition, review or CI interventions, AI proposal constraints, and calibration plans.

Minimal workbench contract
Surface Selected contents Boundary to preserve
Inputs PRD, design memo, issue plan, codebase state, architecture signature, review / CI history, incident history, and AI agent policy. Private evidence, unavailable evidence, stale evidence, and unknown remainder must be explicit.
Intermediate artifacts Artifact descriptor, operation support estimate, forecast cone skeleton, theorem-boundary items, and calibration hooks. Intermediate success is not extractor completeness or forecast correctness.
Outputs ConsequenceEnvelope, affected axes, witness families, missing boundaries, risky default paths, issue decomposition, governance interventions, AI constraints, and feedback plan. Outputs are review and planning artifacts, not a causal proof of the future.
  • What it can make visible Which axes are affected, which witnesses are plausible, which evidence is missing, and which review / CI gates should be strengthened.
  • What it cannot certify That the selected proposal is safe, that all runtime effects are covered, or that the organization will choose the modeled path.
  • What closes the loop Observed PR, review, CI, incident, and runtime traces must feed calibration hooks and posterior field updates.
Minimal artifact flow
Layer Input Output Boundary
Input normalization PRD, Issue, AI proposal, and source refs. ArtifactDescriptor. Source refs and missing evidence are preserved.
Forecast construction Descriptor, policy constraints, and bounded horizon. OperationSupportEstimate and ForecastConeSkeleton. Finite support and unknown remainder are preserved.
Review projection Cone family, signature axes, and theorem boundary. ConsequenceEnvelope. Reviewer-facing report, not causal forecast.
Governance projection AI policy and review / CI mediation refs. AI proposal governance or lifecycle decision surface. Allowed, forbidden, and unknown support stay separate.
Feedback join Observed PR, review, CI, outcome refs, and operational artifacts. ForecastCalibrationHook, calibration review, and hypothesis refresh input. Observation linkage, not proof of forecast correctness.
Workbench phase plan
Phase Purpose Next required artifact
Artifact inventory List operational, forecast, governance, and unavailable adapter artifacts. Workbench run description.
Offline bundle Manually bundle one PRD, Issue, or AI proposal with available trace refs. Manifest for artifact refs, excluded inputs, and non-conclusions.
Review-loop rehearsal Connect generated envelopes and governance projections to review / CI checklists. Reviewer decision and CI outcome refs.

  • Forecast artifact pipeline Creates bounded forecast artifacts through ConsequenceEnvelope; it does not prove forecast correctness.
  • Future adapters Normalize GitHub Issue JSON, AI proposal JSON, framework semantics, and runtime / incident traces as supplied evidence.
  • Operational feedback Stores observed outcome, calibration review, threshold, ownership, repair adoption, incident correlation, and hypothesis refresh.
  • Calibration hook Connects forecast item refs with observed refs and passes them to the benchmark protocol.
  • Deployed workbench boundary Bundles artifacts into one review cycle, without adding deployment correctness, automatic governance correctness, or causal theorem status.

SFT does not replace developers. It makes software evolution visible as something that can be observed, bounded, computed, governed, and revised.

Research boundary

The research program is deliberately split across formal, tooling, and empirical tracks. Lean work can prove bounded schemas; ArchSig and the workbench can produce evidence and reports; calibration studies can test forecast quality. None of these statuses should be collapsed into the others.

This page therefore treats SFT as a large computational theory with visible non-conclusions. The scope is broad, but every claim must still state whether it is defined only, theorem-shaped, tool-supported, empirically calibrated, or operationally deployed. The SFT status page is the website-level index for those distinctions.
