Theorem-bearing fragment

The formal core is the part of SFT that can be stated as bounded mathematical structure: selected field states, supported operations, step relations, finite horizons, cone projections, envelope projections, and safety relations between supports.

The fragment does not assert that a real organization has been completely extracted, that a forecast will be correct, or that a tool report proves a software system safe. Those claims require observation models, datasets, calibration, and governance evidence outside the theorem-bearing core.

Core objects

  • SoftwareField A bounded slice of the development field, with explicit modeling boundary and architecture projection.
  • OperationSupport The relation saying which operations are visible, plausible, cheap, reviewable, or otherwise supported under the field.
  • StepRelation The transition relation generated by supported operations, policies, and selected observations.
  • ForecastCone A bounded set of reachable futures under a field, support, policy, and horizon.
  • ConsequenceEnvelope A projection of the cone into affected axes, obstruction candidates, missing invariants, and governance questions.
  • Support safety Relations such as inclusion, simulation, and safe-region preservation that can narrow a cone without treating prediction as proof.

AAT and ArchSig boundaries

AAT supplies the architecture-side algebra: objects, operations, invariants, obstruction witnesses, and signatures. SFT uses that algebra through the architecture projection of a field; it does not turn every SFT forecast into an AAT theorem.

ArchSig supplies bounded observation and review artifacts. It can record evidence for a field estimate, an operation-support estimate, or an envelope skeleton, but extractor output is not a complete field and not a Lean proof.

Non-conclusions

  • No complete extraction.A real organization is not fully reconstructed by a repository scan or artifact parser.
  • No point prediction.A cone describes bounded reachable futures under assumptions, not the one future that must occur.
  • No automatic theorem promotion.A useful forecast or tool report remains empirical or tooling evidence until a formal statement and proof boundary are supplied.
