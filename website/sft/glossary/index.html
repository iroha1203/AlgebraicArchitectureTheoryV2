Glossary and Terminology Boundary

SFT uses field, force, attractor, basin, cone, envelope, support, policy, and calibration as bounded modeling terms. This glossary fixes the computational reading and the readings that are excluded.

Reader model

SFT does not say

Software evolution is governed by physical laws.

SFT says

Software evolution can be modeled as bounded computation over selected artifacts, practices, policies, observations, and feedback.

SFT does not say

A forecast is a single predicted future.

SFT says

A forecast is set-valued or report-valued under an explicit horizon, support relation, policy, and observation boundary.

SFT does not say

Tool output is an architecture theorem.

SFT says

Tool output is an observation, estimate, report, or review artifact with non-conclusions attached.

SFT does not say

Organization culture and human intention are fully modeled.

SFT says

The model records selected evidence and preserves unknown or unmodeled remainder.

Term boundary

field

Formal reading
A selected development context or SoftwareField slice containing codebase memory, artifacts, support, policy, observations, and feedback.
Avoided reading
A physical field or complete model of the organization.
Related parts
Part I, Part III

force

Formal reading
An informal name for artifact-mediated pressure that changes candidate field updates, operation support, policy, or observation components.
Avoided reading
A physical vector quantity or deterministic cause.
Related parts
Part III, Part VI

dissipation

Formal reading
Loss, leakage, or untracked remainder across a bounded model, such as missing evidence, review rework, or unobserved risk.
Avoided reading
Thermodynamic dissipation or automatic degradation law.
Related parts
Status, Part VII

attractor

Formal reading
A stable or preferred region only when stability, recurrence, policy, or probability semantics are supplied.
Avoided reading
A guaranteed destination or informal label for any good design.
Related parts
Part IV, Status

basin

Formal reading
A reachable preimage or region of support that tends toward a selected stable region under named semantics.
Avoided reading
A vague danger zone without a transition, support, or policy definition.
Related parts
Part IV

ForecastCone

Formal reading
A set of reachable field paths relative to a field model, operation support, policy, horizon, and observation boundary.
Avoided reading
A point prediction, empirical forecast, or global risk proof without calibration.
Related parts
Part III, Status

ConsequenceEnvelope

Formal reading
A report form that summarizes selected cones, path classes, affected axes, witnesses, missing evidence, and non-conclusions.
Avoided reading
A Lean proof, causal proof, or complete impact analysis.
Related parts
Part V, Part VI

support

Formal reading
The operation family considered possible, natural, low-cost, accepted, or generated under an explicit field model.
Avoided reading
Universal permission or proof that unsupported paths cannot occur in reality.
Related parts
Part III, Part IV

policy

Formal reading
A selection, preorder, cost, review, CI, AI, or governance rule over supported operations.
Avoided reading
A guarantee that all future choices are safe or compliant.
Related parts
Part III, Part V

calibration

Formal reading
Comparison of forecast reports or model outputs against trace, PR, review, CI, incident, or dataset evidence.
Avoided reading
Proof of causality, universal validity, or Lean theorem status.
Related parts

Core distinctions

  • ForecastCone vs. ConsequenceEnvelope The cone is the set-valued reachable-path object. The envelope is the boundary-aware report that packages one or more cones for review and governance.
  • Attractor vs. stable region A stable region is a selected safe or preferred region. Calling it an attractor requires additional stability, recurrence, policy, or probability semantics.
  • support vs. policy Support names the operations under consideration. Policy ranks, accepts, rejects, delays, or governs those operations.
  • measured zero vs. absence A zero on a selected axis is not safety on unmeasured axes and not absence of latent action.

AAT / ArchSig / SFT

Layer Role Boundary
AAT Local algebra of architecture objects, operations, invariants, signatures, paths, and theorem boundaries. AAT does not depend on SFT, and an AAT theorem is not an empirical prediction.
ArchSig Tooling layer that maps real artifacts into observations, reports, signature axes, witnesses, and field estimates. ArchSig is not AAT or SFT; extractor output is not a ground-truth architecture object.
SFT Computational theory of field-shaped software evolution, forecast cones, consequence envelopes, policy, feedback, and calibration. SFT preserves model boundary, missing evidence, unknown remainder, and claim level.
