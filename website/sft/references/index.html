What moved here

The old reference layer mixed formal core, Lean anchors, theorem candidates, research planning, positioning, status, and vocabulary as separate sibling pages. The new structure groups them by reader question: what is formally anchored, what remains research work, and what do the terms mean?

Reading order

  1. Formal Core and Anchors.Use this when you want to know which parts of SFT are protected by Lean-facing objects and proved boundaries.
  2. Research Program and Boundaries.Use this when you want to know what SFT does next, how empirical claims are promoted, and what SFT does not claim.
  3. Glossary.Use this as the compact vocabulary surface while reading the main chapters.
