Research ladder

  • Formal core.State bounded field objects, support relations, step relations, cones, envelope projections, and safety claims.
  • Workbench.Build tools that turn PRDs, issues, reviews, and incidents into field estimates and consequence envelopes.
  • Calibration.Join forecast artifacts to later PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome references under explicit protocols.
  • Governance.Use feedback to reshape support, policy, review surfaces, AI proposal boundaries, and lifecycle capacity.

Benchmarks

SFT benchmark work asks whether forecast artifacts improve review, issue decomposition, invariant discovery, shortcut detection, and lifecycle decisions. It does not ask Lean to prove empirical forecast correctness.

Closed-loop tooling

The long-term tool shape is closed-loop: artifact descriptors produce bounded forecast artifacts; review and implementation produce observed outcomes; calibration records error and missing evidence; the posterior field updates the next support and policy estimates.

Positioning

  • Not a metrics dashboard.Metrics can become observation axes, but SFT studies field-shaped reachable futures.
  • Not a process checklist.Workflow matters because it changes support, policy, observation, and feedback memory.
  • Not formal verification of a whole organization.SFT has formal fragments, but reconstruction and calibration remain bounded claims.
  • Not an AI benchmark.AI proposal governance is one field intervention surface, not the whole theory.

Claim levels

  • Conceptual definition.A stable vocabulary item in the SFT exposition, not necessarily a formal Lean object.
  • Theorem-shaped claim.A statement that can be formalized after objects, hypotheses, and non-conclusions are fixed.
  • Formal anchor.A Lean declaration or theorem package already present in the SFT-facing formal layer.
  • Tooling artifact.A report, schema, or CLI output that supports review and calibration, not a proof.
  • Empirical hypothesis.A claim about observed development behavior that needs data and evaluation protocol.
  • Calibrated result.An empirical claim backed by matched forecast items and observed outcomes under a stated protocol.

Promotion rules

  1. To formal claim.Provide definitions, hypotheses, proof statement, and a boundary saying what the theorem does not imply.
  2. To tooling claim.Provide schema, fixture, validation rule, and report interpretation rule.
  3. To empirical claim.Provide dataset boundary, matching protocol, measured outcome, uncertainty, and calibration limits.

Non-conclusions

A ForecastCone is not a point prediction, a ConsequenceEnvelope is not an incident-causality proof, and an ArchSig report is not a Lean certificate. SFT makes these boundaries visible so field language can remain ambitious without becoming careless.
