Audit scope

This is the Lean-facing audit surface for SFT. It does not replace the main SFT reading path. It gathers the old Formal Core, Formal Anchors, and Theorem Candidates material into one Lean-facing reference page.

The pattern is the same as AAT: prose carries the theory's force, while Lean keeps the claim grammar bounded. A field is represented by selected state and projection data. A force is represented by support, policy, action, update, or observation boundary. A forecast is represented by finite path membership, not by point prediction.

Formal core

The theorem-bearing fragment of SFT is the part that can be stated as bounded mathematical structure: selected field states, supported operations, step relations, finite horizons, cone projections, envelope projections, and safety relations between supports.

This fragment does not assert complete organizational extraction, forecast correctness, or empirical calibration. It gives the typed surface on which those later claims must depend.

  • SoftwareFieldA bounded slice of the development field with architecture projection and modeling boundary.
  • ArtifactActionA set-valued artifact-mediated change relation with explicit interpretation and non-conclusion boundaries.
  • OperationSupportThe field-relative family of operations that are visible, plausible, cheap, reviewable, or otherwise supported.
  • ForecastConeA finite reachable path set under selected support, step relation, source field, and horizon.
  • ConsequenceEnvelopeA loss-aware projection of cone families into reviewer-facing regions, axes, witnesses, and boundary items.
  • Reachability and StableRegionMay / must reachability, stable regions, and reachable preimages separated from strong attractor claims.
  • AttractorEngineeringA bounded support-shaping package for selected target regions, measurement boundaries, and bad-region avoidance.

Theorem candidates

The old theorem-candidates page is folded into this audit surface. Candidate statements are useful when they are close to the Lean anchors that would support them and the counterexamples that prevent overclaiming.

Cone inclusion from support inclusion

If every step supported by policy A is simulated by policy B under the same bounded field and horizon, the forecast cone for A projects into the forecast cone for B.

Status
proved schema
Anchors
ForecastConeProjection.forecastCone_projects_of_supportInclusion_and_stepSimulation and related projection theorems.

Safe-region preservation under guarded support

When all selected steps preserve a safe region, every bounded path generated by those steps stays inside that region.

Status
proved schema
Anchors
StableRegion.forecastCone_target, SFTSupportSafetyPackage.supportSafety_preserves_forecastTrajectory, and forecastCone_and_supportSafety.

Envelope projection preserves boundaries

A projection that forgets path detail can preserve affected-axis, missing-boundary, theorem-boundary, unknown-remainder, and non-conclusion information.

Status
proved schema
Anchors
EnvelopeProjection.envelope_preserves_missingBoundary, envelope_preserves_theoremBoundary, and envelope_does_not_strengthen_forecast_claim.
