Can a software field be designed so better futures become the natural path?

Attractor engineering

Attractor engineering designs the field so desirable futures are natural to find and repeat. It lowers the cost of lawful paths and makes shortcut paths costly, visible, and review-mediated.

An attractor is a future that the field keeps pulling work toward. It may appear as a repeated PR shape, a default issue decomposition, a copied local pattern, or an AI-generated shortcut that looks natural because the surrounding code already suggests it. SFT treats that pull as a field property.

Vocabulary

Attractor

A field-relative tendency for future changes to repeatedly move toward a selected region, path shape, or operation family.

Appears as
Default PR shape, recurrent issue decomposition, copied local pattern, or repeated shortcut.
SFT reading
A support-and-policy phenomenon under a selected field model, observation boundary, and horizon.
Vocabulary

AttractorEngineering

Designing support and policy so desired architecture futures become natural, low-cost, observable, and repeatable.

Formal reading
Support shaping, policy shaping, governance intervention, observation boundary, and feedback update rule.
Boundary
Selected target region, support relation, policy, observation boundary, and horizon.

Intervention habit. The strongest governance makes the checklist and the codebase point toward the same lawful move.

Codebase as prompt

A codebase is implementation, prompt, training surface, review memory, and local grammar for future work. The next human or AI proposal will tend to follow the structure the field makes obvious: nearby APIs, copied examples, missing tests, named invariants, ownership routes, and review precedents.

This is why attractor engineering matters in AI-mediated development. AI agents amplify the field. If shortcuts are local and invisible, generated proposals will often rediscover them. If lawful paths are local, tested, named, and reviewable, AI proposals can amplify that structure just as quickly.

Vocabulary

Codebase prompt

The way existing code, tests, documents, reviews, incidents, and ownership routes suggest the next change to humans and AI agents.

Pull
What the current field makes obvious, cheap, and easy to copy.
Design task
Make lawful paths more prompt-like than shortcuts.

Beyond guardrails

Guardrails matter, and attractor engineering reaches further upstream. A guardrail reacts to a proposed path. Attractor engineering changes the field before the proposal exists: the examples developers copy, the APIs that feel natural, the tests that encode invariants, the review questions that become routine, and the ownership paths that make lawful work easier.

Mechanism Main action Attractor-engineering reading
Guardrail Stops forbidden output or operation. Downstream control over proposal generation.
Harness Wraps an agent with evaluation and limits. External control around the field.
Policy enforcement Defines and rejects forbidden sets. Shapes support when it changes cost, visibility, and feedback.
Review gate Accepts, rejects, or requests changes before merge. Becomes attractor engineering when review changes future proposal support.
Field design Changes examples, boundaries, invariants, tests, ownership, and feedback memory. Makes good paths naturally selected before review has to rescue them.

Design levers

The practical work is to change the support and policy landscape. A well-shaped field makes boundary-preserving operations local, discoverable, low-friction, observable, and easy to reinforce. A drifting field makes shortcuts local, cheap, and hard to observe.

Lever

Support shaping

Change the operation families that are easy to construct: APIs, templates, examples, module boundaries, ownership routes, and issue decomposition.

Lever

Policy shaping

Lower the cost of lawful paths and raise the cost of shortcut paths through review defaults, CI requirements, typing, architecture rules, and migration scaffolds.

Lever

Observation shaping

Make drift visible by adding tests, traces, signature axes, witness checks, runtime guards, and missing-boundary reports.

Lever

Feedback shaping

Preserve review outcomes, incidents, rejected shortcuts, and successful lawful paths as field memory for the next proposal.

Stable regions

Stable-region language gives the intuition a computable shape. A region is stable when supported steps remain inside it. A recurrent path is a repeated development pattern under the selected support and policy. In ordinary engineering language, this is the moment where the system begins to feel as if it wants a certain kind of next change.

Basin language names the surrounding region from which that tendency becomes reachable. A bad basin is the part of the field from which shortcuts, drift, or hidden coupling become easy to reach. A good basin is the part of the field from which lawful paths remain nearby.

Vocabulary

Basin

A field-relative region of starting states from which a selected attractor, stable region, or path family becomes reachable under the chosen support, policy, and horizon.

Good basin
Starting states from which lawful paths are nearby, supported, and reviewable.
Bad basin
Starting states from which shortcuts, drift, or hidden coupling become cheap and repeatable.
Vocabulary

StableRegion

A selected region where accepted supported steps remain inside the region under the chosen semantics.

May reach
Some supported path reaches the region within the horizon.
Must reach
Every selected supported path reaches the region within the horizon.

Semantic boundary. Strong attractor and basin claims require selected stability, recurrence, policy, or probability semantics. The SFT core decomposes them into stable regions, reachable preimages, ForecastCones, support safety, and proposal accounting.

Measurement surface

To engineer an attractor, the field has to expose signals. SFT uses a bounded measurement surface that can compare support risk, basin movement, shortcut visibility, return time, and feedback update across selected regions.

Signal Question it asks
DefaultPath Which path does the field make easiest before anyone argues?
RecurrentPattern Which development path repeats across issues, PRs, or AI proposals?
Good basin mass How much selected initial-state mass reaches the desired region?
Bad basin visibility How quickly does drift toward an unsafe region become observable?
Escape cost How expensive is it to repair or refactor out of a bad region?
Field shaping delta What changed after the intervention: support, policy, observation, or feedback?

Measurement habit. Attractor engineering starts by making basin movement observable.

Shaping moves

Proposition 3-2. Attractor engineering changes reachable architecture futures by shaping support, policy, observation, and feedback around a selected target region.

Proof idea. The ForecastCone is generated from the starting field, support, step relation, and horizon. Support and policy shaping change the operation families that generate paths. Observation and feedback shaping change the evidence and memory used by the next cone. Therefore the selected target region can become easier to reach, preserve, and repeat.

Example 3-2. A coupon review rule that requires discount composition and refund semantics lowers the cost of the DiscountPolicy path and raises the cost of UI-only drift. This is attractor engineering: the better path becomes easier to select.
