Introduction

Part 3 is where SFT turns from reachable futures to software evolution as an engineered process. Part 2 asks which futures are reachable under a selected field model. Part 3 asks how a team can reshape that field when the reachable shape diverges from the future it wants to inhabit.

The answer is rarely a single gate. A review rule may remove a shortcut from support. A type boundary may make an invariant cheap to preserve. A runtime check may make a hidden path observable. A lifecycle decision may retire a surface the field can no longer support. These interventions change the future shape of the system.

This is the practical promise of SFT: once software evolution is visible as a field-shaped computation, engineering can move upstream. We can design the conditions under which the next good change becomes obvious.

Chapters

Part 3 develops software evolution in four steps: field shaping, attractor engineering, AI proposal governance, and lifecycle decisions with closed-loop feedback.

  • 3-1. Field ShapingReview, CI, type systems, runtime guards, ownership, and feedback as transformations of support and observation.
  • 3-2. Attractor EngineeringStable regions, design levers, and measurement surfaces that make desirable futures natural and repeatable.
  • 3-3. AI Proposal GovernanceControlling AI-generated proposal support through theorem boundaries, shortcut witnesses, review, CI, and feedback.
  • 3-4. Lifecycle and Closed-Loop EvolutionRepair, migration, contraction, deletion, end-of-life, and posterior field updates as lifecycle interventions.
