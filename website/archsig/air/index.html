Why AIR exists

Sig0 tells you what the scanner observed. AIR tells the review system what that observation means. It is the layer that joins source evidence, policy evidence, runtime evidence, semantic witnesses, generated-change provenance, coverage, and claims.

This is the main difference between ArchSig and a metric dashboard. ArchSig does not stop at values such as sccMaxSize or fanoutRisk. AIR records which claim those values support, what evidence backs the claim, what was not measured, and which formal preconditions would be required for a stronger reading.

Inputs

The air command reads the current signature and any review context available for the change. Validation and diff input are optional, but useful AIR is usually built from all three.

  • --sig0 The current architecture observation.
  • --validation Artifact quality and coverage checks for the selected universe.
  • --diff Before / after changes and comparable or unmeasured axes.
  • --pr-metadata Review context, changed files, attribution candidates, and PR refs.

AIR shape

AIR is intentionally broader than a dependency graph. It keeps identity, provenance, components, relations, policies, semantic diagrams, paths, witnesses, signature axes, coverage, claims, operation trace, and extension summary in one intermediate representation.

Claims

AIR claims are the heart of the representation. A claim names a subject, predicate, claim level, classification, measurement boundary, theorem refs, evidence refs, assumptions, missing preconditions, and non-conclusions.

  • Measured witness The tool observed evidence for the selected universe. It can guide review without becoming a proof.
  • Blocked formal claim The claim points toward a formal reading, but theorem bridge preconditions are missing.
  • Formal proved The strongest reading: theorem refs are registered and required preconditions are discharged.

AIR makes claim level visible. That is why later reports can warn, block, advise, or defer without pretending that every measured witness is a theorem.

Coverage

Coverage is not footnote material. It is a precondition for reading every claim. AIR records measured axes, unmeasured axes, projection rules, extraction scope, exactness assumptions, and unsupported constructs per layer.

  • Static coverage What import or dependency structure the scanner observed.
  • Runtime coverage Which runtime edges were supplied as evidence and which were not.
  • Semantic coverage Which diagrams or witnesses were selected for semantic reading.
  • Unsupported constructs Dynamic imports, plugin loading, framework conventions, or private evidence that the current adapter cannot measure.

Reports

AIR is not the final user-facing report. It is the common source used by theorem checks, Feature Extension Reports, policy decisions, and PR comments.

  • theorem-check Reads AIR claims and reports whether formal preconditions are present or missing.
  • feature-report Projects AIR into split status, witnesses, coverage gaps, and repair suggestions.
  • policy-decision Applies organization policy to report evidence and emits pass / warn / fail / advisory outcomes.
  • pr-comment Turns review evidence into Markdown for GitHub Checks or PR comments.

When a report looks surprising, inspect AIR first. It is where you can see whether the issue came from source evidence, policy evidence, missing coverage, or claim promotion boundaries.
