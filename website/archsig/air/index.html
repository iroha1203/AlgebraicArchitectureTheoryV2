Why AIR exists

Sig0 and signature diffs are measurement artifacts. Reviewers need interpretation: which movement matters, what supports the interpretation, which assumptions are missing, and how the result should appear in reports. AIR is the intermediate representation for that translation.

Inputs

  • Sig0The current architecture signature and metric status.
  • ValidationArtifact coherence and warnings.
  • Signature diffBefore/after movement.
  • PR metadataReview context, changed files, labels, and linked issues.
  • ArchMapAn optional evidence-carrying map candidate for semantic structure that was read from code, docs, tests, traces, or PR context.
  • Law policy and framework adapterOptional interpretation layers that connect measured facts to architectural claims.

ArchMap input

ArchMap is the AIR input for architecture observations that cannot be reduced to static imports. air-from-archmap projects archmap-v0 source refs, object mappings, relation mappings, semantic diagrams, nonfillability witnesses, missing evidence, and non-conclusions into AIR without treating the map as proof.

AIR shape

An AIR record should preserve source artifacts, claim candidates, evidence references, coverage records, theorem precondition status, report projections, and non-conclusions. It is the place where a measurement becomes a bounded architecture interpretation.

Claims

AIR claims should be narrow and inspectable. They can describe a boundary crossing, non-split extension, semantic dependency, missing runtime evidence, or policy-relevant movement. They should not become broad architecture judgments without evidence.

Coverage

Coverage records say where the interpretation is strong and where it is incomplete. They are what let a report say, for example, that static code paths were inspected while runtime behavior and external provider behavior were not verified.

Reports

Feature reports, theorem checks, policy decisions, PR comments, and SFT artifacts consume AIR. When a report feels vague, inspect the AIR evidence refs and coverage before rewriting the report language.

SFT connection

AIR is also the bridge from review evidence to software evolution reasoning. It lets daily PR signals become inputs to operation support estimates, forecast cone skeletons, consequence envelopes, and calibration records.
