Commands

Operational commands produce and validate datasets for PR history, feature extension outcomes, report outcomes, calibration reviews, team thresholds, ownership boundaries, repair adoption, incident correlations, and hypothesis refresh cycles.

Feedback records

  • Report outcome ledgerRecords whether review signals were accepted, rejected, suppressed, or converted into action.
  • Calibration review recordDocuments whether thresholds and interpretations should change.
  • Team threshold policyCaptures organization-specific warning and gate levels.
  • Ownership monitorTracks whether ownership crossings create review pressure.
  • Repair adoption recordTracks whether suggested repairs were adopted and whether later signals changed.

Calibration

Calibration makes ArchSig more useful for a specific team. It can adjust thresholds, suppress known baseline risks, prioritize confirmed witnesses, and refine semantic observations. Calibration records should preserve why a decision was made.

Incidents

Incident correlation monitors can connect architecture signals with operational outcomes. Treat this as empirical evidence. It can guide hypotheses, thresholds, and review emphasis, but it is not causal proof.

Non-conclusions

  • Correlation is not causationIncident linkage supports empirical learning, not a theorem.
  • Thresholds are localA team threshold policy is not a universal architecture law.
  • Feedback is not retroactive proofLater outcome data can calibrate the signal, not change what the original artifact observed.
