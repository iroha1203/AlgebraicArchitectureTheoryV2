Evidence inventory

An evidence inventory lists the source universe used for a signal: files, atom observations, molecule observations, semantic observations, selected LawPolicy, runtime traces, unavailable evidence, unsupported constructs, private data, and assumptions required for interpretation.

Measurement status

  • MeasuredThe axis was evaluated in the selected universe.
  • UnmeasuredThe artifact has no measurement for that axis.
  • UnsupportedThe adapter or command does not observe that construct.
  • Private or unavailableThe evidence may exist, but this artifact cannot inspect it.
  • Out of scopeThe axis is intentionally outside the selected run.

Interpretation levels

A report can support review action, policy decision, theorem-precondition checks, or empirical calibration. ArchSig keeps these levels readable so the reviewer can use the right artifact for the job.

Formal promotion

Formal promotion requires bridge assumptions: extractor completeness for the selected universe, exactness of policy mapping, theorem preconditions, and proof-relevant definitions. ArchSig can report that those assumptions are satisfied, missing, or outside the artifact.

Runtime and semantic evidence

Runtime evidence and semantic evidence are powerful because architecture risk often lives outside static imports. Traces, incidents, business-rule observations, and human-confirmed AIR records help reviewers see movement that import graphs miss.

Follow-up actions

  • Inspect changed filesOpen the source refs behind the signal.
  • Confirm semantic movementCheck business rules, state transitions, and responsibility movement with the team.
  • Add runtime observationAttach traces, incidents, latency, or error propagation when runtime behavior matters.
  • Update policyPromote repeated review signals into organization-specific thresholds or warnings.
  • Calibrate over timeFeed accepted, rejected, suppressed, and repaired signals back into future review.
