LLM role

An LLM can read code, PR text, and domain notes to propose semantic observations: a business rule moved, a state transition changed owner, a feature now depends on payment semantics, or a hidden assumption appeared. In ArchSig, that output first belongs in ArchMap as a map candidate with source refs, then becomes Semantic AIR only after projection and validation preserve its evidence boundary.

  • semantic_observationWhat the model observed in code or text.
  • semantic_hypothesisA proposed interpretation that needs review.
  • review_signalA bounded signal sent to PR review.
  • evidence_boundaryWhat sources were inspected and what was not inspected.
  • requires_human_confirmationWhether a human reviewer must confirm the semantic reading.

Semantic AIR record

A semantic AIR record should include observed behavior, architectural interpretation, source refs, required confirmation, evidence boundary, and the ArchMap coverage record that explains the selected source universe. It should avoid final language such as “this is wrong” unless policy and human review have made that decision explicit.

Human confirmation

Semantic claims often touch domain ownership. ArchSig should preserve model observations, but confirmation belongs to reviewers or domain owners. The report can ask for confirmation, record the decision, and feed the outcome into calibration.

Risk controls

  • Keep source refsEvery semantic reading needs inspected code, PR text, issue text, trace, or domain note references.
  • Mark uncertaintyDistinguish observation, hypothesis, and confirmed interpretation.
  • Do not silently blockGenerated semantic observations should become review signals unless policy explicitly says otherwise.
  • Calibrate outcomesTrack when semantic observations were confirmed, rejected, or refined.
