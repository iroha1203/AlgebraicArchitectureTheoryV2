CI shape

The strongest ArchSig CI path compares a base signature and a head signature, validates both, generates a diff, projects the diff into AIR, and then produces a human-readable review artifact.

Before and after scans

Keep before and after artifacts comparable. Record the revision SHA, branch, policy path, extractor output path, tags, and validation report used to construct each snapshot.

AIR and reports

AIR is the bridge from measured output to review language. It records evidence references, coverage, theorem precondition status, and claim records before reports are generated.

PR comment

The PR comment should summarize review action, not dump raw JSON. A good comment tells reviewers which architectural movement matters, what evidence supports it, what was not established, and which files or assumptions deserve inspection.

Review gate policy

  • Block on artifact invaliditySchema or validation failures can block because the review artifact is not trustworthy.
  • Warn on measured riskMeasured architectural risk should usually become reviewer attention unless the organization has an explicit policy threshold.
  • Do not block on unknown as if it were failureUnknown, unmeasured, unsupported, and private evidence states should be visible, not silently converted into pass/fail.
  • Record suppressionsBaseline suppressions need justification so the signal can be recalibrated later.

Minimal workflow

A minimal first CI integration can stop after scan, validate, and signature diff. Add AIR, feature reports, policy decisions, and PR comments once the team agrees on how signals should be read.
