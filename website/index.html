Reading paths

The nav follows the intended sequence.

The top-level pages are not independent slogans. They form the public reading route from formal architecture theory, through the AAT-to-SFT interface, into field-shaped software evolution and bounded observation artifacts.

For theory readers Start with AAT, then read the chapter clusters and Lean status. For boundary readers Use the interface before translating AAT claims into SFT terms. For SFT readers Read SFT after the dependency and claim boundary are explicit. For tooling readers Use ArchSig as measurement and reporting, not theorem promotion. For public articles Route outreach essays back to the canonical English pages. For maintainer context Use the profile for author identity, contact routes, and publication boundary.
