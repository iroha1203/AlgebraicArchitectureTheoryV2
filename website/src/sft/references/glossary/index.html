Terms

Field
The selected organizational and technical state from which supported software-evolution paths are read.
Force
An artifact-mediated or governance-mediated change in operation support, selection policy, or observation boundary.
Dissipation
Loss of available support, attention, evidence, or capacity as work moves through the field.
Attractor
A recurrent supported path pattern that the field tends to make natural; not a physical destiny.
Basin
The region of field states from which a recurrent path pattern or stable region becomes reachable under selected support.
ForecastCone
A bounded set of reachable futures under a field estimate, support relation, policy, and horizon.
ConsequenceEnvelope
A readable projection of a forecast cone into path classes, affected axes, obstruction candidates, missing invariants, and governance questions.
Support
The relation that makes operations available, cheap, reviewable, visible, or otherwise plausible inside a field.
Policy
A selection rule or governance constraint that filters supported operations and shapes the cone.
Calibration
The empirical process of comparing forecast items with observed PR, review, CI, incident, runtime, or architecture outcomes.

AAT / SFT / ArchSig

AAT provides the architecture algebra. SFT studies field-shaped software evolution using that algebra as an architecture projection. ArchSig supplies bounded diagnostic and review artifacts. The three layers are connected, but their claims have different evidence standards.
