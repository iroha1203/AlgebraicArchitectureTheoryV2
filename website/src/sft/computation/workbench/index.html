How does a formal cone become reviewable engineering evidence with its boundary intact?

Envelope

The envelope is a loss-aware projection. It preserves the facts reviewers can act on: reachable path classes, affected architecture regions, comparable signature axes, expected axis delta ranges, obstruction candidates, missing boundaries, and unknown remainder.

This projection makes a formal cone usable in engineering work. SFT computes bounded reachable paths, then carries the relevant consequences into review: touched regions, missing invariants, moving signature axes, and questions that should become review, CI, or issue decomposition work.

Vocabulary

ConsequenceEnvelope

A reviewer-facing projection of one or more ForecastCones into path classes, affected regions, signature axes, obstruction candidates, missing boundaries, and unknown remainder.

Audience
Reviewers, issue authors, CI designers, and tool reports.
Shape
Loss-aware report projection from a selected cone family.

Projection rule. An envelope can be useful before it is complete. Its job is to preserve the review surface, the evidence boundary, and the unknown remainder.

Proposal accounting

A proposal first appears as pressure before it becomes an accepted transition. SFT separates raw proposal pressure from accepted, rejected, delayed, unresolved, and review-mediated outcomes. That accounting matters because rejected and delayed paths still teach the field.

This is especially important in AI-mediated development. A generated patch that fails to land can still reveal a low-cost shortcut, a missing invariant, a review bottleneck, or a dangerous default path. SFT treats rejected and delayed proposals as field evidence.

Vocabulary

Proposal accounting

The bookkeeping that separates generated proposal pressure from accepted, rejected, delayed, unresolved, and review-mediated transitions.

Why
Rejected and delayed proposals still reveal support, policy, and governance pressure.
Use
Feeds review mediation, feedback update, and later calibration records.

Computational problems

Once cones and envelopes exist, SFT becomes a family of computable work items for software evolution. Each problem has inputs, outputs, support assumptions, observation boundaries, and update records.

Problem Question
Field reconstruction What bounded SoftwareFieldEstimate is supported by artifact, review, CI, and incident traces?
Support inference Which operation families are available, natural, cheap, dangerous, or selected in the current field?
Envelope generation What path classes, signature axes, obstruction candidates, and missing invariants follow from a proposal?
Cone narrowing Which constraints preserve the intended feature direction while removing selected witness families?
Feedback update How should forecast error, review mediation, CI outcome, and unexpected witnesses update the posterior field?
Support safety checking Does selected support or governance preserve the selected safe region over accepted paths?
AI proposal governance How can AI-generated proposal support be bounded by the field model, theorem boundary, review, and CI?
Lifecycle decision Should the field move toward repair, migration, contraction, deletion, or end-of-life under current capacity and risk?

The problems are deliberately heterogeneous. Some are reconstruction problems, some are generation problems, some are safety checks, and some are governance or lifecycle decisions. What unifies them is the SFT shape: selected field estimate, operation support, observation boundary, forecast or envelope, and feedback update.

Computational reading. SFT gives software evolution a bounded problem surface that can be generated, reviewed, narrowed, and calibrated.

ArchSig pipeline

A practical SFT system is a pipeline from proposal artifact to bounded review surface. The output is a structured object that keeps forecast boundary, unknown remainder, and later outcome references attached to the same computation.

Artifact Role
artifact-descriptor-v0 Captures source artifact, action class, scope, refs, and missing evidence.
operation-support-estimate-v0 Records candidate operation families, constraints, forbidden support, and unknown remainder.
forecast-cone-skeleton-v0 Stores finite support refs, bounded horizon, path class candidates, and forecast boundary.
consequence-envelope-report-v0 Projects cone skeletons into reviewer-facing regions, axes, witnesses, and recommendations.

Simulator maturity

SFT can be implemented gradually. A tool may begin as an action classifier, grow into support generation, then cone skeleton generation, and later become calibrated forecasting. The ladder keeps the ambition high while making each engineering milestone concrete.

Level Reading Minimum output
1 Action classification Artifact descriptor and action class.
2 Support generation Candidate operation support and forbidden support.
3 Cone generation Bounded path classes and forecast boundary.
4 ConsequenceEnvelope Signature axes, witnesses, missing boundaries, review recommendations.
5 Calibration Forecast item refs joined to observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome refs.

The minimum line for SFT as a computation theory is cone generation. The practical value becomes strong when envelopes guide review. Scientific evaluation begins when forecasts are linked to held-out traces and calibration records.

Lifecycle decisions

A software field grows, stabilizes, drifts, repairs, migrates, contracts, and sometimes exits. In SFT, an end-of-life decision becomes a field reconfiguration problem over architecture signatures, operation cost, runtime risk, ownership capacity, and envelope width.

Intervention Reading Main comparison axes
Repair Keep the current field and add local support or invariants. repair adoption, missing invariant, runtime regression risk, ownership capacity.
Migration Move support and memory from an old field into a new field. projection mismatch, dual-run cost, consumer compatibility, rollback path.
Contraction Reduce unsupported surface and reallocate field capacity. removed operation family, affected runtime boundary, simplified signature axes.
Deletion Remove a field component or subsystem from the lifecycle. dependent operation loss, data or API retirement boundary, incident avoidance, unknown remainder.

The envelope gives these choices a common comparison surface. A wide envelope can indicate migration pressure, repair pressure, contraction pressure, or deletion pressure. A cheap repair can preserve the current path or leave future safety unresolved. The decision remains relative to selected field capacity and observation boundary.

Calibration

Definition 2-4. Calibration is the empirical linkage between bounded forecast artifacts and later observed PR, review, CI, incident, and outcome references under an explicit benchmark protocol.

Vocabulary

Calibration

The empirical linkage between forecast items and later observed PR, review, CI, incident, rollback, or outcome records.

Categories
Matched, unmatched, unavailable, private, non-comparable, inconclusive.
Purpose
Turns SFT from review artifact generation into an empirically testable research program.

Workbench habit. The envelope is useful before calibration as a review artifact. Calibration begins when the same forecast items are joined to observed outcomes.

Calibration is also where SFT becomes an empirical research program. PRD-to-issue, issue-to-PR signature, review mediation, incident feedback, AI shortcut, and lifecycle decision benchmarks all ask the same core question: did the bounded forecast artifact preserve enough structure to be compared with later traces?

Proposition 2-4. A ConsequenceEnvelope is a loss-aware projection from one or more ForecastCones into reviewer-facing regions, axes, witnesses, missing boundaries, and recommendations.

Proof idea. The projection selects visible path classes, affected regions, comparable axes, obstruction candidates, and boundary items from the cone family under the observation boundary. The preserved object is the review surface plus its unknown remainder.

Example 2-4. A coupon envelope can report a lawful policy insertion path, a hidden PaymentAdapter dependency path, a rounding semantic obstruction path, and an unknown remainder. Reviewers can turn that report into issue decomposition and invariant questions before the PR is written.
