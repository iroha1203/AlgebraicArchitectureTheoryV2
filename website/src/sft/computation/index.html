Introduction

SFT makes software evolution computable by selecting a bounded field estimate and keeping its boundary explicit. The development field remains large; the computation names which evidence, projection, support, policy, horizon, and observation boundary have been selected.

A SoftwareFieldEstimate records selected evidence, unavailable evidence, and unknown remainder. AAT supplies the local algebra for the architecture projection. ArchSig supplies bounded observations. SFT adds artifact action, support, policy, step semantics, horizons, ForecastCones, workbench projections, and feedback hooks.

This is how software evolution becomes a computable object. SFT computes a bounded space of paths: which architecture futures remain reachable when this field estimate, support relation, policy, observation boundary, and horizon are selected.

Chapters

Part 2 develops the computation in four steps: interface, computational core, ForecastCone, and workbench.

  • 2-1. InterfaceConnects bounded field estimates to AAT projection, ArchSig observation, and explicit observation boundaries.
  • 2-2. Computational CoreDefines artifact action, operation support, policy, step relation, and bounded paths as the engine of SFT computation.
  • 2-3. ForecastConeComputes bounded reachable futures, reachability regions, support safety, and cone narrowing from supported paths.
  • 2-4. WorkbenchProjects cones into reviewer-facing envelopes, proposal accounting records, ArchSig artifacts, computational problems, and calibration hooks.
