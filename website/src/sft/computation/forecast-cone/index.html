Which futures become reachable when a field, support relation, and horizon are fixed?

ForecastCone

Vocabulary

ForecastCone

The bounded set of reachable field paths generated from a starting field, a support relation, step semantics, and a finite horizon.

Shape
Bounded path set from a selected starting field.
Use
Turns reachability, widening, narrowing, and safety into theorem-shaped questions.

The cone is the central mathematical image of SFT. It gathers the futures a field can reach under stated support, step semantics, and horizon. A broad prompt can widen it; a precise constraint can narrow it; governance can change the support from which the next cone is generated.

Cone narrowing gives the first theorem-shaped control over this object. A constraint that preserves the intended feature direction and removes a selected witness family changes support. When the narrower support simulates into the wider support, the narrower cone projects into the wider cone over the same horizon.

Reachability regions

Once the cone is explicit, SFT separates reachability questions that ordinary review often compresses into one risk judgment. May-reach, must-reach, stable-region, and reachable preimage become different computational objects.

Vocabulary

StableRegion

A region of field states closed under selected accepted steps.

May reach
At least one supported path reaches the target region within horizon.
Must reach
Every selected supported path reaches the target region within horizon.

This gives attractor language a disciplined reading. A field can have stability, recurrence, policy preference, probability mass, or a visible default path; SFT asks which structure carries the word in the selected model.

Support safety

Definition 2-3. Support safety studies selected supported steps against a selected safe region or a simulated safer transition system over a bounded horizon.

Computation habit. Compute supported paths first; then identify which constraints narrow, preserve, or redirect the paths that matter.

Proposition 2-3. Pointwise support inclusion plus step simulation gives a projection from the narrower ForecastCone into the wider ForecastCone over the same bounded horizon.

Proof sketch. Induct on the horizon. At horizon zero, the starting fields are related by assumption. At the successor horizon, take a supported step in the narrower system. Step simulation supplies a corresponding step in the wider system and preserves the field relation. Apply the induction hypothesis to the remaining suffix.
