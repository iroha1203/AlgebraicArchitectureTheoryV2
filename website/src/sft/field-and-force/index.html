Introduction

Lehman's laws placed change at the center of software engineering. Useful software lives with its environment: users move, organizations move, requirements move, and every change adds memory that future changes must inherit.

SFT continues that line by naming the field in which change is produced. Requirements, artifacts, agents, ownership, review, CI, incidents, and governance become forces that make the next change likely, cheap, visible, reviewable, or nearly unthinkable.

Chapters

Part 1 develops the vocabulary in four steps: field, force, organization, and governance.

  • 1-1. Field Why a codebase and its development organization form the state from which future operation support is read.
  • 1-2. Force Why PRDs, issues, specs, reviews, incidents, and AI proposals act on the future shape of change.
  • 1-3. Organization How developers, AI agents, ownership, communication paths, and Conway-style pressure create recurrent development paths.
  • 1-4. Governance How review, CI, type checking, incidents, and postmortems update field memory and shape architecture futures.
