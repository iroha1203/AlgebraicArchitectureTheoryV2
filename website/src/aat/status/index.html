Status vocabulary

Status Reading Typical artifact
proved A Lean theorem establishes the claim under its stated premises. Theorem, bridge, accessor, finite example, counterexample.
defined only A carrier, schema, predicate, metric, or report shape exists. Structure, inductive type, definition, metadata package.
future proof obligation A theorem-shaped statement belongs to the research plan. Proof obligation, issue, planned bridge theorem.
empirical hypothesis A claim belongs to repository behavior, review quality, or operational evidence. Study design, dataset, CI report, pilot protocol.

Status rule 1.1. A website statement with a Lean name should be read at the status of that Lean declaration. A carrier gives vocabulary; a theorem gives a conclusion; an example theorem demonstrates a finite reading; an empirical hypothesis belongs to observed repository behavior.

Formal core

The current AAT formal core starts at AtomAxiomSystem and AAT.AATCore, then builds molecules, laws, obstruction circuits, zero-curvature packages, architecture signatures, operation packages, path geometry, analytic representation, and finite examples around that root.

Status 3.1. The formal core is a family of checked carriers and theorem packages. Each chapter names the package it uses, so the reader can move from public explanation to the corresponding Lean surface without changing the claim being read.

Proved surface

The proved surface is broadest where the claim is finite, selected, and theorem-package shaped. Representative proved anchors include atom non-generation accessors, law-relative obstruction circuits, finite witness-count zero bridges, required signature-axis lawfulness bridges, selected repair measure decrease, synthesis candidate soundness, path invariant preservation, homotopy observation equality, diagram filler refutation, analytic preservation/reflection accessors, and canonical counterexamples.

Defined carriers

Many AAT surfaces are intentionally defined before every intended bridge theorem is proved. This includes operation kinds, role schemas, calculus-law carriers, signature axes, report metadata, analytic extension formula packages, selected continuation readings, and some higher-level theorem-package registries.

Status 4.1. A defined carrier gives vocabulary and type-level discipline. A proved theorem gives the corresponding conclusion.

Status 4.2. The expected pattern is carrier first, bridge theorem second, example theorem third, and empirical evaluation separately. This lets AAT publish vocabulary before overclaiming completeness.

Obligations and hypotheses

Future proof obligations focus on broader completeness, richer operation laws, extended geometry, representation strength, and cross-package bridges. Empirical hypotheses belong to extraction quality, review usefulness, operational prediction, cost, and adoption evidence.

Status 5.1. Proof obligations extend theorem packages; empirical hypotheses evaluate how those packages behave when attached to real repositories, review workflows, and operational evidence.
