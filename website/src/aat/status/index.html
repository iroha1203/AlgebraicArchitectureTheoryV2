Status vocabulary

Since the peer-review hardening pass (PRD-R), theorem-grade statuses in the AG ledgers are read through a three-way normalization, alongside three further non-theorem tags.

Status Reading
proved A real Lean proof. Assumptions are explicit, and the conclusion is not simply read off a field of an assumption package.
packaged (assumption-relative) A consequence obtained by composing fields of an explicit assumption record, certificate, or selected package. A Lean theorem exists, but it is not counted as an unconditional proved under peer review.
statement-only A typed candidate surface exists in Lean, but it is not counted as a proved theorem. Candidate rows also marked as future proof obligations keep their obligation separately.
defined only A carrier, structure, predicate, or metadata package exists. It gives vocabulary, not a conclusion. Outside the three-way normalization.
future proof obligation A theorem-shaped statement recorded in the obligations ledger as planned work, often with an explicit non-goal boundary. Outside the three-way normalization.
empirical hypothesis A claim about repository behavior, extraction quality, or operational evidence. It belongs to the tooling and downstream ledgers, not to the AG mathematical ledger.

Status rule 1.1. A theorem label in the canonical text or on this website is read as Lean proved only when the AG Lean theorem index has a corresponding ledger row. A label without a ledger row is a mathematical statement of the canonical text, at the grade the text assigns it, and nothing more.

Ledger discipline

Three documents carry the formal accounting, and this page is a summary of them, not a replacement.

  • docs/aat/lean_theorem_index_ag_aat.md maps each canonical-text label of Parts I–X to its Lean declarations in Formal/AG with a status column. It is organized by the ten formalization tranches (PRD-1 through PRD-10), one per part.
  • docs/aat/proof_obligations_ag_aat.md is the per-part obligations ledger: residual boundaries, future proof obligations, explicit non-goals, and the PRD-R re-judgment of the old acceptance criteria.
  • docs/aat/lean_ag_peer_review_inventory.md defines the proved / packaged (assumption-relative) / statement-only normalization and holds the row-level overlay used to reclassify earlier statuses.

The whole Formal/AG tower builds with lake build, and the ledgers record that the declaration-level scan for axiom, admit, sorry, and unsafe over Formal/AG has no matches.

Part-by-part Lean surface

Each part of the monograph has a dedicated tranche in Formal/AG. The anchors below are representative proved rows of the AG Lean theorem index; the index itself remains the complete listing.

Part I — Atoms, Objects, and Laws

Formal/AG/Atom/ and Formal/AG/Examples/FiniteModel.lean. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.ArchitectureObject.atom_origin (every architecture object is generated from an atom family), AAT.AG.lawfulness_iff_omegaU_zero (lawfulness iff zero aggregate obstruction, relative to a required law universe and zero-reflecting aggregation), and AAT.AG.concreteThreeReadingAgreement (the concrete three-reading agreement built from the required-law index). Theorem I.10.5 is carried by AAT.AG.AATCorePackage with the HEq-free existence theorems proved and the older ThreeReadingAgreementAssumptions surface kept as packaged (assumption-relative).

Part II — Architecture Geometry, Sites, and Sheaves

Formal/AG/Site/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.Site.AATGrothendieckTopology.eq_coverage_toGrothendieck (the generated topology agrees with the Mathlib coverage bridge under explicit pullback and base-change premises), AAT.AG.Site.AATSheafCondition.descent (descent read from the sheaf condition), and AAT.AG.Site.finitePosetCechCohomology_vanishes_above_nerveDimension (finite poset Čech cohomology vanishes above the nerve dimension). The standard finite-poset Čech route of PRD-10 R1 is exposed here with its packaged standardFinitePosetCechComplex surface.

Part III — Law Algebra, Obstruction Ideal, and the Lawful Locus

Formal/AG/LawAlgebra/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.LawAlgebra.SquareFreeWitnessRegime.obstructionIdeal_eq_stanleyReisnerIdeal (the square-free obstruction ideal is the Stanley–Reisner ideal), AAT.AG.LawAlgebra.IdempotentCollapse.quotientAlgEquivPi (finite idempotent coordinate algebras collapse onto Boolean point products, with higher-Tor and Kähler vanishing), and AAT.AG.LawAlgebra.LawfulSectionData.lawful_iff_factorsThroughLawfulLocus (a section is lawful iff it factors through the lawful locus). The Lawfulness–Ideal Correspondence (lawfulnessIdealCorrespondence) is packaged (assumption-relative), and the Architecture Nullstellensatz (ArchitectureNullstellensatzCandidate) is statement-only.

Part IV — Obstruction Cohomology

Formal/AG/Cohomology/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.Cohomology.CoverRelativeCechComplex.d_comp_d_eq_zero (the cover-relative Čech differential squares to zero), AAT.AG.Cohomology.localFlatnessGap_no_globalLawfulSection (a nonzero hidden-coupling class forbids a compatible global lawful section), and AAT.AG.Cohomology.BoundaryResidueHypotheses.boundaryResidueTheorem (selected global U-flatness iff zero boundary holonomy, under its explicit hypothesis block). The cohomological flatness criterion is packaged (assumption-relative); the low-degree five-term sequences (Theorem candidate 10.4 / 14.2) are statement-only.

Part V — Derived Law Geometry and Repair

Formal/AG/Derived/. Part VI — Singularity, Monodromy, and Stacks

Formal/AG/SingularityMonodromyStack/. Part VII — Representation, Periods, and Analysis

Formal/AG/RepresentationAnalysis/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.RepresentationAnalysis.FiniteDirectedGraphTarget.adjacencyMatrixPower_eq_zero_at_card_of_acyclic (adjacency-matrix nilpotence for acyclic finite graphs), AAT.AG.RepresentationAnalysis.PeriodSeparationExample62.periodSeparation_theorem6_1 (period separation: equal graph reading, distinct semantic and effect readings), and AAT.AG.RepresentationAnalysis.RepresentationConservativityUnderAdequacy.representation_conservativity_under_adequacy (under adequacy and witness exactness, analytically-zero classes are the zero class). Part VIII — Measurement Theory

Formal/AG/Measurement/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.Measurement.MeasurementVerdict.unmeasured_ne_measured_zero (verdict discipline: unmeasured is never measured-zero), AAT.AG.Measurement.stanleyReisner_alexanderDual_repair (Stanley–Reisner / Alexander-dual repair: minimal generators correspond to minimal vertex covers), and AAT.AG.Measurement.finiteHodgeDecomposition (finite Hodge decomposition under explicit inner-product cochain-model data). Part IX — Evolution Geometry

Formal/AG/Evolution/. Proved anchors: AAT.AG.Evolution.TemporalDescentCriterion.temporal_descent_criterion (a vanishing temporal cohomology class with compatible adjusted data yields a global replay transition) and AAT.AG.Evolution.FiniteDissipationStopping.no_infinite_nonterminal_path (well-founded dissipation admits no infinite non-terminal evolution path). Part X — Semantic Repair Descent and the SAGA Comparison Theorem

Appendix — Status ledger of the canonical text

The appendix of the monograph is the canonical status ledger for undefined terms, primitive predicates, and future design obligations. It has no separate Lean tranche; its entries are tracked through the per-part ledgers above.

Obligations and hypotheses

The obligations ledger keeps theorem-shaped future work named and bounded. Representative entries include the Architecture Nullstellensatz proof (III.Theorem candidate 7.2A), the low-degree five-term sequences (IV.Theorem candidate 10.4 / 14.2), CotangentComplexGeneralConstruction, NonAbelianGerbeCohomologyGeneralTheory, GeneralSingularHomologyRealization, and ForceIntegrabilityObstructionCandidate. Many of these are paired with explicit non-goals: the current tranches deliberately do not claim general derived categories, general cotangent complexes, algebraic-stack general theory, or unconditional analytic-to-structural inferences.

Empirical hypotheses — extraction quality, review usefulness, operational prediction — are not rows of the AG mathematical ledger. They live with the classical and downstream ledgers and the tooling documentation, entered through docs/aat/proof_obligations.md.
