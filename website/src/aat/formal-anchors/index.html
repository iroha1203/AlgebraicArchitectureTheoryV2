Role and scope

The canonical AAT text is published here as eleven chapter pages: Parts I through X and the appendix. The formalization lives in the Lean namespace AAT.AG under Formal/AG, and the correspondence between the text's labeled claims and Lean declarations is tracked row by row in the ledger document docs/aat/lean_theorem_index_ag_aat.md.

Each part below shows a selection of that ledger: the chapter page, the Lean source files, and five to ten representative rows with their canonical text label, representative Lean declarations, and status. The rows shown here are representative anchors, not the full inventory; the complete enumeration of declarations and statuses is the ledger itself, docs/aat/lean_theorem_index_ag_aat.md, read together with the proof-obligation ledger docs/aat/proof_obligations_ag_aat.md.

How statuses are read

AAT states its claims relative to a fixed vocabulary, law universe, coverage topology, coefficient sheaf, and representation family. The canonical text keeps three claim grades distinct.

Formal theorem
A mathematical proposition proved from the axioms, definitions, and stated assumptions.
Certified bounded inference
A relative inference that holds under explicitly stated coverage, exactness, or witness-completeness assumptions. It is read inside those assumptions, never as an unconditional absolute claim.
Analytic reading
A way of reading a constructed geometric object through graphs, signatures, periods, or metrics.

Status phrases in the tables below are quoted from the ledger. defined only marks vocabulary placed in Lean without a theorem claim; proved marks a completed Lean proof; phrases of the form proved under explicit ... assumptions mark certified bounded inferences whose assumptions are explicit Lean arguments; statement only and theorem candidate statement-only mark statements placed without a proof claim. Since the PRD-R R0 inventory, theorem-grade statuses are additionally normalized by docs/aat/lean_ag_peer_review_inventory.md into the three-way vocabulary proved / packaged (assumption-relative) / statement-only: theorems read off structure-field accessors or explicit assumption packages are classified as packaged (assumption-relative) rather than as unconditional proved.

Appendix

The appendix chapter is the status ledger of the canonical text: it collects the principal undefined terms, primitive predicates, and future design obligations of Parts I through X. Its audit surface is the ledger pair itself — docs/aat/lean_theorem_index_ag_aat.md for implemented Lean declarations and docs/aat/proof_obligations_ag_aat.md for proof obligations and empirical hypotheses — together with the peer-review status normalization in docs/aat/lean_ag_peer_review_inventory.md. The appendix carries no Lean declaration rows of its own, so this page anchors it to those documents rather than to a declaration table.
