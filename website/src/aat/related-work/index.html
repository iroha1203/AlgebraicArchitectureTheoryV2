Novelty claim

AAT's distinctive contribution is a full algebro-geometric stratification of architecture reasoning. Lawfulness is not defined on a dependency graph; it is defined as the lawful locus — the zero set of an obstruction ideal sheaf generated by law equations inside a ringed AAT site. Local law failures that refuse to glue globally appear as cohomology classes. Repair is read through derived geometry, and semantic repair gluing is compared with cover-relative Čech cohomology in the SAGA comparison theorems.

Novelty 1.1. The central novelty is the tower: Atom axioms generate atom families and architecture objects; architecture contexts with a coverage topology form an AAT site; sheaves of architectural data and a structure sheaf of law algebras make it a ringed topos; law equations generate an obstruction ideal sheaf whose zero set is the lawful locus; obstruction cohomology, derived law geometry, singularity and monodromy readings, measurement profiles, evolution geometry, and the SAGA comparison rise on top of that base. No single stage is claimed as unprecedented; the tower as one connected discipline, with explicit claim grades and Lean status ledgers, is the contribution.

Novelty 1.2 (Non-replacement). AAT does not replace architecture description standards, review methods, metrics suites, graph analyzers, or formal verification. Each neighbor keeps its own strength; AAT supplies the geometric carrier on which their findings can be stated with an inspectable scope, coefficient choice, and claim grade.

Contribution

AAT organizes neighboring architecture methods around one relative geometry. Fixing an atom vocabulary, a law universe, a coverage topology, and a coefficient ring determines an architecture geometry, and every claim is read inside it. The grammar asks which site the claim lives on, which sheaf carries its data, which law equations cut it out, and which cohomological or measurement reading supports it.

Architecture description

Architecture description standards such as ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 provide the reader-facing vocabulary of viewpoints, concerns, and correspondence rules. AAT gives that vocabulary a geometric carrier: a viewpoint selects local contexts on the AAT site, a concern selects law equations in the law algebra, and a correspondence rule selects restriction maps and descent conditions between overlapping contexts.

Position 3.1. A view is a local context W on the AAT site; a concern is a family of law equations contributing to the obstruction ideal over W; a correspondence rule is compatibility data on overlaps whose failure to glue is a descent obstruction rather than an informal inconsistency.

Concern Law equations in the law algebra Generators of the obstruction ideal over the selected context.
Correspondence Restriction / descent data Compatibility on overlaps; gluing failure is a cohomological obstruction.

Review methods

Review methods such as ATAM make risk concrete through scenarios and tradeoffs. AAT preserves that concreteness by locating a scenario on the site: a scenario selects a cover, a family of local sections, and the law equations those sections must satisfy. The review finding then names where it lives and what evidence supports it.

Position 4.1. A scenario is an input to AAT when it selects a cover of contexts, local witnesses of law satisfaction or failure, and a coefficient reading. A tradeoff that holds locally on every chart but not globally is exactly the situation obstruction cohomology is built to name.

Position 4.2. A review finding becomes theorem-shaped when it names its architecture geometry X_C^{V,U,J,k}, its cover, its witness sections, and its claim grade. The review still supplies judgement and prioritization; AAT supplies the geometric grammar in which the finding is stated and bounded.

Metrics and graphs

Metrics and graph analysis become readings inside AAT's finite measurement geometry. Cycle counts, violation counts, spectral readings, and transport costs are measured on finite poset sites, cellular sheaves, Stanley-Reisner complexes, Tor groups, period pairings, and Laplacians, always within a selected measurement profile that fixes the data source, coefficients, and resolution.

Position 5.1. The same codebase supports a graph reading, a sheaf reading, a cohomological reading, and a period reading. AAT keeps these readings aligned by deriving them from one geometry: each is an analytic reading of X_C^{V,U,J,k}, and the bridges between them are stated, not assumed.

Position 5.2. Metrics become strongest when their zero readings are tied to the lawful locus: a measured zero supports a lawfulness conclusion only through the stated soundness, completeness, and coverage assumptions of the selected profile, and a nonzero value locates its obstruction on a named context rather than in an aggregate score.

Formal methods

Lean gives AAT a checked core for the tower's definitions, bridges, equivalences, finite models, and counterexamples. The formal subset is deliberately stated through status labels: proved, defined only, future proof obligation, and canonical-text statement. A theorem label without a ledger row is a mathematical statement in the canonical text, not a current Lean proved claim.

Position 6.1. AAT's formal contribution is strongest where a Lean package connects a stage of the geometric tower — atom axioms, site and sheaf structure, law algebra and lawful locus, cohomology, or the SAGA comparison — to a proved statement whose assumptions are visible in the statement itself.

Formal-methods pattern AAT use

Category and sheaf models

Categorical and sheaf-theoretic models of software have used composition, functoriality, and diagrams as organizing ideas. AAT commits to a specific and heavier apparatus: an architecture context category with a coverage topology forms an AAT site; sheaves of architectural data and a structure sheaf of law algebras make it a ringed topos; affine AAT charts glue, when the cocycle conditions hold, into an architecture scheme; and derived intersections, cotangent complexes, monodromy representations, and decomposition stacks carry the singular and stacky phenomena.

Position 7.1. The apparatus stays architecture-facing. The site's covers are selected architectural decompositions; the obstruction ideal is generated by law equations about dependencies, contracts, effects, and semantics; a nonzero H^1 class is a concrete statement that local law repairs exist on every chart of a cover but cannot be glued into one global repair.

Position 7.2. The stratification is used with discipline: ringed AAT topos is the base floor; the word scheme is used in its strong sense only when chart compatibility holds; derived and stacky readings are invoked only where lawful loci genuinely intersect non-transversally or decompositions genuinely carry automorphisms. The vocabulary is a graded commitment, not a metaphor applied uniformly.

Reading rules

Adjacent method gives AAT asks AAT produces
A view or model Which contexts and covers on the AAT site? A bounded local-to-global architecture judgement.
A scenario or risk Which law equations and which witness sections? A located obstruction reading with a claim grade.
A metric or graph fact Which measurement profile and coefficient ring? An analytic reading scoped to its profile.
A formal specification Which stage of the geometric tower does it discharge? A Lean status label and ledger row.

This is the reason AAT reads as a synthesis rather than a replacement. The neighboring method supplies the surface; AAT supplies the site, the sheaf, the ideal, and the claim grade that make the conclusion inspectable. The chapters from Atoms, Objects, and Laws through Semantic Repair and SAGA carry the constructions this page compares against.
