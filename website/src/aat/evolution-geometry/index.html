Part IX

Evolution Geometry

Part VIII fixed the conditions under which AAT geometry is read measurably inside a fixed finite measurement profile. An architecture, however, also appears as a sequence of selected geometries under selected operations, repairs, migrations, and state transitions. Part IX defines the minimal structure that handles the time direction while staying inside the purely mathematical scope of the AAT text: a fixed trace category, a state transition presheaf, a temporal coefficient, temporal laws and obstructions, a temporal descent criterion, dissipative policies with finite stopping, a Lyapunov reading, and a force integrability reading.

How to read the claims

AAT states its claims relative to a fixed vocabulary, law universe, coverage topology, coefficient sheaf, and representation family. Three claim grades are kept distinct throughout the monograph.

Formal theorem
A mathematical proposition proved from the axioms, definitions, and stated assumptions.
Certified bounded inference
A relative inference that holds under explicitly stated coverage, exactness, or witness-completeness assumptions. It is never an unconditional absolute claim.
Analytic reading
A way of reading a constructed geometric object through graphs, signatures, periods, or metrics.

A theorem candidate is a candidate for a future definition and proof design; it becomes a central Lean proof obligation only when a corresponding ledger row exists. A principle fixes a claim boundary, a way of reading, or a non-claim. Theorem labels on this page follow the canonical text. A label carries a Lean status badge only when the AG Lean index has a corresponding ledger row.

The claims of Part IX are confined to the selected trace category, state transition presheaf, and temporal coefficient. About events, transitions, and state spaces outside that selection, the text is silent.

1. From static to evolution geometry

Part VIII read AAT geometry measurably inside a fixed finite measurement profile. Part IX adds the time direction: an architecture also appears as a sequence of selected geometries produced by selected operations, repairs, migrations, and state transitions. 

static geometry:
  X

evolution geometry:
  X_0 -> X_1 -> ... -> X_n

The goal of Part IX is to define the minimal structure for the time direction while preserving the purely mathematical scope of the AAT text. Temporal laws and obstructions are read inside a fixed trace category, state transition presheaf, temporal coefficient, law universe, and measurement profile.

2. Trace category

Definition IX.2.1 (Trace Category). For an architecture evolution profile E, the trace category is written \( \mathrm{Tr}_E \). Its objects are selected time points, operation stages, or abstract event states; its morphisms represent selected transitions.

Representative morphisms:

apply transition
replay transition
project state
compensate action
migrate state
invert selected transition
compose transitions

Which transitions are adopted as objects and arrows is part of the evolution profile.

Principle IX.2.2 (Trace Relativity). Temporal laws are relativized to the selected trace category: 

different trace category
  -> different temporal law reading.

About unselected events, paths, and external state, the AAT text claims neither zero, nor lawful, nor safe. This relativity is the counterexample boundary of the section: the same architecture under two different trace categories yields two different temporal readings, and neither reading extends to the other's unselected transitions.

3. State transition presheaf

Definition IX.3.1 (State Transition Presheaf). Fix an AAT site X and a trace category \( \mathrm{Tr}_E \). The state transition presheaf assigns to each context W and trace object t the state space and transition monoid visible on W at time t: 

St_A(W,t)
  =
  State_A(W,t)
  together with
  Trans_A(W,t)

Here State_A is the selected state presheaf and Trans_A is the selected transition monoid presheaf. Restriction follows context restriction: 

res_{W,V} : St_A(W,t) -> St_A(V,t)

When the descent condition is confirmed in the selected regime, this presheaf is called a state transition sheaf.

Definition IX.3.2 (Temporal Coefficient). The abelian coefficient sheaf measuring differences, mismatches, and gluing defects of temporal law data is the temporal coefficient, written \( \mathrm{TempCoeff}_A \). It serves as the coefficient of temporal obstructions once a selected regime is fixed in which differences of states and transitions read as abelian coefficients.

Definition IX.3.3 (Temporal Law). A temporal law is read as a commutative diagram or relation over St_A and \( \mathrm{Tr}_E \). Representative examples: 

replay(e, replay(e, s)) = replay(e, s)
decode(encode(s)) ~ s
compensate(action(s)) ~ s
migrate(project_old(s)) = project_new(migrateEvents(s))

These are equations or descent conditions on the selected state transition presheaf, not slogans.

Definition IX.3.4 (Temporal Obstruction). When a temporal law L_t holds locally on a selected context family yet fails to cohere with trace composition or restriction, the mismatch is a temporal obstruction:

\[ \mathrm{Ob}_t \in H^n(\mathrm{Tr}_E \times X,\ \mathrm{TempCoeff}_A). \]

Here \( \mathrm{Tr}_E \times X \) is a selected product site or incidence model built from the trace category and the AAT site. When the product structure, the temporal coefficient, and the mismatch cocycle are not fixed, the display above is treated as a reading schema rather than a definition — this is the explicit non-conclusion boundary of the section.

4. Temporal descent

Definition IX.4.1 (Replay Descent Data). Fix a cover U = {W_i -> W} and a trace arrow e : t -> t'. Suppose that on each W_i there is replay data 

r_i : State_A(W_i,t) -> State_A(W_i,t')

When the differences on overlaps read as a 1-cocycle m(r) of the temporal coefficient, the family is called replay descent data.

Theorem IX.4.2 — Certified bounded inference

Temporal Descent Criterion

Lean: proved

Assume:

Tr_E and X are finite.
selected product/incidence site Tr_E x X is fixed.
TempCoeff_A is an abelian coefficient sheaf in the selected regime.
local replay data has a temporal mismatch cocycle m(r).
[m(r)] = 0 in H^1(Tr_E x X, TempCoeff_A).

Then, in the selected regime, after local adjustment the replay data glues to a global transition: 

temporal mismatch class = 0
  +
adjust local replay data by a 0-cochain
  =>
global replay transition exists.
Proof idea
Inside the fixed trace category and temporal coefficient, the vanishing of the mismatch class means the mismatch cocycle is a coboundary. Adjusting the local replay data by the corresponding 0-cochain kills the overlap differences, so the adjusted local data satisfies the compatibility needed to glue into a global replay transition. The reading is exactly the Part IV descent pattern transported to the temporal incidence site: local transition data with coboundary mismatch glues globally.
Lean anchors
ReplayDescentData with mismatch, localReplay, GlobalReplayTransition, zeroMismatchCocycle, zeroMismatchClass; ReplayMismatchCocycle with differential_zero_holds and toTemporalCocycle; EffectiveTemporalAdjustment with the proved equation m(adjust(c,r)) = m(r) - d c (mismatch_adjust_eq); and TemporalDescentCriterion with class_matches_mismatch, class_vanishes, adjusted_data_compatible, and the theorem temporal_descent_criterion — a proved theorem package (IX.§4 / R5).
Claim boundary
The Lean package proves the one-directional criterion: a vanishing class together with a compatible adjustment yields the global replay transition. The conclusion is read inside the selected finite trace regime, temporal cover, and coefficient; it issues no claim about unselected covers or coefficients.

A finite example and a finite counterexample both live in the Lean fixture layer (IX.R10). The zero-mismatch fixture (zeroReplayDescentData, replay_zero_theorem42_global_transition_exists) reads a global replay transition through Theorem IX.4.2. The pseudo-circle fixture (pseudoCircleMismatch, pseudoCircleMismatch_ab_nonzero, replay_nonzero_edge_witness) exhibits a nonzero replay mismatch on a selected edge, so no vanishing-class conclusion is available there — the criterion stays silent, as it should.

5. Dissipative policy

Definition IX.5.1 (Evolution Functional). Inside a measurement profile M, a reading \( \Phi_M(A) \) assigning a nonnegative value to an architecture state A is an evolution functional. Representative examples: 

obstruction mass
harmonic mass ||h(g)||
distance-to-flatness
transfer residue norm

Definition IX.5.2 (Dissipative Policy). An operation family F is dissipative with respect to \( \Phi_M \) when every selected operation f : A -> B satisfies \( \Phi_M(B) \le \Phi_M(A) \). It is strictly dissipative when the inequality is strict at selected non-terminal states.

Theorem IX.5.3 — Certified bounded inference

Finite Dissipation Stopping

Lean: proved

Assume:

selected state set is finite.
Phi_M takes values in a well-founded ordered set.
selected operation policy is strictly dissipative outside terminal states.

Then every selected evolution path reaches a terminal state in finite time.

Proof idea
A path that stays outside terminal states produces a strictly decreasing sequence of \( \Phi_M \) values. In a well-founded order no infinite strictly descending sequence exists, so no infinite path stays outside the terminal states; a maximal selected path with executable endpoints therefore ends at a terminal state.
Lean anchors
no_infinite_descending_sequence, InfiniteSelectedEvolutionPath with StaysOutsideTerminal and sourceValue_strict_decrease, FiniteDissipationStopping with no_infinite_nonterminal_path, and SelectedEvolutionPath.maximal_path_reaches_terminal — a proved theorem package (IX.定理5.3 / R7), on top of the defined-only surface EvolutionFunctional, DissipativePolicy, TerminalState, StrictlyDissipativeOutsideTerminal, SelectedEvolutionPath with pathwise non-increase and strict-decrease predicates (IX.§5.1-5.2 / R6, defined only with proved accessors).
Claim boundary
Reaching a terminal state and the terminal state being lawful are separate matters: lawfulness of the terminal state additionally requires the obstruction vanishing, exactness, and coverage assumptions of Parts III and IV. The Lean fixture layer keeps this boundary concrete: the two-step fixture reaches its terminal by Theorem IX.5.3 (twoStep_dissipation_reaches_terminal_by_theorem53), while nonlawful_terminal_is_terminal and nonlawful_terminal_not_lawful exhibit a terminal state that is not lawful.

6. Lyapunov reading

Definition IX.6.1 (AAT Lyapunov Reading). An evolution functional \( \Phi_M \) is an AAT Lyapunov reading when it is non-increasing along a dissipative policy and takes its minimum near selected obstruction zero. When a harmonic debt decomposition is available,

\[ \Phi_M(A) = \lVert h(g_A) \rVert \]

is the representative example: the harmonic mass of Part VIII read as a Lyapunov functional along the evolution.

Principle IX.6.2 (Lyapunov Is Not Forecast). A Lyapunov reading reads dissipativity inside the selected finite evolution profile. It makes no claim about unselected transitions, unconstructed state spaces, or arbitrary future paths: 

dissipative in profile
  !=
dissipative outside the selected trace category.

This principle is the non-conclusion boundary of the section: the reading is a statement about the selected profile, never a forecast.

7. Force integrability reading

Definition IX.7.1 (Force). A force on evolution geometry is a selected transition sending an architecture state to the next state: 

F : A_t -> A_{t+1}

A force whose local law data integrates to global law data is an integrable force.

Theorem candidate IX.7.2

Force Integrability Obstruction

Lean: statement-only

Suppose the temporal mismatch class produced by a force F,

\[ \mathrm{ob}(F) \in H^1(\mathrm{Tr}_E \times X,\ \mathrm{TempCoeff}_A), \]

is defined. Then, when \( \mathrm{ob}(F) \neq 0 \), the expectation is that the selected temporal law data does not integrate to a global evolution along F.

Status as a candidate
This becomes a theorem only once the construction of \( \mathrm{ob}(F) \), coefficient exactness, the trace product site, and the detectability of temporal descent are fixed. Until then it is a theorem candidate in the canonical text, read through Theorem IX.4.2 as its intended proof route.

8. Conclusion of Part IX

Part IX formalizes the time direction of AAT geometry through the trace category, the state transition presheaf, and the temporal coefficient: 

Trace category
  -> State transition presheaf
  -> Temporal coefficient
  -> Temporal law
  -> Temporal obstruction
  -> Dissipative policy
  -> Lyapunov reading

With this tower, AAT handles purely mathematically the situation 

local transition laws
but
global temporal obstruction remains

Part IX makes no claim about unselected traces, unconstructed transitions, or arbitrary future paths. Its conclusions are read only inside the fixed temporal and evolution geometry. Part X connects the law equations of Part III, the cover-relative Čech \( H^1 \) of Part IV, and the repair theory of Part V into one theorem chain inside selected finite data.

Terms

Trace category
\( \mathrm{Tr}_E \): selected time points, stages, or event states with selected transitions as morphisms; temporal laws are relativized to it (Principle IX.2.2).
State transition presheaf
St_A(W,t): the state space and transition monoid visible on context W at trace object t, with restriction along contexts; a sheaf when the selected descent condition is confirmed.
Temporal coefficient
\( \mathrm{TempCoeff}_A \): the abelian coefficient sheaf measuring differences and gluing defects of temporal law data.
Temporal obstruction
The mismatch class \( \mathrm{Ob}_t \in H^n(\mathrm{Tr}_E \times X, \mathrm{TempCoeff}_A) \) of a locally holding temporal law; a reading schema until the product site, coefficient, and cocycle are fixed.
Replay descent data
Local replay maps over a cover whose overlap differences read as a temporal 1-cocycle; the input of Theorem IX.4.2.
Dissipative policy
An operation family along which the evolution functional \( \Phi_M \) never increases, strictly decreasing outside terminal states in the strict case; finite stopping under well-foundedness (Theorem IX.5.3).
Lyapunov reading
A non-increasing evolution functional minimized near selected obstruction zero; dissipativity inside the profile, never a forecast (Principle IX.6.2).
Force
A selected transition F : A_t -> A_{t+1}; its integrability obstruction is Theorem candidate IX.7.2.
