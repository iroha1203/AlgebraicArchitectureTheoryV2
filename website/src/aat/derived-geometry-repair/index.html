Part V

Derived Law Geometry and Repair

When an architecture must satisfy two law universes at once, the classical intersection remembers the common zeros but forgets how the loci meet. Part V reads latent law conflict as the derived intersection \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \), defines law conflict sheaves as Tor sheaves, and rebuilds repair as controlled movement through lawful loci.

1. From Part IV to Part V

Can an architecture be \( U \)-lawful and \( V \)-lawful at once?

Part III defined the lawful locus of a law universe \( U \) as the zero locus of the obstruction ideal sheaf, \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_U) \). Part IV asked, for a single law universe, whether local lawful sections glue to a global lawful section, and read the failure as an obstruction cohomology class: 

forall i, Flat_U(W_i)
and
[g] = 0 in H^1(X, Ob_U)
--------------------------------
Flat_U(W)

Part V asks a different question. Two or more law universes \( U, V \) live over the same architecture geometry \( X \), each with its own lawful locus \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \). Joint lawfulness is not merely the problem of solving \( I_U = 0 \) and \( I_V = 0 \) simultaneously: what matters is how \( I_U \) and \( I_V \) intersect — whether the two families of law constraints cross transversely. Two laws that are separately satisfiable can leave a latent compatibility obstruction when satisfied together, and the classical intersection loses sight of it. 

lawfulness is vanishing.
joint lawfulness is intersection.
law conflict is derived non-transversality.

The residue retained by the derived intersection is what makes repair a substantive operation: repair is not mere local improvement but the movement, resolution, or transfer of derived obstruction between lawful loci.

2. Lawful loci

Definition V.2.1 (Lawful locus for a law universe). For a law universe \( U \), write the obstruction ideal sheaf as \( I_U \subset \mathcal{O}_X \), abbreviating the Part III notation \( I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \). The lawful locus of \( U \) is \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_U) \), and likewise \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) = V(I_V) \) for a law universe \( V \).

Principle V.2.2 (Law universes are distinct readings). \( U \) and \( V \) are different law readings of the same architecture geometry \( X \). Representative examples: 

U_cycle:
  dependency cycle law universe.

U_substitution:
  substitution compatibility law universe.

U_authority:
  authority / effect law universe.

U_semantic:
  semantic consistency law universe.

U_runtime:
  runtime interaction law universe.

A law universe does not generate atoms. It specifies which defect sections are read, which ideal is formed, and which lawful locus is cut out: 

law does not create atoms.
law cuts out loci.

Definition V.2.3 (Derived lawful locus). Suppose the closed defect data of \( U \) is given as a defect section valued in a perfect module or vector bundle \( E_U \): \( \delta_U \in \Gamma(X, E_U^{\vee}) \). The derived lawful locus of \( U \) is the derived zero locus of \( \delta_U \), written \( \mathrm{Flat}_U^{\mathrm{der}}(X) \). Locally its structure sheaf is presented by the Koszul complex,

\[ \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U^{\mathrm{der}}} \simeq \mathrm{Kosz}(\mathcal{O}_X;\, \delta_U), \]

and the classical lawful locus is recovered as its truncation:

\[ t_0\, \mathrm{Flat}_U^{\mathrm{der}}(X) = \mathrm{Flat}_U(X). \]

When one starts from the ideal \( I_U \) alone, a family of selected defect sections generating \( I_U \) is chosen and their Koszul complex is used. Where the choice of generators or perfectness matters, the construction of the derived zero locus is stated explicitly as an assumption.

Principle V.2.4 (Classical locus is the truncation). \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_U) \) is the classical vanishing locus of the closed equational law, while \( \mathrm{Flat}_U^{\mathrm{der}}(X) \) retains the derived residue of how the law equations vanish: 

classical lawful locus:
  common zeros.

derived lawful locus:
  common zeros plus higher vanishing data.

Law conflict in the sections below can therefore be read not only on the intersection of classical loci but as the interaction of derived lawful loci.

3. Classical intersection

Definition V.3.1 (Classical joint lawful locus). The classical locus of architectures satisfying both \( U \) and \( V \) is

\[ \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) = V(I_U + I_V), \]

the locus on which every \( \delta \in I_U \) and every \( \varepsilon \in I_V \) vanish.

Principle V.3.2 (Classical intersection forgets how). The classical intersection records the result of the intersection but does not sufficiently remember how the two loci met: 

classical intersection remembers the common zeros.
derived intersection remembers the way of intersection.

A repair achieving \( U \)-lawfulness and a repair achieving \( V \)-lawfulness may each exist separately without being compatible as one architecture deformation: 

U-safe
and
V-safe
does not imply
jointly safe

This difference can be invisible in the classical intersection.

4. Derived intersection

Definition V.4.1 (Derived joint lawful locus). The derived intersection of the lawful loci is written \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \): the intersection of \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \) retained together with its derived excess structure. When derived lawful loci are constructed, it is read more structurally as \( \mathrm{Flat}_U^{\mathrm{der}}(X) \times_X^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V^{\mathrm{der}}(X) \). Locally the structure sheaf is given formally by the derived tensor product:

\[ \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V} = \mathcal{O}_X / I_U \otimes^{L}_{\mathcal{O}_X} \mathcal{O}_X / I_V. \]

This formula is read sheaf-theoretically: on a local chart it is computed as a derived tensor product of commutative rings, and globally one works with the derived tensor sheaf or its hypercohomology: 

local derived intersection:
  (O_X/I_U) tensor^L_{O_X} (O_X/I_V) as a complex of O_X-modules.

global derived reading:
  derived global sections / hypercohomology of the local complex.

Principle V.4.2 (Derived intersection remembers conflict). Beyond the classical common zeros, the derived intersection retains the excess structure that appears when the two ideals meet non-transversely: 

transverse laws:
  derived residue vanishes.

non-transverse laws:
  derived residue remains.

This residue is read as a latent compatibility obstruction between the laws themselves: 

law conflict is not merely two laws both failing.
law conflict is non-transverse interaction of lawful loci.

Example V.4.3 (Non-transverse laws). Suppose a repair that fixes a cycle law splits a component boundary. The dependency cycle disappears, but the semantic boundary or the authority / effect boundary can be distorted: 

repair cycle obstruction
  -> creates semantic boundary residue

This is not a mere side effect of the repair; it is the appearance of the non-transversality of the intersection of \( \mathrm{Flat}_{\mathrm{cycle}}(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_{\mathrm{semantic}}(X) \).

5. Law conflict sheaf

Definition V.5.1 (First law conflict sheaf). For law universes \( U \) and \( V \), the first law conflict sheaf is

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/I_U,\ \mathcal{O}_X/I_V), \]

the sheaf-theoretic obstruction expressing that the intersection of the lawful loci is non-transverse at first order. With a fixed cohomological grading convention it is read as the degree \( -1 \) part of the derived intersection:

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \cong H^{-1}\big(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V}\big). \]

When a global conflict group is needed, one further fixes either of: 

H^0(X, LawConflict_1(U,V)):
  global sections of the first conflict sheaf.

hypercohomology:
  derived global reading of
  O_X/I_U tensor^L_{O_X} O_X/I_V.

Definition V.5.2 (Higher law conflict sheaves). The derived intersection can carry non-transversality beyond first order. For \( i > 0 \) set

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_i^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/I_U,\ \mathcal{O}_X/I_V). \]

\( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1 \) is the low-degree component and reads first-order non-transversality; \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i \) reads higher coherence, multi-boundary, and derived repair obstruction. When the full derived intersection is read, the basic object is \( \mathcal{O}_X/I_U \otimes^{L}_{\mathcal{O}_X} \mathcal{O}_X/I_V \), and each \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i \) is a component of its homology or cohomology sheaves under the selected grading convention.

Principle V.5.3 (Conflict is structural). \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \) is not a defect count: 

defect count:
  how many failures are visible.

law conflict object:
  how law ideals fail to intersect transversely.

Definition V.5.4 (Monomial law conflict regime). When, on a local chart \( W \), the obstruction ideals of \( U \) and \( V \) are given as square-free monomial ideals \( I_U(W), I_V(W) \subset R_W \) over the same polynomial witness algebra \( R_W = k[E_W] \), the pair \( (U,V) \) is in the monomial law conflict regime on \( W \), and

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V)\big|_W = \mathrm{Tor}_i^{R_W}\big(R_W/I_U(W),\ R_W/I_V(W)\big). \]

Proposition V.5.5

The multidegrees appearing in the computation are read as least common multiples of a forbidden \( U \)-support and a forbidden \( V \)-support, that is, as combined witness supports:

Example V.5.6 (Shared witness factor conflict). Take the local coordinate ring \( R = k[x,y,z] \) and let the two law universes select the forbidden witness supports 

Forb_U(W) = { {x,y} }
Forb_V(W) = { {x,z} }
I_U = < x y >
I_V = < x z >

Here \( x \) is a shared boundary witness, \( y \) is the extra witness on the \( U \) side, and \( z \) is the extra witness on the \( V \) side. Both ideals are principal monomial ideals, yet the shared factor \( x \) makes the intersection derived non-transverse. Tensoring the resolution

\[ 0 \to R \xrightarrow{\ xy\ } R \to R/I_U \to 0 \]

with \( R/I_V \) gives \( \mathrm{Tor}_1^{R}(R/I_U, R/I_V) = \ker\big( R/I_V \xrightarrow{\ xy\ } R/I_V \big) \). The class of \( z \) lies in the kernel, since \( xy \cdot z = xyz \in \langle xz \rangle \). Therefore

\[ \mathrm{Tor}_1^{R}\big(R/\langle xy \rangle,\ R/\langle xz \rangle\big) \neq 0. \]

In AAT terms:

U forbids the joint support {x,y}.
V forbids the joint support {x,z}.
both laws share boundary witness x.
repairing one side can transfer residue through the shared support.

6. Derived Law Conflict theorem

Theorem V.6.1

Derived Law Conflict

packaged (assumption-relative)

O_X is a commutative structure sheaf

I_V subset O_X

Any repair or deformation that handles a \( U \)-lawful deformation and a \( V \)-lawful deformation simultaneously must account for this residue:

7. Non-transversality of laws

Definition V.7.1 (Transverse law universes). \( U \) and \( V \) intersect transversely at first order when \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) = 0 \). Then no first derived conflict remains on the intersection of \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \): transverse law intersection means no first derived law conflict.

Definition V.7.2 (Derived-transverse law universes). \( U \) and \( V \) intersect derived-transversely when every positive-degree conflict vanishes:

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = 0 \quad \text{for all } i > 0. \]

Equivalently, locally the derived tensor product \( \mathcal{O}_X/I_U \otimes^{L}_{\mathcal{O}_X} \mathcal{O}_X/I_V \) carries no higher Tor residue — the same information as the classical tensor product. This condition is strictly stronger than \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1 = 0 \): if first-order conflict vanishes but a higher conflict remains, the pair is not called derived-transverse.

Theorem V.7.3

Derived Transversality Criterion

packaged (assumption-relative)

Assume:

O_X is a commutative structure sheaf
I_U and I_V define the selected lawful loci
derived tensor product is defined in the chosen category
the grading convention for Tor is fixed

Then \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \) are, in the selected derived reading, derived-transversely compatible if

\[ \mathrm{Tor}_i^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/I_U,\ \mathcal{O}_X/I_V) = 0 \quad \text{for all } i > 0; \]

conversely, whenever a positive-degree Tor survives, the pair carries a derived law conflict in that degree. Even when classical common zeros exist, nonzero positive-degree Tor leaves a derived residue in the way the loci meet.

Definition V.7.4 (Non-transverse law universes). \( U \) and \( V \) intersect non-transversely when \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) \neq 0 \) for some \( i > 0 \). The joint realization of the two law universes then carries a derived law conflict: non-transverse law intersection is a latent compatibility obstruction. When first-order non-transversality is meant specifically, one writes \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \neq 0 \).

Principle V.7.5 (Non-transversality is not failure count). Even with few visible failures, if the lawful loci meet non-transversely a repair can transfer obstruction to another axis: 

few visible defects
but
nonzero derived conflict

A locally small-looking change can still touch the constraint imposed by the derived conflict.

8. Repair path

Convention for the rest of Part V: no theorem is stated without a fixed comparison profile or pairing. Every repair theorem is read relative to a fixed profile and to whether the comparison is at the section level or the geometry level. The descent question — whether local repairs glue to a global repair — is treated as an equivalence theorem over a selected finite cover in Part X.

Definition V.8.1 (Repair comparison profile). To state that a repair improves anything, a comparison structure is fixed. A repair comparison profile \( P_U \) contains at least two comparisons: 

prec^{sec}_{P_U}:
  section-level comparison.

prec^{geom}_{P_U}:
  geometry / object-level comparison.

The section-level comparison compares two sections \( s, s' : T \to X \); the geometry-level comparison compares whole presentations of the architecture geometry or scheme, induced from section-level comparisons along all selected test sections or a designated family of probes. Representative profiles: 

ideal-order repair:
  s'^* I_U subsetneq s^* I_U at the section level.

valuation repair:
  nu_U(s') < nu_U(s).

rank repair:
  rank Ob_U(s') < rank Ob_U(s).

support repair:
  Supp(s'^* I_U) subsetneq Supp(s^* I_U).

Comparisons are written generically as \( s' \prec^{\mathrm{sec}}_{P_U} s \) and \( X' \prec^{\mathrm{geom}}_{P_U} X \).

Definition V.8.2 (Pullback repair profile). To compare obstructions across a geometric repair \( r : X \to X' \), the contravariance of scheme morphisms is made explicit. Where needed, \( P_U \) contains the comparison map

\[ r^{-1} I'_U \cdot \mathcal{O}_X \ \le_{P_U}\ I_U, \]

where \( I_U \subset \mathcal{O}_X \) is the obstruction ideal before repair and \( I'_U \subset \mathcal{O}_{X'} \) after; the left side pulls \( I'_U \) back to \( X \) for the comparison. When a probe family \( s : T \to X \) is fixed, the section-level form \( (r \circ s)^{*} I'_U \le_{P_U} s^{*} I_U \) is used. Repair improvement therefore splits into at least two kinds: 

Internal repair:
  section deformation s -> s' on the same X.
  obstruction comparison is s'^* I_U <=_{P_U} s^* I_U.

Geometric repair:
  architecture geometry morphism r : X -> X'.
  obstruction comparison is made after a chosen pullback / probe profile.

Definition V.8.3 (Conflict comparison profile). To state that a repair does not increase derived conflict, a comparison profile for the law conflict sheaves is fixed separately from the obstruction ideal. For a pair \( (U,V) \), a degree set \( D \subset \mathbb{N}_{>0} \), and a repair \( r : X \to X' \), the conflict comparison profile \( Q_{U,V} \) contains at least: 

degree profile:
  selected positive degrees D.

comparison maps:
  cmp_i(r) :
    pullback_r LawConflict'_i(U,V)
      -> LawConflict_i(U,V)
  or probe-level maps along selected s : T -> X.

order / vanishing predicate:
  <=_{Q_{U,V}} on selected conflict readings,
  and a notion of zero / nonzero after comparison.

Here \( \mathrm{LawConflict}'_i(U,V) \) is the conflict sheaf computed on the post-repair geometry \( X' \); whether pullback, pushforward, or probe-level comparison is used is part of \( Q_{U,V} \). The repair does not increase the selected derived conflict when 

pullback_r LawConflict'_i(U,V)
  <=_{Q_{U,V}}
LawConflict_i(U,V)
for all i in D

or when the selected probe-level comparison with the same meaning holds.

Definition V.8.4 (Repair path). A repair path is a deformation or morphism on architecture geometry, written \( r : X \to X' \), or a deformation of sections \( s \to s' \). The repair decreases the \( U \)-axis obstruction when, under the fixed profile \( P_U \), \( s' \prec^{\mathrm{sec}}_{P_U} s \), that is, \( s'^{*} I_U \) is smaller than \( s^{*} I_U \) under \( P_U \).

Principle V.8.5 (Repair is not scalar improvement). Repair is not the improvement of a single scalar score: 

repair:
  morphism in architecture geometry.

selected improvement:
  projection of repair to selected law axis.

Obstruction can decrease on one axis while a transferred obstruction arises on another: 

selected repair decrease
  does not certify
all axes non-increasing

This gap can arise as a structural phenomenon from the non-transversality of the lawful loci.

9. No monotone repair guarantee

Principle V.9.1 (No Monotone Repair Guarantee). Suppose a repair path \( r : X \to X' \) decreases the \( U \)-axis obstruction under the geometry-level comparison of a fixed profile \( P_U \): \( X' \prec^{\mathrm{geom}}_{P_U} X \). Suppose further that this profile carries only the \( U \)-axis comparison and no \( V \)-axis monotonicity rule — no \( V \)-axis monotonicity certificate is assumed. Inside this profile, the decrease of the \( U \)-axis obstruction alone does not license the claim that the \( V \)-axis obstruction is non-increasing: 

U-axis obstruction decreases
does not certify
V-axis obstruction non-increases

This is a claim-boundary discipline: a fixed comparison profile speaks only the monotonicity it carries. That \( U \)-axis improvement actually fails to imply \( V \)-axis non-increase is shown by the monomial counterexample family below. \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \neq 0 \) is a structural witness of the derived non-transversality that can explain this non-implication; to assert transfer along a specific repair path, the support-localized pairing of Definition V.10.4 is fixed.

Reading of Principle V.9.1: a \( U \)-axis repair moves the section closer to \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \). But when the derived intersection of \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \) and \( \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \) is non-transverse, movement toward \( \mathrm{Flat}_U \) through the non-transverse intersection does not guarantee monotone movement toward \( \mathrm{Flat}_V \). Watching only the classical intersection, this residue looks like a side effect; in derived law geometry it is a candidate for the structural compatibility residue read by \( \mathrm{Tor}_1 \). Attribution to a concrete repair direction is stated with the support-localized pairing of Definition V.10.4.

10. Transferred obstruction

Definition V.10.1 (Transferred obstruction). When a repair \( r \) decreases the \( U \)-axis obstruction while the \( V \)-axis obstruction increases or becomes manifest, that \( V \)-axis obstruction is called a transferred obstruction, written \( \mathrm{TransOb}_{U \to V}(r) \). It is read as: 

U obstruction decreases.
V obstruction appears or increases.
selected derived conflict class is paired nontrivially with the repair direction.

Example V.10.2 (Repair transfer). Representative transfers: 

cycle repair
  -> semantic boundary obstruction

component split
  -> authority / effect obstruction

state consolidation
  -> runtime interaction obstruction

contract simplification
  -> substitution obstruction

feature isolation
  -> boundary holonomy

These can be read not as mere implementation failures but as derived non-transversality between lawful loci — provided the target law universes, conflict support, repair direction, and transfer pairing are fixed.

Principle V.10.3 (Transfer is relative). Transferred obstruction is relativized to the fixed pair of law universes: \( \mathrm{TransOb}_{U \to V} \). Changing \( U \) and \( V \) changes what appears as transfer.

Definition V.10.4 (Repair direction and transfer pairing). To assert that a specific repair transfers obstruction to the \( V \)-axis, one fixes not the whole repair path but its first-order or selected deformation direction \( v \in T_X \), and a selected conflict class obtained from the derived non-transversality of \( U \) and \( V \): \( \kappa_{U,V} \in \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \), or, along the chosen global / local reading, \( \kappa_{U,V} \in H^0(X, \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V)) \). Write \( \mathrm{Supp}(\kappa_{U,V}) \subset X \) for its support. A repair path or direction is support-localized when its image, specialization, or first-order support meets \( \mathrm{Supp}(\kappa_{U,V}) \) nontrivially in the selected coefficient regime. For repairs that are not support-localized, no transfer residue is concluded from ambient \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \neq 0 \) alone. One further fixes the transfer residue target and its zero predicate: 

TransRes_{U,V}:
  selected transfer residue object.

0_{TransRes}:
  distinguished zero residue.

Nontrivial_{TransRes}:
  selected nonzero / nontrivial residue predicate.

The repair transfer pairing is

\[ \langle -, - \rangle_{U,V} : T_X \times \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \to \mathrm{TransRes}_{U,V}, \qquad \mathrm{Transfer}_{U \to V}(v) := \langle v, \kappa_{U,V} \rangle_{U,V}. \]

The target of the pairing is relativized to a selected coefficient module, normal cone reading, or obstruction residue module.

12. Hilbert series conflict accounting

Definition V.12.1 (Graded monomial conflict regime). Let \( R = k[x_1, \dots, x_n] \) be the standard graded polynomial ring. When the lawful loci of \( U \) and \( V \) are defined by homogeneous monomial ideals \( I_U, I_V \subset R \), the pair is in the graded monomial conflict regime, and each \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_i^{R}(R/I_U, R/I_V) \) is read as a graded module.

Theorem V.12.2 — Certified bounded inference

Hilbert Series Conflict Identity

packaged (assumption-relative)

In the graded monomial conflict regime, suppose the Hilbert series of each Tor module is defined. Then, by the Euler characteristic of a graded free resolution,

\[ \frac{H_{R/I_U}(t) \cdot H_{R/I_V}(t)}{H_R(t)} = \sum_i (-1)^i\, H_{\mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V)}(t), \]

where

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_0(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_0^{R}(R/I_U, R/I_V) = R/(I_U + I_V). \]

Proof reading
Tensor a graded free resolution of \( R/I_U \) with \( R/I_V \) and apply the Euler characteristic to Hilbert series. The Hilbert series of a free module is a sum of shifts of \( H_R(t) \), and the homology after tensoring is \( \mathrm{Tor}_i^{R}(R/I_U, R/I_V) \), which yields the alternating sum.

Consequently the interference series

\[ \mathrm{Int}_{U,V}(t) = H_{R/(I_U + I_V)}(t) - \frac{H_{R/I_U}(t) \cdot H_{R/I_V}(t)}{H_R(t)} \]

accounts for the deviation from the value expected of an independent intersection as an alternating sum of Tor mass. In the finite worked example of the appendix, the \( \mathrm{Tor}_0 \) expression makes the Hilbert series of \( R/(I_U + I_V) \) appear as the base term of the interference series.

13. Well-founded repair

Definition V.13.1 (Well-founded repair comparison profile). A repair comparison profile contains a set \( S \) of architecture states, a well-founded order \( <_{\mathrm{rep}} \), and a repair step relation \( A \to_r B \). The profile is a well-founded repair comparison profile when every repair step satisfies \( B <_{\mathrm{rep}} A \).

Definition V.13.2 (Sound repair step). A repair step \( A \to_r B \) is sound when, for the selected target obstruction predicate, one of the following holds: 

target obstruction strictly decreases.
target obstruction is cleared.
selected certificate records why no target repair exists.

Soundness is relativized to the selected target and the selected certificate. It does not mean removal of all obstructions or a shortest repair.

Theorem V.13.3 — Certified bounded inference

Repair Termination

packaged (assumption-relative)

Fix a well-founded repair comparison profile and suppose every selected repair step decreases with respect to \( <_{\mathrm{rep}} \). Then no infinite repair sequence exists: every sequence \( A_0 \to_r A_1 \to_r A_2 \to_r \cdots \) terminates in finite length.

Proof reading
By the definition of well-foundedness: an infinite repair sequence would give an infinite descending chain \( A_0 >_{\mathrm{rep}} A_1 >_{\mathrm{rep}} A_2 >_{\mathrm{rep}} \cdots \), contradicting the well-foundedness of \( <_{\mathrm{rep}} \).

14. Conclusion of Part V

Part V treated the intersection of multiple lawful loci as derived geometry. The first-order non-transversality of the derived intersection \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \) was defined as the first law conflict sheaf \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/I_U, \mathcal{O}_X/I_V) \), extended to all positive degrees by \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_i^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/I_U, \mathcal{O}_X/I_V) \) for \( i > 0 \), and read, under a fixed cohomological grading, as \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \cong H^{-1}(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U \cap^{R} \mathrm{Flat}_V}) \). When \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = 0 \) for all \( i > 0 \), the pair \( U, V \) is derived-transverse in the selected derived reading. The core of Part V: 

law conflict is derived non-transversality.
repair is controlled movement through lawful loci.
bad repair transfers obstruction.
refactoring is derived-geometric deformation.
Hilbert series accounts for graded conflict mass.
well-founded repair terminates under selected comparison profile.

AAT can therefore handle the phenomenon of \( U \)-lawful improvement accompanied by \( V \)-axis obstruction transfer — a structural obstruction residing in the derived intersection of the lawful loci. The next question is where this derived obstruction concentrates in architecture geometry. To read its concentration points, stratification, and path dependence, Part VI proceeds to singularity, monodromy, and stacks.
