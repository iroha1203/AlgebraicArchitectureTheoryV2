Part IV

Obstruction Cohomology

Local lawfulness does not imply global lawfulness, and the difference is a cohomology class. Part IV takes the lawful locus \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U) \) constructed in Part III and asks when local lawful sections glue to a global lawful section. When they do not, the failure lives in \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \): visible failure in \( H^0 \), gluing failure in \( H^1 \), coherence failure in \( H^2 \).

1. From Part III to Part IV

If every local context is lawful, is the whole architecture lawful?

Part III placed a sheaf of commutative rings \( \mathcal{O}_X^U \) on the architecture geometry \( X \) and defined the obstruction ideal sheaf \( I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U \) generated by the selected law defects. Lawfulness then became a zero-locus statement:

\[ \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U). \]

The Lawfulness–Ideal Correspondence of Part III reads, for a single architecture section and under the selected law universe, witness family, signature axes, coverage, descent, and exactness assumptions:

\[ \mathrm{Lawful}_U(s) \iff s^{*} I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0 \iff s \text{ factors through } \mathrm{Flat}_U(X). \]

Part IV changes the question from one section to a family. Fix a cover \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\}_{i \in I} \) and suppose each local section is lawful:

\[ s_i : W_i \to X, \qquad s_i \text{ factors through } \mathrm{Flat}_U(X). \]

Local lawfulness does not automatically produce a global lawful section \( s : W \to X \) with \( s|_{W_i} = s_i \). Even when every defect vanishes on each local context, the gluing on overlaps can fail. That failure is not a single law defect section; it is a local-to-global obstruction class: 

local lawful sections
  + gluing data
  + descent condition
  -------------------
  global lawful section

When this inference fails, the failure is represented by a class in the cohomology group \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \). Whether local lawful sections glue depends on overlap compatibility, the cocycle condition on triple overlaps, the coverage topology, and the type of the coefficient sheaf, not merely on the presence or absence of local defects. The central statement of Part IV is direct: 

local flatness does not imply global flatness.
The gap is obstruction cohomology.

2. Obstruction sheaf

Definition IV.2.1 (Obstruction sheaf). For a law universe \( U \), the obstruction sheaf is written \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \). Its sections \( \mathrm{Ob}_U(W) \) are the obstruction witnesses, defect residues, boundary mismatches, and gluing failure data readable on the context \( W \): the obstruction data visible on \( W \).

Typical members of \( \mathrm{Ob}_U(W) \) include:

law defect residue
boundary mismatch
hidden coupling witness
signature residue
semantic mismatch residue
runtime interaction residue
descent failure witness

Definition IV.2.2 (Coefficient type). Cohomology requires coefficients, and coefficients must be named. In Part IV, \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \) is read as an abelian group sheaf or as a module sheaf over \( \mathcal{O}_X^U \). A set-valued obstruction sheaf can occur, but whenever the ordinary group \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) is used, \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \) is at least an abelian group sheaf.

Principle IV.2.3 (Obstruction layers). Obstruction cohomology has at least three layers, ordered by coefficient strength. Part IV works in the first layer, so the computability of abelian cohomology is never confused with the strength of non-abelian gluing. 

Abelian obstruction layer:
  Ob_U is an abelian group sheaf or O_X^U-module.
  H^n(X, Ob_U) is ordinary sheaf cohomology.

Torsor obstruction layer:
  local lawful sections form a torsor under a sheaf of groups G_U.
  gluing obstruction lies in non-abelian H^1(X, G_U).

Gerbe / stack layer:
  decompositions, refactor equivalences, and semantic identifications
  form groupoids.
  obstruction is a gerbe / stack obstruction class.

Non-abelian torsors, gerbes, and stacks of local obstruction data are treated by the stack-theoretic structures of Part VI. Part IV concentrates on abelianized or module-valued obstruction data.

Definition IV.2.4 (Canonical obstruction package). \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \) is a coefficient sheaf chosen per problem; when the starting point is a closed equational law, the package of this definition is the preferred standard. When the lawful locus \( i : \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \to X \) is cut out by the obstruction ideal \( I_U = I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U \), the standard obstruction package is

\[ \mathrm{Def}_U := I_U, \qquad \mathrm{ConDef}_U := \bigl( I_U / I_U^2 \bigr) \ \text{on } \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \ \text{as an } \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)}\text{-module}, \] \[ \mathrm{DerOb}_U(M) := \operatorname{Ext}^1\!\bigl( L_{\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)/X}, M \bigr) \quad \text{for } M \in \operatorname{Mod}\bigl(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)}\bigr). \]

Here \( I_U / I_U^2 \) is read as the conormal sheaf on the lawful locus, so the coefficient category is \( \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)} \)-modules. \( \mathrm{Def}_U \) is the visible closed defect ideal. \( \mathrm{ConDef}_U \) is the first-order defect residue seen from the lawful locus. \( \mathrm{DerOb}_U(M) \) is the derived obstruction space relative to a selected deformation module \( M \). To compare \( \mathrm{ConDef}_U \) on \( X \), the pushforward along the inclusion is written explicitly as \( i_{*} \mathrm{ConDef}_U \); on the lawful locus one uses the conormal sheaf of the closed immersion, not \( i^{*} I_U / i^{*} I_U^2 \). Before writing any cohomology group, the category in which the coefficient lives is fixed. The degrees then read: 

H^0(X, Def_U):
  visible closed defect sections.

H^1(X, ConDef_U):
  first-order gluing residue of closed defect data.

Ext^1(L_{Flat_U(X)/X}, M):
  deformation obstruction for selected module M.

When \( \mathrm{ConDef}_U \) is treated as a sheaf on the lawful locus, the group is \( H^1(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X), \mathrm{ConDef}_U) \); read on \( X \), it is \( H^1(X, i_{*}\mathrm{ConDef}_U) \). The package exists so that every cohomological claim names which obstruction coefficient it uses: \( \mathrm{Ob}_U = \mathrm{Def}_U \), \( \mathrm{Ob}_U = \mathrm{ConDef}_U \), a module derived from \( L_{\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)/X} \), or an abelianization of torsor or stack obstruction data.

Principle IV.2.5 (Obstruction sheaf relativity). \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \) is relative to the atom vocabulary \( V \), the law universe \( U \), the coverage topology \( J \), the coefficient structure, the selected witness family, and the selected signature axes. Consequently \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) is not an absolute set of architecture debt; it is a group of obstruction classes relative to a fixed AAT geometry and coefficient sheaf.

3. Čech obstruction complex

Definition IV.3.1 (Cover). A cover of a context \( W \) is written \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\}_{i \in I} \), with overlaps \( W_{ij} = W_i \times_W W_j \) and \( W_{ijk} = W_i \times_W W_j \times_W W_k \). These are the pullbacks and overlaps of the AAT site of Part II.

Definition IV.3.2 (Čech cochains). For a cover \( \mathcal{U} \) and the obstruction sheaf \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \), the Čech cochain groups are

\[ C^0(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) = \prod_i \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_i), \qquad C^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) = \prod_{i,j} \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ij}), \qquad C^2(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) = \prod_{i,j,k} \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ijk}), \] and in general \[ C^n(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) = \prod_{i_0, \dots, i_n} \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{i_0 \dots i_n}). \]

\( C^0 \) records families of local obstruction data, \( C^1 \) records mismatches on overlaps, and \( C^2 \) records coherence failures on triple overlaps.

Definition IV.3.3 (Čech differential). The alternating sum of restriction maps defines the differential \( d^n : C^n(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \to C^{n+1}(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \), satisfying \( d^{n+1} \circ d^{n} = 0 \). Cohomology follows:

\[ H^n(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) = \ker d^n / \operatorname{im} d^{n-1}. \]

For a fixed cover \( \mathcal{U} \), this group is the cover-relative Čech obstruction cohomology. The notation \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) is used when the selected topology and coefficient sheaf let Čech cohomology compute sheaf cohomology, or when an explicit refinement system is named and the limit is taken. The comparison theorem (SAGA) that connects the cover-relative \( H^1 \) of a selected finite cover with descent equivalence of repairs and with coefficients generated from the Atom axioms is the subject of Part X.

4. Obstruction cohomology

Definition IV.4.1 (Obstruction cohomology). For the architecture geometry \( X \) and obstruction sheaf \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \), obstruction cohomology is \( H^n(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \), the sheaf cohomology with coefficients in \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \). In concrete computation it is usually handled as the Čech cohomology \( H^n(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) of a cover; the distinction between the two notations is exactly as fixed in Definition IV.3.3.

Meaning IV.4.2 (Cohomological degrees). Each degree has an architectural reading. 

H^0(X, Ob_U):
  global obstruction sections.
  law failure visible in the large.

H^1(X, Ob_U):
  local patches are lawful, but overlaps do not glue.
  hidden coupling, boundary holonomy, interface mismatch.

H^2(X, Ob_U):
  triple overlap / multi-boundary coherence failure.
  decomposition obstruction, semantic coherence failure,
  policy coherence failure.

H^n(X, Ob_U):
  n-fold architecture coherence obstruction.

\( H^0 \) is close to directly visible failure. From \( H^1 \) onward, the classes record gluing failures that no single local section can see: 

H^0: visible obstruction.
H^1: gluing obstruction.
H^2: coherence obstruction.

Principle IV.4.3 (Cohomology is not a metric). Obstruction cohomology is not a scalar score: \( H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) is not a number. Rank, dimension, norm, mass, and representative counts can be layered on top as analytic readings, but those are representations or measures of cohomology classes, not the cohomology itself.

Principle IV.4.4 (Concrete class, not merely nonzero group). Nonvanishing of the group \( H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) alone does not show that a given family of local lawful sections fails to glue; it shows only that nontrivial obstruction classes are possible. To assert a gluing failure, one names the concrete cocycle \( g \in C^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) determined by the local data, its class \( [g] \in H^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \), and the hypothesis \( [g] \neq 0 \). Conversely, to pass from \( [g] = 0 \) to a global lawful section one fixes the torsor or module structure through which local adjustments actually act, together with an effectivity assumption. 

nonzero group:
  possible obstruction classes exist.

nonzero concrete class [g]:
  this local gluing data is obstructed.

5. Local flatness and descent data

Definition IV.5.1 (Local flatness data). For a cover \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\} \), local flatness data is a family of lawful sections \( s_i : W_i \to X \), each factoring through \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \); equivalently \( s_i^{*} I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0 \) for every \( i \).

Definition IV.5.2 (Gluing mismatch). On an overlap \( W_{ij} = W_i \times_W W_j \), the restrictions \( s_i|_{W_{ij}} \) and \( s_j|_{W_{ij}} \) are compared. Their disagreement is the gluing mismatch. For a selected local data sheaf \( F \), a comparison map is assumed given,

\[ \mathrm{mismatch}_{ij} : F(W_{ij}) \times F(W_{ij}) \to \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ij}), \qquad g_{ij} = \mathrm{mismatch}\bigl( s_i|_{W_{ij}},\, s_j|_{W_{ij}} \bigr) \in \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ij}), \]

and the family \( g = (g_{ij}) \) is a 1-cochain \( g \in C^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \).

Principle IV.5.2A (Torsor-normalized mismatch). The strongest standard form of the gluing mismatch arises when the local lawful sections form a pseudo-torsor under a sheaf of groups \( G_U \): each \( \mathrm{Lawful}_U(W_i) \) is a \( G_U(W_i) \)-pseudo-torsor. The overlap difference is then \( g_{ij} = s_j - s_i \in G_U(W_{ij}) \), and the torsor composition law gives, automatically on triple overlaps,

\[ g_{ij} + g_{jk} + g_{ki} = 0. \]

Hence \( g \) is a 1-cocycle and \( [g] \) is a torsor class, with \( [g] = 0 \) if and only if the pseudo-torsor has a global section, provided the torsor action is effective. When Part IV uses ordinary cohomology, this torsor class is read after abelianization or through a module-valued coefficient sheaf; for an arbitrary \( \mathrm{mismatch}_{ij} \), the cocycle condition and the effectivity of local adjustments are assumed separately.

Definition IV.5.3 (Descent obstruction class). When the gluing mismatch \( g \) satisfies the cocycle condition \( d^1 g = 0 \), its cohomology class \( [g] \in H^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) is the descent obstruction class; when it is stable under refinement it is read as \( [g] \in H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \). Under a fixed local adjustment structure: 

[g] = 0:
  mismatch is removable by local adjustment,
  when the adjustment action is fixed and effective.

[g] != 0:
  mismatch is a genuine global obstruction.

6. Hidden coupling class

Definition IV.6.1 (Hidden coupling cocycle). Let \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\} \) be a cover on which every local section factors through \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \). It can still happen that the gluing mismatches \( g_{ij} \in \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ij}) \) of the local lawful sections form a nontrivial cocycle, \( d^1 g = 0 \). This cocycle is the hidden coupling cocycle.

Definition IV.6.2 (Hidden coupling class). The class defined by a hidden coupling cocycle \( g \) is

\[ [\mathrm{hc}_U(X)] = [g] \in H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U). \]

It represents coupling that looks lawful in every local context yet fails to glue on overlaps. This is the precise shape of a familiar architectural situation: 

local modules are lawful.
local services are lawful.
local tests may pass.
overlaps carry nonzero gluing obstruction.

Hidden coupling is a global obstruction that appears as an overlap cocycle in the selected cover and obstruction sheaf.

7. Local Flatness Gap Theorem

Theorem IV.7.1

Local Flatness Gap

proved under explicit soundness data
Claim grade
Certified bounded inference. Assume the cover \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\} \) is \( U \)-adequate for the selected witnesses and axes, every \( s_i \) factors through \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \), and the overlap mismatch defines — as a torsor-normalized cocycle or under an explicit cocycle condition — a hidden coupling class with \( [\mathrm{hc}_U(X)] \neq 0 \) in \( H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \).
Conclusion
No global lawful section restricts to the family: there is no \( s : W \to X \) with \( s|_{W_i} = s_i \) factoring through \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \). Local flatness does not imply global flatness; the gap is \( H^1 \).
Lean names
HiddenCouplingData, CompatibleGlobalLawfulSection, localFlatnessGap_no_globalLawfulSection and its variants via GlobalLawfulRestrictionCoboundaryData and FiniteGlobalRestrictionPointwiseSoundness.
Status detail
AG index: proved under explicit finite overlap-wise restriction/coboundary soundness. The theorem is proved contrapositively: a compatible global lawful section forces the hidden coupling class to vanish.

Proof reading. Suppose a global lawful section \( s : W \to X \) exists with \( s|_{W_i} = s_i \). Then the restrictions agree on overlaps, \( s_i|_{W_{ij}} = s_j|_{W_{ij}} \), so the gluing mismatch cocycle is a coboundary and \( [\mathrm{hc}_U(X)] = 0 \). This contradicts the hypothesis \( [\mathrm{hc}_U(X)] \neq 0 \); hence no such global lawful section exists.

Principle IV.7.2 (Local success is not global lawfulness). Lawfulness of every local context matters, but by itself it is not global lawfulness: 

local lawful
  + compatible overlaps
  + vanishing obstruction class
  =
global lawful

Without compatibility and the vanishing of the cohomology class, local success does not glue into global success.

8. Boundary residue

Definition IV.8.1 (Feature extension cover). A feature extension is written \( C' = C_{\mathrm{core}} \cup F \) with boundary \( B = C_{\mathrm{core}} \cap F \), where \( C_{\mathrm{core}} \) is the core architecture context, \( F \) the feature architecture context, and \( B \) the boundary overlap context. Even when \( C_{\mathrm{core}} \) and \( F \) are each lawful, a mismatch can remain on \( B \): 

C_core is U-flat.
F is U-flat.
Boundary carries residue.

Definition IV.8.2 (Boundary mismatch section). The mismatch visible on the boundary is a global obstruction section over the boundary context: \( b_U \in H^0(B, \mathrm{Ob}_B) \). Representative boundary mismatches include: 

core / feature contract mismatch
authority / effect mismatch
semantic / runtime mismatch
state transition mismatch
projection mismatch
coverage mismatch

Definition IV.8.3 (Boundary connecting homomorphism). When the obstruction sheaf is given as restrictions of one coefficient object on \( C' = C_{\mathrm{core}} \cup F \), and restriction to \( B \) and extension by zero are defined in the standard way, the Mayer–Vietoris triangle in the derived category reads

\[ \mathrm{Ob}_{C'} \longrightarrow \mathrm{Ob}_{\mathrm{core}} \oplus \mathrm{Ob}_F \longrightarrow \mathrm{Ob}_B \longrightarrow \mathrm{Ob}_{C'}[1], \]

and in this case the distinguished triangle is the standard Mayer–Vietoris form for the selected coefficient object, not an extra architectural assumption. If \( \mathrm{Ob}_{\mathrm{core}} \), \( \mathrm{Ob}_F \), and \( \mathrm{Ob}_B \) are chosen independently, the existence of the triangle is assumed separately. The long exact sequence yields the connecting homomorphism

\[ H^0(C_{\mathrm{core}}, \mathrm{Ob}_{\mathrm{core}}) \oplus H^0(F, \mathrm{Ob}_F) \longrightarrow H^0(B, \mathrm{Ob}_B) \xrightarrow{\ \delta\ } H^1(C', \mathrm{Ob}_{C'}), \]

which sends boundary residue to a global obstruction class.

9. Boundary Holonomy Theorem

Definition IV.9.1 (Boundary holonomy). For a boundary mismatch section \( b_U \in H^0(B, \mathrm{Ob}_B) \), the boundary holonomy of the feature extension is

\[ \mathrm{Hol}_U\bigl(\partial(C_{\mathrm{core}}, F)\bigr) := \delta(b_U) \in H^1(C', \mathrm{Ob}_{C'}). \]

It is the obstruction class that remains after traversing the boundary of the feature extension once: 

boundary mismatch
  -> connecting homomorphism
  -> H^1 obstruction class
Theorem IV.9.2

Boundary Residue Theorem

proved under explicit hypotheses
Claim grade
Certified bounded inference. Hypotheses: \( C_{\mathrm{core}} \) and \( F \) are \( U \)-flat; boundary witnesses are covered; axis exactness holds; \( \mathrm{Ob}_{C'} \) is a boundary-exact coefficient object (such as \( \mathrm{ConDef}_U \) or a fixed abelianized torsor coefficient); ring restriction compatibility holds; local lawful adjustments form the selected torsor/module with effective descent; all selected global obstruction classes are represented by \( \delta(b_U) \); and NoHigherBoundaryObstruction\( (C', \mathrm{Ob}_{C'}) \) holds.
Conclusion
\( C' \) is globally \( U \)-flat \( \iff \mathrm{Hol}_U(\partial(C_{\mathrm{core}}, F)) = 0 \iff \delta(b_U) = 0 \).
Lean names
BoundaryResidueHypotheses with boundaryResidueTheorem, globallyUFlat_of_boundaryHolonomy_zero, boundaryHolonomy_zero_of_globallyUFlat.
Status detail
AG index: proved under explicit Boundary Residue hypotheses. The hypothesis block carries every listed assumption as an explicit field; this is not an assumption-free global criterion.

Proof reading. Since \( C_{\mathrm{core}} \) and \( F \) are \( U \)-flat, each local section passes through the lawful locus and the obstruction ideal vanishes along it: \( s_{\mathrm{core}}^{*} I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0 \) and \( s_F^{*} I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0 \). The mismatch remaining in the boundary gluing is collected into \( b_U \) as an element of the selected adjustment torsor/module. By boundary exactness and effective descent, \( \delta(b_U) = 0 \) lets the local adjustment descend to a global section; conversely a global \( U \)-flat section makes the boundary mismatch a coboundary in the same exact sequence, so \( \delta(b_U) = 0 \). The hypotheses that all selected global obstruction classes are represented by \( \delta(b_U) \) and that NoHigherBoundaryObstruction holds are exactly what makes the boundary class a complete criterion. If the boundary-exact coefficient is not fixed, the statement is read one-directionally: a nonzero concrete class blocks gluing, and the equivalence is not claimed. 

feature is locally lawful.
core is locally lawful.
boundary holonomy is nonzero.
therefore the extension is not globally lawful.

Example IV.9.3 (Pseudo-circle boundary model)

Consider a cover by a core context \( C_0 \) and a feature context \( C_1 \) whose overlap splits into two connected boundary channels:

\[ C' = C_0 \cup C_1, \qquad C_0 \cap C_1 = B_{\mathrm{sync}} \sqcup B_{\mathrm{async}}. \]

Take the constant sheaf \( \mathbb{Z} \) as coefficient. The difference between the local lawful sections \( s_0 \in \mathrm{Lawful}_U(C_0) \) and \( s_1 \in \mathrm{Lawful}_U(C_1) \) is read on the two channels as \( r_{\mathrm{sync}}, r_{\mathrm{async}} \in \mathbb{Z} \). The cover-relative Čech complex is

\[ C^0 = \mathbb{Z} e_0 \oplus \mathbb{Z} e_1, \qquad C^1 = \mathbb{Z} b_{\mathrm{sync}} \oplus \mathbb{Z} b_{\mathrm{async}}, \qquad C^n = 0 \ \text{for } n \geq 2, \] with differential \[ d^0(a_0, a_1) = (a_1 - a_0,\; a_1 - a_0). \]

Every element of \( C^1 \) is a 1-cocycle and the coboundaries are the diagonal copy of \( \mathbb{Z} \), so

\[ H^1 = \operatorname{coker}(d^0) = \mathbb{Z}^2 / \Delta(\mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z}, \qquad q(r_{\mathrm{sync}}, r_{\mathrm{async}}) = r_{\mathrm{sync}} - r_{\mathrm{async}}. \]

The boundary residue \( b_U = (r_{\mathrm{sync}}, r_{\mathrm{async}}) \in H^0(B_{\mathrm{sync}}, \mathbb{Z}) \oplus H^0(B_{\mathrm{async}}, \mathbb{Z}) \) has boundary holonomy

\[ \mathrm{Hol}_U\bigl(\partial(C_0, C_1)\bigr) = [b_U] = r_{\mathrm{sync}} - r_{\mathrm{async}} \in \mathbb{Z}. \]

In particular \( b_U = (1, 0) \) gives \( \mathrm{Hol}_U(\partial(C_0, C_1)) = 1 \), and this local lawful data does not glue to a global lawful section: 

forall i, s_i factors through Flat_U(X)
Hol_U(Boundary(C_0,C_1)) != 0
--------------------------------
not exists s : C' -> Flat_U(X)
with s|C_i = s_i after local adjustment.

Example IV.9.4 (Pseudo-circle cycle pairing)

From the same cover, build the chain complex of the Čech nerve with boundary components:

\[ C_1 = \mathbb{Z} e_{\mathrm{sync}} \oplus \mathbb{Z} e_{\mathrm{async}}, \qquad C_0 = \mathbb{Z} v_0 \oplus \mathbb{Z} v_1, \] \[ \partial(e_{\mathrm{sync}}) = v_1 - v_0, \qquad \partial(e_{\mathrm{async}}) = v_1 - v_0. \]

Then \( \gamma = e_{\mathrm{sync}} - e_{\mathrm{async}} \) is a 1-cycle, and pairing it with the cochain \( \omega = (r_{\mathrm{sync}}, r_{\mathrm{async}}) \in C^1 \) gives

\[ \langle \omega, \gamma \rangle = r_{\mathrm{sync}} - r_{\mathrm{async}}, \]

which agrees with the isomorphism \( H^1 \cong \mathbb{Z} \) of Example IV.9.3.

10. H² and higher coherence

Definition IV.10.1 (Triple overlap coherence failure). On a triple overlap \( W_{ijk} = W_i \times_W W_j \times_W W_k \), the gluing data of the pairwise overlaps can fail to cohere with one another. This failure appears as a 2-cocycle \( h_{ijk} \in \mathrm{Ob}_U(W_{ijk}) \) with \( d^2 h = 0 \), and its class lies in \( [h] \in H^2(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \).

Meaning IV.10.2 (H²). \( H^2 \) represents coherence failure one level above plain boundary mismatch. Representative cases: 

triple boundary coherence failure
decomposition obstruction
semantic coherence failure
policy compatibility failure
multi-feature interaction residue

For example, any two features may each be compatible with the core, while gluing all three simultaneously breaks semantic roles, authority boundaries, or state transitions: 

pairwise compatible
but
triple-overlap incompatible

This is an \( H^2 \)-type obstruction.

Principle IV.10.3 (Higher coherence before stack). Part IV reads \( H^2 \) as an abelianized coherence obstruction. Non-abelian higher gluing structure — stacks and gerbes — is deferred to Part VI. This separation keeps Part IV inside the range of obstruction cohomology.

Theorem candidate IV.10.4 (Multi-feature Mayer–Vietoris spectral sequence). Fix a finite feature cover \( C = \bigcup_{i \in I} W_i \) and the coefficient sheaf \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \). When cohomology is defined on each finite intersection \( W_{i_0 \dots i_p} = W_{i_0} \cap \dots \cap W_{i_p} \) and a Čech-to-derived comparison is fixed, the Mayer–Vietoris spectral sequence has the form

\[ E_1^{p,q} = \bigoplus_{i_0 < \dots < i_p} H^q\bigl( W_{i_0 \dots i_p}, \mathrm{Ob}_U \bigr) \;\Longrightarrow\; H^{p+q}(C, \mathrm{Ob}_U), \qquad d_1 = \sum_j (-1)^j \operatorname{res}_j. \]

In particular, even when every pairwise overlap class vanishes, a triple-overlap coherence class can survive through \( d_2(E_2^{0,1}) \subset E_2^{2,0} \) or through \( E_2^{2,0} \neq 0 \):

triple overlap class remains
--------------------------------
H^2(C, Ob_U) may be nonzero.

Theorem IV.11.1

Cohomological Flatness Criterion

proved under explicit hypotheses
Claim grade

Hypotheses: every \( s_i \) factors through \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \); \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \) is an abelian sheaf or \( \mathcal{O}_X^U \)-module on the chosen site; the overlap mismatch cocycle \( g \) is an \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \)-valued Čech 1-cocycle; the local lawful sections form an effective \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \)-torsor; \( \mathcal{U} \) is \( U \)-adequate; obstruction soundness, obstruction completeness, axis exactness, and witness coverage hold; the coefficient object satisfies descent; and the local adjustment action is fixed and effective.

12. Topological debt

Definition IV.12.1 (Cover nerve). For a finite cover \( \mathcal{U} = \{\, W_i \to W \,\} \), the nerve complex with explicit overlap components is written \( N(\mathcal{U}) \): vertices are the charts \( W_i \), edges are the connected components of \( W_i \cap W_j \), and faces are the connected components of triple overlaps. When an overlap has several components, \( N(\mathcal{U}) \) is read as a multigraph or multicomplex.

Theorem IV.12.2

Topological Debt Capacity

proved under explicit accounting data
Claim grade
Certified bounded inference. In a finite poset regime with finite-dimensional \( k \)-vector-space cochain groups, rank-nullity gives the capacity lower bound \[ \dim_k H^1(\mathcal{U}, F) \;\geq\; \dim C^1(\mathcal{U}, F) - \dim C^0(\mathcal{U}, F) - \dim C^2(\mathcal{U}, F). \]
Proof idea
Finite-dimensional linear algebra: \( \dim H^1 = \dim \ker d_1 - \dim \operatorname{im} d_0 \), with \( \dim \ker d_1 \geq \dim C^1 - \dim C^2 \) and \( \dim \operatorname{im} d_0 \leq \dim C^0 \). With fixed stalk dimensions per overlap component, the right-hand side is read directly off the charts, edges, and faces of \( N(\mathcal{U}) \).

Corollary IV.12.3 (Constant coefficient nerve reading). When the coefficient sheaf is the constant sheaf \( k \) with standard restrictions, \( H^1(\mathcal{U}, k) \cong H^1(N(\mathcal{U}), k) \), and therefore \( \dim_k H^1(\mathcal{U}, k) = b_1(N(\mathcal{U})) \): the number of independent loops of the selected nerve complex. 13. Period Stokes accounting

Definition IV.13.1 (Cochain-chain pairing). In a finite poset / Čech homology model, fix an orientation and a cochain-chain pairing \( \langle -, - \rangle : C^n(\mathcal{U}, F) \times C_n(\mathcal{U}, F^{*}) \to k \), standardly by \( \langle \sigma^{*}, \sigma \rangle = 1 \) on a basis simplex \( \sigma \) and its dual basis cochain \( \sigma^{*} \), extended linearly. When boundary and coboundary are adjoint with respect to this pairing, the pairing is called Stokes-compatible.

Theorem IV.13.2

Period Stokes Identity

proved under explicit pairing data
Claim grade
Certified bounded inference. With the standard alternating-sign conventions of the finite poset / Čech model, for every cochain \( \omega \) and chain \( \gamma \): \[ \langle d\omega, \gamma \rangle = \langle \omega, \partial\gamma \rangle. \]
Connecting form
When a boundary mismatch section \( b \) from a feature extension cover and the connecting homomorphism \( \delta : H^0(\partial, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \to H^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) are fixed, and \( \delta \) is constructed as a coboundary representative of the boundary restriction, then \( \langle \delta(b), \gamma \rangle = \langle b, \partial\gamma \rangle \).
Proof idea
Verification on basis simplices: the alternating signs of \( d \) carry the same face incidence numbers as those of \( \partial \), so both sides sum the same incidence contributions. The connecting form applies the identity to the chosen representative.
Lean names
FiniteCechChainComplex, CechCochainChainPairing, StokesCompatiblePairing.cechStokes, ConnectingBoundaryRepresentative.connectingStokes.

The identity means that a period measured along a release loop or feature boundary agrees with the signed accounting of the boundary mismatch.

Principle IV.13.3 (Stokes accounting boundary). The Stokes identity is mathematics inside the constructed AAT geometry. It does not claim that unselected data sources or external procedures preserve the pairing: 

accounting identity in geometry
  !=
external procedure correctness.

Definition IV.13.4 (Extension holonomy accounting reading). For a feature extension \( A \to B \) with extension interface \( f \), boundary mismatch section \( b_f \), and boundary holonomy \( \mathrm{Hol}_U(\partial f) \) all defined in the same Mayer–Vietoris / boundary residue regime, additivity of the selected obstruction accounting is fixed by

\[ \kappa_U(B) = \kappa_U(A) + \kappa_U(f) + \mathrm{Hol}_U(\partial f). \]

Here \( \mathrm{Hol}_U(\partial f) \) is the boundary residue or correction term chosen so that this equality holds. It is therefore an accounting convention on the Mayer–Vietoris / boundary residue regime, not an automatic corollary of Theorem IV.13.2; it can be promoted to a theorem only when the value range, additivity, and boundary-residue detectability of \( \kappa_U \) are fixed separately. 14. Scale-stable debt

Definition IV.14.1 (Aggregation morphism). A change of scale from a fine site to a coarse site is a morphism of finite sites \( \pi : X_{\mathrm{fine}} \to X_{\mathrm{coarse}} \). The pushforward \( \pi_{*} \mathrm{Ob} \) is the obstruction coefficient visible on the coarse site, and \( R^q \pi_{*} \mathrm{Ob} \) reads the higher obstruction that remains inside coarse cells.

Theorem candidate IV.14.2 (Leray five-term debt sequence). For a finite site morphism \( \pi \) and coefficient object \( \mathrm{Ob} \), in a regime where the Leray spectral sequence can be constructed, the expected low-degree five-term exact sequence is

\[ 0 \to H^1(X_{\mathrm{coarse}}, \pi_{*}\mathrm{Ob}) \to H^1(X_{\mathrm{fine}}, \mathrm{Ob}) \to H^0(X_{\mathrm{coarse}}, R^1\pi_{*}\mathrm{Ob}) \to H^2(X_{\mathrm{coarse}}, \pi_{*}\mathrm{Ob}) \to H^2(X_{\mathrm{fine}}, \mathrm{Ob}). \]

The sequence reads how a fine-scale hidden coupling class is distributed between a component still visible at coarse scale and a component localized inside the aggregation units.

Definition IV.14.3 (Scale-stable debt). For a selected aggregation family \( \Pi \), an obstruction class \( \alpha \in H^1(X_{\mathrm{fine}}, \mathrm{Ob}) \) is scale-stable when it comes from the coarse side for every \( \pi \in \Pi \):

\[ \alpha \text{ is scale-stable} \iff \forall \pi \in \Pi,\; \alpha \in \operatorname{im}\bigl( H^1(X_{\mathrm{coarse}}, \pi_{*}\mathrm{Ob}) \to H^1(X_{\mathrm{fine}}, \mathrm{Ob}) \bigr). \]

Scale-stable debt is not an artifact of a particular granularity choice; it is an obstruction class that persists through the selected aggregation family.

Principle IV.14.4 (Scale relativity). Scale stability is relative to the selected aggregation family. No invariance is claimed over all decompositions, all module boundaries, or all runtime groupings.

15. Conclusion of Part IV

Part IV formulated the local-to-global problem of the lawful locus of Part III as obstruction cohomology. Even when \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(I_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U) \) is given, local lawful sections need not glue to a global lawful section; the difference appears as an obstruction class in \( H^1(X, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \). The core of Part IV is the degree reading: 

H^0: visible obstruction.
H^1: gluing obstruction.
H^2: coherence obstruction.

With this, AAT can state precisely the situation 

local lawful
but
global obstruction remains

and read it not as empirical complexity but as a cohomological obstruction. Beyond the core statements, the shape of a finite cover itself controls the capacity of \( H^1 \) (Theorem IV.12.2), the period pairing reads as an accounting identity for boundary mismatch (Theorem IV.13.2), and under change of scale the Leray-type low-degree sequence tracks which obstruction classes persist between fine and coarse sites (Theorem candidate IV.14.2).

The realization that closes this local-to-global gap inside selected finite data — an unbroken theorem chain from the Atom axioms through coefficients, site, and \( H^1 \) — is given in Part X. The next question is what happens when several lawful loci are imposed at once: the intersection \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \) can look correct classically while carrying excess obstruction derivedly. That question leads to the derived law geometry of Part V.
