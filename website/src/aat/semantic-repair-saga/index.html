Part X

Semantic Repair Descent and the SAGA Comparison Theorem

1. From Part IX to Part X

Part III defined a law as a witness ideal and lawfulness as its vanishing. Part IV formalized the gap by which locally lawful sections fail to glue globally, as cover-relative Čech obstruction cohomology. Part V defined a repair as a deformation of a morphism or a section on the architecture geometry. Through Part IX, the static structure and the time-directed structure of AAT geometry were in place. The question that remained open is: 

local repairs may each succeed.
Do they glue to a global repair?

Part X answers this inside selected finite data. The repair-gluing obstruction is read as the boundary membership of a residual (the descent theorem), promoted to a class in the true quotient \( Z^1 / B^1 \) (the semantic repair additive \( H^1 \)), and compared — by the SAGA comparison theorem — with the cover-relative Čech \( H^1 \) on the AAT site whose coefficient is generated by law equations. The central statement of the chapter is: 

repair gluing is descent.
the obstruction is a residual class.
the coefficient is generated by law equations.
semantic repair H^1 and cover-relative Cech H^1
agree under the selected comparison.

The objects of Part X are an architecture object A, its AAT site, a law universe U, and selected finite chart, overlap, repair, and residual data. Throughout, selected means relativization to input data, never an existence claim. Two words are kept distinct in every statement of the chapter:

Supplied
Data given as input and constrained by compatibility conditions.
Generated
What is constructed from the input and proved.

The discipline of Part X is to never accept a structure that appears in a theorem's conclusion as a supplied certificate. For each stage — coefficient, restriction, interpretation, descent, comparison — the statement itself fixes where supplied ends and generated begins.

2. Semantic atoms and repair closure

To speak of gluing repairs, a vocabulary is needed that reads, at atom granularity, what a repair is grounded on.

Definition X.2.1 (Semantic atom projection). A semantic atom projection is a triple 

Lambda : semantic atom vocabulary (a set)
K      : component vocabulary (a set)
pi     : Lambda -> K

K is the granularity at which component-level readings are distinguished; \( \Lambda \) is the semantic granularity behind it. Wherever a fiber of \( \pi \) fails to be a singleton, distinct atoms project to the same component — aliases arise.

Definition X.2.2 (Semantic residual atom). Fix a selected cover datum 

U = ( finite chart family,
      overlap transition data theta,
      holonomy support R(U) subset K )

where \( R(\mathcal{U}) \) is the set of components on which the overlap transition data exhibits a nontrivial gluing mismatch (the reading of Part IV, Definition 5.2); it is part of the selected data. An atom \( a \in \Lambda \) is a semantic residual atom when \( \pi(a) \in R(\mathcal{U}) \). The residual locus is

\[ \mathrm{Res}(\pi, \mathcal{U}) = \pi^{-1}(R(\mathcal{U})). \]

The residual is a raw value determined by the selected cover datum \( \mathcal{U} \); it is not a verdict.

Definition X.2.3 (Repair support and semantic closure). A repair support is a subset \( S \subset \Lambda \). It is semantically closed when \( \mathrm{Res}(\pi, \mathcal{U}) \subset S \): every semantic residual atom lies in \( S \). When a repair's support is semantically closed, the repair is read as a semantic repair certificate.

Definition X.2.4 (Residual component coverage and faithfulness). For a repair support \( S \), two predicates: 

residual component covered:
  for every residual atom a there is s in S with pi(s) = pi(a).

residual component faithful:
  if a residual atom a and s in S satisfy pi(s) = pi(a), then a in S.

Coverage says that someone in the same component is supported; faithfulness says the support returns to the residual atom itself, not to an alias.

Lemma X.2.5 (Closure decomposition). For a repair support \( S \): \( S \) is semantically closed if and only if \( S \) is residual component covered and residual component faithful.

Example X.2.6 (Coverage without faithfulness). Take \( \Lambda = \{ t, s, o \} \) with \( \pi(s) = \pi(o) \) (so s and o project to the same component) and \( \pi(t) \) in a different component. Let the residual locus contain \( \{ o \} \) and take the repair support 

S = { t, s }

Then \( S \) is residual component covered but not faithful, hence not semantically closed. Faithfulness is a substantive condition: being covered at component level does not imply being closed at semantic level.

3. The finite semantic repair-gluing complex and descent

Definition X.3.1 (Finite semantic repair-gluing complex). A finite semantic repair-gluing complex \( \mathfrak{K} \) is the following data: 

charts:
  a finite chart set, with complete enumeration.

overlaps:
  finite overlap sets O(x, x') per chart pair.
  the totality of overlaps is completely enumerated by a finite
  list, and the completeness of this enumeration is part of the data.

C^0:
  a finite set of local repair primitives, completely enumerated.
  each g in C^0 assigns, per overlap w, a left restriction
  res_L(g, w) subset Lambda and a right restriction
  res_R(g, w) subset Lambda.

C^1:
  a finite set of residual repair-gluing cochains, completely
  enumerated. each c in C^1 assigns, per overlap w and atom a,
  a predicate ev(c, w, a): "c charges a on w".

delta^0 : C^0 -> C^1:
  a differential satisfying the restriction-difference rule:
  for all g, w, a,
    ev(delta^0 g, w, a)
      iff
    exactly one of res_L(g, w)(a) and res_R(g, w)(a) holds.

r in C^1:
  the selected residual (supplied data).

semantic side:
  a semantic atom projection (Lambda, K, pi), a cover datum U,
  and a support assignment supp : C^0 -> P(Lambda).

A primitive \( g \in C^0 \) records a family of chart-local repairs through their readings on overlaps, and \( \delta^0 g \) records how far the global candidate \( g \) disagrees on overlaps. The restriction-difference rule for \( \delta^0 \) is a condition imposed on the supplied data, not a derived fact. The cover datum \( \mathcal{U} \) is selected separately from the complex's charts and overlaps; the residual-atom reading (Definition X.2.2) comes from \( \mathcal{U} \), and the residual cochain r lives on the complex's overlaps.

Definition X.3.2 (Boundary membership and global semantic repair coherence). For a complex \( \mathfrak{K} \), set \( B^1 = \delta^0(C^0) \). The obstruction vanishes when \( r \in B^1 \) and is nonzero when \( r \notin B^1 \). Global semantic repair coherence is: 

GlobalCoherent(K)
  iff
exists g in C^0,
  delta^0 g = r
  and
  supp(g) is semantically closed

That is: a primitive exists that kills the residual as a boundary, and that primitive is not a formal solution but a semantic repair certificate in the sense of Definition X.2.3. In this section the vanishing of the obstruction is boundary membership; the words class and \( H^1 \) as a true quotient are reserved for the construction of Section 4.

Definition X.3.3 (Semantic faithfulness hypothesis). A complex \( \mathfrak{K} \) satisfies the semantic faithfulness hypothesis when, for every \( g \in C^0 \) with \( \delta^0 g = r \), the support \( \mathrm{supp}(g) \) is residual component covered and residual component faithful.

Theorem X.3.4 — Certified bounded inference

Finite Semantic Repair-Gluing Descent

Let \( \mathfrak{K} \) be a finite semantic repair-gluing complex.

(i) necessity (unconditional):
  GlobalCoherent(K)  =>  r in B^1
  contrapositively,
  r not in B^1  =>  not GlobalCoherent(K)

(ii) sufficiency (under the semantic faithfulness hypothesis):
  r in B^1  =>  GlobalCoherent(K)

(iii) descent equivalence (under the semantic faithfulness hypothesis):
  GlobalCoherent(K)  iff  r in B^1
Proof idea
(i) follows immediately because the boundary equation \( \delta^0 g = r \) is part of the definition of global coherence; the substance of the proof lies in the design of the definition. (ii) is the center of gravity: take \( g \) with \( r = \delta^0 g \); the semantic faithfulness hypothesis gives coverage and faithfulness of \( \mathrm{supp}(g) \), and the closure decomposition (Lemma X.2.5) makes \( \mathrm{supp}(g) \) semantically closed, so \( g \) is a semantic repair certificate rather than a formal solution. The hard point is not cohomology algebra; it is the identification of formal solutions with semantic repairs — faithfulness.

Theorem X.3.5 (Complete-support faithfulness). On the same selected semantic atom projection and cover datum as Example X.2.6, consider the family of finite complexes in which every primitive is assigned complete support, \( \mathrm{supp}(g) = \Lambda \). For every complex \( \mathfrak{K} \) in this family, the semantic faithfulness hypothesis holds as a theorem — it need not be supplied as an assumption — and hence, unconditionally inside the family,

\[ \mathrm{GlobalCoherent}(\mathfrak{K}) \iff r \in B^1. \]

The closedness of complete support decomposes by Lemma X.2.5 into coverage and faithfulness, so this is a positive witness that the hypothesis is not vacuous. The substance of the faithfulness condition is carried by the negative witness of Example X.2.6.

Example X.3.6 (Component coverage is not closure). Take a complex with a single chart, a single overlap, and a two-element \( C^1 \); assign the support \( S = \{ t, s \} \) of Example X.2.6 (the atom o is not recorded) and let the residual r charge the atom o. Then 

r not in B^1
not GlobalCoherent(K)

while, simultaneously, every component-level coverage check and every visible local-repair compatibility holds. Component-level passing does not yield boundary membership: the semantic residual obstruction remains inside the fiber of \( \pi \) — in the difference between aliases.

4. Semantic repair additive H¹

Section 3 spoke of boundary membership. This section promotes it to a class in a true quotient.

Definition X.4.1 (Finite repair cover and cover nerve). A finite repair cover consists of: 

cover charts:
  a finite chart family, completely enumerated.

pairwise overlaps:
  overlap components per chart pair, completely enumerated.

triple overlaps:
  triple overlap components per chart triple, completely
  enumerated, each with its three boundary edges
  d01, d12, d02.

This cover determines a cover nerve — the 2-skeleton version of Part IV, Definition 12.1 — whose vertices are the charts, whose edges are the overlap components, and whose faces are the triple overlap components with their three edges.

Definition X.4.2 (Čech-type coefficient data). Čech-type coefficient data over a selected finite repair cover is the tuple: 

C^0, C^1, C^2   (C^0, C^1 carry selected enumerations;
                 completeness is not required)
0_1 in C^1, 0_2 in C^2
delta^0 : C^0 -> C^1
delta^1 : C^1 -> C^2

compatibility:
  delta^1 . delta^0 = 0_2   (on every primitive)
  delta^1(0_1) = 0_2
  the selected residual r in C^1 satisfies
  delta^1(r) = 0_2   (r is a 1-cocycle)

Here \( C^0 \) holds local repair primitives, \( C^1 \) holds residual cochains on overlaps, and \( C^2 \) is the receptacle of coherence on triple overlaps. The identity \( \delta^1 \circ \delta^0 = 0_2 \) is the complex-level expression of the nerve's face relations. The differentials \( \delta^0, \delta^1 \) are not derived from the cover's geometry; they are data supplied alongside the selected cover, and the compatibility conditions above are the whole of their required conformance.

Definition X.4.3 (Additive regime). The Čech-type coefficient data is in the additive regime when \( C^0 \) and \( C^1 \) are commutative groups, \( 0_1 = 0 \), and \( \delta^0 \) is a group homomorphism.

Definition X.4.4 (Semantic repair additive H¹). For Čech-type coefficient data in the additive regime:

\[ Z^1 = \{ c \in C^1 \mid \delta^1 c = 0_2 \}, \qquad B^1 = \delta^0(C^0), \]

\[ c \sim c' \iff c - c' \in B^1, \qquad H^1 = Z^1 / \sim. \]

Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of \( \sim \) are proved from the homomorphism property of \( \delta^0 \), and the coset \( [c] \) is well defined. Writing \( [r] \) for the residual class and \( [0] \) for the zero class, \( H^1 \)-zero is defined as \( [r] = [0] \).

Lemma X.4.5 (Quotient zero reading). \( [r] = [0] \iff r \in B^1 \). The quotient equation and boundary membership coincide.

Definition X.4.6 (Boundary-relation faithfulness data). Supply a closure predicate \( P \subset C^0 \), read as “\( g \) is a semantic repair certificate”; on the complexes of Section 3, \( P(g) \) is the semantic closure of \( \mathrm{supp}(g) \). Global semantic repair coherence becomes: there exists \( g \in C^0 \) with \( \delta^0 g = r \) and \( P(g) \). Boundary-relation faithfulness data is the triple: 

zero primitive:
  z in C^0 with delta^0 z = 0_1

residual support predicate:
  Q subset C^1 with Q(r)

faithfulness law:
  Q(delta^0 g) => P(g)

This form does not demand a global selector assigning a primitive to each cocycle. In the additive regime the zero primitive can be taken as \( z = 0 \); the faithfulness data is fixed in a form that does not assume additivity.

Definition X.4.7 (True sheaf certificate). For an AAT site \( S \) (Part II, Definition 8.1), a presheaf \( F \), and a cover selected separately on \( S \), a true sheaf certificate is the pair: 

cover membership:
  the sieve generated by the selected cover — the family of
  morphisms into the base that factor through some chart of
  the cover — belongs to the topology of S.

global sheaf condition:
  F satisfies the sheaf condition (Part II, Definition 10.1)
  for every cover of the topology of S.

The correspondence between the repair cover (Definition X.4.1) and this cover is delegated to the incidence data of Definition X.6.3.

Theorem X.4.8 — Certified bounded inference

True Sheaf H¹ Semantic Repair-Gluing

Lean: proved

Fix a selected finite repair cover, Čech-type coefficient data on it in the additive regime, boundary-relation faithfulness data, and a true sheaf certificate. Then all of the following hold: 

1. cover sheaf condition and descent:
   F satisfies the sheaf condition for the selected cover, and
   every gluing data on that cover has a unique global section.
   this per-cover condition is not a per-cover assumption; it is
   derived from the global sheaf condition and cover membership.

2. cocycle:
   r and every delta^0-boundary are 1-cocycles.

3. descent equivalence:
   GlobalCoherent  iff  [r] = [0] in H^1 = Z^1 / B^1

4. quotient reading:
   [r] = [0]  iff  r in B^1

5. higher layers:
   the higher conditions corresponding to torsor layers
   (Part IV, Principle 2.3), higher coherence (Part IV,
   Meaning 10.2), and stack-like structure (Part VI) hold
   trivially from the additive-regime construction; they are
   not supplied as external assumptions.
Proof idea
Sufficiency (\( [r] = [0] \Rightarrow \) GlobalCoherent): from the quotient equation obtain \( r \in B^1 \), take a boundary primitive \( g \), and derive \( P(g) \) from \( Q(r) \) and the faithfulness law. Necessity follows from the design of the definition. Conclusions 1 and 3 are parallel conclusions of the same package: 3 is not derived from 1 but from the faithfulness data and the additive regime.

5. Generating the coefficient — law equation realization

The coefficient data of Section 4 was supplied. This section generates the coefficient itself from law equations. Fix a law equation realization (Part III, Definition 11.3): an observable presheaf with restriction-compatible violation coordinates and a chart-local law-defect tie. Then by Part III, Theorem 11.4, the obstruction quotient presheaf

\[ Q = \mathcal{O} / \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}} \]

is generated, and its restriction, vanishing, faithfulness, and restriction evaluator follow as theorems. The coefficient is not a supplied certificate: it is constructed from the vocabulary of witness ideals and proved.

Definition X.5.1 (Law equation grounded surface). A law equation grounded surface is the following selected data: 

finite poset atom/law cover geometry:
  a finite context poset with meets, a finitely indexed
  admissible cover, a finite nerve, and U-adequacy of the cover
  (Part II, Definition 7.2), read in the finite poset AAT site
  regime of Part II, Definition 7.2B.

skeleton:
  a selected support atom and required law per 0-simplex.

defect source:
  per cover index, a displayed local reading (local context,
  architecture object, nonempty required law support, defect
  observable; Part III, Definition 11.3) and a chart-local
  law-defect tie.

incidence bridge:
  a selected repair cover (Definition X.4.1) and provenance data
  (Definition X.6.3) sending its charts / pairwise / triple
  overlaps to the 0 / 1 / 2-simplices of the generated
  Cech complex.

quotient sheaf condition:
  the generated quotient coefficient satisfies the sheaf
  condition for the selected cover.

In general the quotient sheaf condition is an assumption. On a finite model instance with exactly one context, it holds as a theorem and need not be supplied (Example X.9.1).

Meaning X.5.2 (The coefficient is generated). On a law equation grounded surface: 

coefficient:
  constructed as a quotient of the witness ideal.

restriction:
  induced from the ideal's restriction compatibility;
  the functor laws are proved.

interpretation:
  generated as a quotient class of the defect. not free data.

vanishing:
  always read as ideal membership (the no-cancellation
  discipline of Part III, Principle 5.6).

6. The atom-generated site and the Čech bridge

This section connects the repair cover of Section 4 with the AAT site of Part II and the Čech apparatus of Part IV.

Theorem X.6.1 (Coverage generation bridge). In the selected finite poset regime (Part II, Definition 7.2B), the sieve generated by the admissible coverage family is a cover of \( J_U \), and the topology of the AAT site coincides with the topology generated by its coverage requirements. This is a definitional coincidence by generation (Part II, Definition 7.1). The topology \( J_U \), generated from admissible covers requiring atom support coverage, is the ground of all descent and sheaf conditions below.

Definition X.6.2 (Selected simplicial Čech complex). The general form in which the cover-relative Čech complex is read over selected simplicial data: 

base and charts:
  a base context and a family of charts (0-simplices).

selected simplices:
  a family of 1-simplices (each with source / target faces),
  a family of 2-simplices (each with three boundary edges
  d01, d12, d02). the simplices of each degree are
  selected data.

cochains and differential:
  C^n is the set of coefficient-valued functions on degree-n
  simplices; the differential is the alternating sum of
  restrictions to faces.

The covers of Part IV, Definitions 3.1–3.3 are the special case in which simplices are the chart-index tuples themselves and overlaps are read as pullbacks. From here on, the Čech-side objects of this chapter are read in this selected simplicial form.

Definition X.6.3 (Repair cover incidence bridge). The bridge from the finite repair cover of Section 4 to a selected simplicial Čech complex (Definition X.6.2) is given as provenance data: 

chart            -> 0-simplex
pairwise overlap -> 1-simplex
triple overlap   -> 2-simplex

Theorem X.6.4 (Incidence is data). This incidence data cannot be constructed uniformly from the simplicial data alone: a selected simplicial cover whose family of 0-simplices is nonempty while its chart index set is empty gives a counterexample. Conversely, from a chart-indexed cover whose 0-simplices are the chart indices themselves, the required incidence is constructed by definition. The correspondence is neither an assumption nor an omission — it is data.

Theorem X.6.5 (Cover nerve provenance). The edges and faces of the Section 4 cover nerve are the selected pairwise and triple overlap components themselves, and each face's boundary edges agree typedly. The nerve is a re-description of the overlap data, not a separate object.

Theorem X.6.6 (Descent is derived). If a presheaf \( F \) satisfies the AAT sheaf condition and the sieve of the selected cover belongs to the topology, then the sheaf condition for that cover, descent, and the unique existence of a global section for any gluing data follow as theorems. From the two inputs — sheaf condition and cover membership — descent is derived. No independent descent certificate is demanded.

7. The SAGA comparison theorem

Definition X.7.1 (H¹ comparison data). Between semantic-side Čech-type coefficient data (Section 4, additive regime) and a cover-relative Čech complex (in the selected simplicial form of Definition X.6.2), \( H^1 \) comparison data is a pair of maps in each degree 0, 1, 2 satisfying: 

degree 1:
  the two maps are mutually inverse and preserve differences.

degree 2:
  zero is preserved.

compatibility:
  the maps commute, in both directions, with the differentials
  in degrees 0 -> 1 and 1 -> 2.

Comparison data stores no quotient equation, no zero class, no global coherence, and no descent conclusion.

Theorem X.7.2 — Certified bounded inference

SAGA Comparison Theorem

Lean: proved

For any semantic coefficient data in the additive regime, any cover-relative Čech complex, and any \( H^1 \) comparison data between them, all of the following hold: 

1. cocycle correspondence:
   the comparison data carries semantic 1-cocycles and
   degree-1 Cech cocycles to each other, in both directions.

2. coboundary correspondence:
   two semantic cocycles share a class iff their images are
   coboundary-related on the Cech side.

3. H^1 comparison equivalence:
   the induced maps on quotients are well defined and mutually
   inverse:

   semantic repair additive H^1  ~=  H^1(U, coefficient)

   under the selected comparison.

4. zero-predicate equivalence:
   the semantic residual class is zero iff the class of the
   residual's image on the Cech side is zero.
Statement in one line
\[ H^1_{\mathrm{sem}} \;\cong\; \check{H}^1(\mathcal{U}, Q) \quad \text{under the selected comparison data,} \] where the left side is the semantic repair additive \( H^1 \) of Definition X.4.4 and the right side is the cover-relative Čech \( H^1 \) on the AAT site, with coefficient generated by law equations when read over a law equation grounded surface (Section 5).
Assumptions
The additive regime on the semantic side (Definition X.4.3) and supplied \( H^1 \) comparison data (Definition X.7.1). No finiteness assumption is needed for this general form: finiteness belongs to the grounded surface (Definition X.5.1) and to the instances (Section 9).
Proof idea
The well-definedness of the induced quotient maps and their two-sided inverseness are not components of the supplied data; they are theorems proved at quotient level from the degree-1 inverse pair, difference preservation, degree-2 zero preservation, and differential compatibility.

8. Boundary theorems — where the law contributes

Where does law satisfaction act inside this cohomology? The location is fixed as a theorem, not excused as a caveat.

Theorem X.8.1 (Degree-zero law contribution) — Certified bounded inference. On a law equation grounded surface, if the displayed required laws are satisfied, the generated 0-cochain is pointwise zero. The point at which law semantics reaches the Čech complex is exactly cochain degree 0, and the Čech-zero of Theorem X.7.5 is the image of that vanishing under the differential.

Theorem X.8.2 (Law-independence of the grounded route). The law-independent conclusion group of Theorem X.7.5 is proved without assuming law satisfaction. On the generated input surface, the residual is constructed as the boundary of a generated primitive, \( \text{residual} = \delta^0(\text{primitive}) \): a construction-level fact, not to be read as descent content grounded in laws.

Meaning X.8.3 (Where law semantics lives). Theorems X.8.1 and X.8.2 pin the location of the law's contribution from above and below: 

law satisfaction acts at H^0: vanishing.
nontrivial H^1 content comes from the geometry of the cover.

With the current generated coefficient, a lawful reading is zero in the quotient. How to read a coefficient in which distinct nonzero lawful readings glue nontrivially — the conormal coefficient \( I_U / I_U^2 \) of Part IV, Definition 2.4 is a natural candidate — is the remaining question of the text.

Theorem X.8.4 (Full sheaf comparison is data). The comparison from cover-relative Čech \( H^1 \) to sheaf cohomology holds only when explicit comparison data is given. There is no unconditional comparison: there exists a finite complex with nonzero cover-relative \( H^1 \) paired with a coefficient selection having no comparison target, on which no comparison map can be constructed. Comparability is relativized to the very existence of explicit comparison data.

Theorem X.8.5 (Refinement naturality is data). Naturality of \( H^1 \) along cover refinement holds only under explicit refinement comparison data (Proposition X.4.10). The unconditional version fails: there is a finite example in which the coarse-side residual is the zero cochain while the fine-side residual is not a coboundary, and on it no residual- and zero-preserving pullback exists. Comparability is data — this is the outer boundary of the SAGA comparison theorem.
