Part III

Law Algebra, Obstruction Ideal, and the Lawful Locus

Part II produced the AAT site and its sheaf category. Part III makes it algebraic geometry: a sheaf of commutative rings \(\mathcal{O}_X^U\) is placed on the architecture geometry, law failure is collected into an obstruction ideal sheaf \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U\), and the lawful locus is defined as its zero set \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U)\). Law is read as equation, obstruction as ideal, and lawfulness as vanishing.

Research question

Can architectural laws be read as equations on a geometric object, so that lawfulness becomes the vanishing of an ideal rather than a checklist of natural-language principles?

Part II built the architecture geometry as a site with a sheaf category: 

ArchitectureObject A_S^V
  -> ArchitectureGeometry X_S^(V,U,J)
  -> AATSite(S,V,U,J)
  -> AATSh(A_S^V,U,J)

A site and a sheaf category are a stage for local data and gluing, but they are not yet algebraic geometry. The theory becomes algebraic geometry when the geometric object carries a sheaf of commutative rings. Part III supplies that ring: it constructs the law algebra sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_X^U\), collects law failure into the obstruction ideal sheaf \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\), and cuts out the lawful locus as the zero set of that ideal. The central diagram of Part III is 

AATSh(A,U,J)
  -> O_X^U
  -> I_Ob^U subset O_X^U
  -> Flat_U(X) = V(I_Ob^U)
  -> affine AAT charts
  -> ArchitectureScheme

Under this structure, a law is not a design slogan. It is an equation, or a family of equations, on the architecture geometry.

From Part II to Part III

The sheaf category obtained in Part II is \(\mathrm{AATSh}(A,U,J) = \mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{ArchCtx}(A), J_U)\). On this stage, each context \(W\) is first assigned a commutative ring of architecture coordinates readable on \(W\):

\[ W \;\longmapsto\; \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W). \]

A context morphism \(i : W' \to W\) induces a restriction homomorphism of commutative rings \(\mathrm{res}_i : \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W) \to \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W')\). Sheafifying this presheaf yields the sheaf of commutative rings on the AAT site:

\[ \mathcal{O}_X^U \;=\; \bigl(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U\bigr)^{+}. \]

The pair \(\bigl(\mathrm{AATSh}(A,U,J),\, \mathcal{O}_X^U\bigr)\) is the ringed AAT topos of Definition III.9.1 below. Everything in Part III — witness ideals, obstruction ideals, affine charts, the lawful locus, and the architecture scheme — lives on this ringed foundation.

Structure sheaf of architecture geometry

Definition III.2.1 (Law Algebra Sheaf). For an architecture geometry \(X = X_S^{V,U,J,k}\), the law algebra sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_X^U\) is the sheaf of commutative rings on the AAT site obtained by sheafifying the raw ambient presheaf: \(\mathcal{O}_X^U \in \mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{ArchCtx}(A), J_U;\ \mathrm{CommAlg}_k)\). For each context \(W\), the section ring \(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) is the commutative ring generated by the architecture coordinates readable on \(W\).

This ring is the function algebra for reading Atoms, signatures, state, effects, semantic facts, and runtime interactions as coordinates. The division of labor is exact: 

Atom is ontology.
Coordinate is a reading of Atom.
Ring is algebra of such readings.

Principle III.2.2 (Commutative Base). The base ring that cuts out the lawful locus is commutative. A coefficient ring \(k \in \mathrm{CommRing}\) is fixed, and each coordinate algebra is read as a commutative \(k\)-algebra: \(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W) \in \mathrm{CommAlg}_k\). Changing \(k\) changes the readings of cancellation, rank, dimension, and derived tensor.

State transition, effect composition, runtime interaction, and operation algebras may be noncommutative. The lawful locus, however, is defined as a zero set, and the coordinate ring that carries zero sets is taken commutative first. Noncommutative structure lives over this base: 

noncommutative operation
  may exist over
commutative law-coordinate geometry.

Noncommutative operations are read as modules, algebra objects, or operation sheaves over the commutative structure sheaf, or as later extensions.

Architectural coordinates

Definition III.3.1 (Coordinate). An architecture coordinate on a context \(W\) is a function \(c : \text{local architecture data on } W \to R\) that reads an Atom configuration, or a derived structure, as a value in a selected coefficient ring \(R\).

Typical coordinate families are atom, signature, law witness, state, effect, authority, semantic, runtime, and boundary coordinates. A coordinate reads structures that were already constructed from the Atom axioms of Part I; it does not create them.

Example III.3.2 (Atom Coordinate). An atom coordinate \(x_a(W)\) reads whether, or with which weight, an Atom \(a\) appears on \(W\). Instances include \(x_{\mathrm{component}}(C)\), \(x_{\mathrm{relation}}(A,B)\), \(x_{\mathrm{capability}}(C,\kappa)\), \(x_{\mathrm{effect}}(e)\), \(x_{\mathrm{authority}}(\mathrm{actor},\mathrm{action},\mathrm{resource})\), \(x_{\mathrm{semantic}}(t,s)\), and \(x_{\mathrm{runtime}}(u,v,h)\).

Example III.3.3 (Signature Coordinate). A signature coordinate reads the value of a selected signature axis: \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{cycle}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{projection}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{substitution}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{state\ transition}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{effect}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{authority}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{semantic}}\), \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{runtime}}\), and \(\mathrm{sig}_{\mathrm{boundary}}\). A vanishing signature coordinate is a zero condition on the selected axis, and only on it: 

selected zero != total zero
unselected axis != zero
unmeasured axis != zero

Example III.3.4 (Semantic Coordinate). Following the Semantic Atom of Part I, a semantic coordinate \(\mathrm{sem}_{\Gamma,R,\rho}(t,s)\) is relativized to a UseContext \(\Gamma\), a UseRuleSet \(R\), and a resolution \(\rho\). Agreement of semantic coordinates means agreement of use-rules inside a fixed language game, not agreement of strings or names.

Ambient law algebra

Definition III.4.1 (Free Typed Commutative Algebra). Write \(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W)\) for the set of architecture coordinates visible on \(W\). The free typed commutative algebra over the coefficient ring \(k\) is \(\mathrm{FreeCommAlg}_k(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W))\) — intuitively, the polynomial ring \(k[x_{\mathrm{atom}}, x_{\mathrm{signature}}, x_{\mathrm{state}}, x_{\mathrm{effect}}, x_{\mathrm{authority}}, x_{\mathrm{semantic}}, x_{\mathrm{runtime}}, x_{\mathrm{boundary}}]\) in the coordinates readable on \(W\).

Definition III.4.2 (Structural Relation). Independently of laws, coordinates carry structural relations \(\mathrm{Rel}_{\mathrm{struct}}(W)\): typing relations, source identity consistency, projection and restriction compatibility, boundary incidence, semantic resolution compatibility, state coordinate typing, and runtime trace typing. Structural relations are not there to erase law failure; they make the coordinates cohere as readings of one architecture object. The basic object is the ideal they generate, \(J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W) \subset \mathrm{FreeCommAlg}_k(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W))\), and the text distinguishes the relation polynomials \(\mathrm{Rel}_{\mathrm{struct}}(W)\) from the generated ideal \(J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W)\).

Definition III.4.3 (Raw Ambient Law Algebra). The raw ambient law algebra on \(W\) is the quotient

\[ \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W) \;=\; \mathrm{FreeCommAlg}_k(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W)) \,/\, J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W). \]

The superscript \(U\) records relativization to the law universe: it selects the coordinates, witnesses, signature axes, and coverage reading. Crucially, selected law witness ideals are not set to zero at the ambient stage. If every law were quotiented out from the start, the obstruction ideal generated by law failure would become invisible. Part III therefore separates two roles: 

structural relations:
  coordinate system itself must satisfy them.

law witness ideals:
  they cut out lawful locus as zeros.

The structure sheaf is the sheafification \(\mathcal{O}_X^U = (\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U)^{+}\).

Condition III.4.4 (Restriction-Stable Structural Relations). For \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U\) to be a presheaf of commutative \(k\)-algebras, the structural relation ideal must be stable under context restriction: for each \(i : W' \to W\), the typed coordinate restriction induces \(\mathrm{res}_i : \mathrm{FreeCommAlg}_k(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W)) \to \mathrm{FreeCommAlg}_k(\mathrm{Coord}_X(W'))\) with \(\mathrm{res}_i\bigl(J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W)\bigr) \subset J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W')\). Under this restriction-stability, \(\mathrm{res}_i\) descends to the quotients \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W) \to \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W')\).

Condition III.4.5 (Presentation-Stable AAT Site). To use the sheafified sections \(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) as the coordinate algebra of the same local configuration functor, the selected finite presentation must survive sheafification: if the canonical map \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W) \to \mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) preserves the selected coordinate generators, structural relations, and local functor representation, the AAT site is called presentation-stable. Without this condition \(\mathcal{O}_X^U\) is still a sheaf of commutative rings, but reading \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W), D_W^U)\) as the affine moduli chart of the raw local configuration functor requires presentation-stability to be stated explicitly.

Principle III.4.6 (Law Does Not Create Coordinates). The law universe \(U\) selects which witness ideals and defect representatives are read, which signature axes are required, and which witness families are chosen. It is not the ground of existence of Atoms or coordinates: 

law does not create atoms
law does not create coordinates
law cuts out loci

Law as equation

Definition III.5.1 (Violation Witness Family). For a law \(L\) and a context \(W\), the selected family of violation witnesses is \(\mathrm{Viol}_L(W)\): the selected coordinate family witnessing failure of \(L\). A cycle law contributes cycle witnesses, an authority law contributes authority/effect mismatch witnesses, a semantic law contributes use-rule mismatch witnesses. Each witness \(v \in \mathrm{Viol}_L(W)\) has a coordinate \(x_v \in \mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\).

Definition III.5.2 (Law Witness Ideal). The local law witness ideal of \(L\) is

\[ \mathcal{I}_L(W) \;=\; \bigl\langle\, x_v \;\big|\; v \in \mathrm{Viol}_L(W) \,\bigr\rangle \;\subset\; \mathcal{O}_X^U(W). \]

\(\mathcal{I}_L(W)\) collects the equations that kill the selected violation witnesses. Inside the ambient ring \(\mathcal{I}_L(W)\) need not be the zero ideal; lawfulness is read as the vanishing of this ideal along a point, a section, or a quotient. This ideal-theoretic encoding is the body of law failure.

Definition III.5.3 (Defect Section). For a law \(L\) and a context \(W\), a defect section \(\delta_L(W) \in \mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) is a coordinate function or witness-ideal representative measuring how far \(L\) fails on \(W\). The law \(L\) holds along a point \(p \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W), D_W^U)\) or a section \(s\) when \(\mathcal{I}_L(W) \subset p\) or \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_L = 0\). In coefficient systems with a no-cancellation discipline — Boolean coordinates or independently readable witness coordinates — the representative reading \(L \text{ holds on } s \iff s^{*}(\delta_L) = 0\) is available. In general, cancellation from characteristic or signed coefficients is possible (Principle III.5.6), so the vanishing of \(\mathcal{I}_L(W)\) is the primary encoding. A law is thereby read as a family of equations, or as an ideal, on the architecture geometry.

Example III.5.4 (Representative Defects). Representative defect sections are \(\delta_{\mathrm{cycle}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{projection}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{substitution}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{state\ transition}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{effect\ replay}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{authority}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{semantic}}\), \(\delta_{\mathrm{runtime}}\), and \(\delta_{\mathrm{boundary}}\). 

depends(A,B)
depends(B,C)
depends(C,A)
----------------
delta_cycle != 0

contract(Base, returns NonNull)
contract(Impl, returns Nullable)
substitutes(Impl, Base)
----------------
delta_substitution != 0

same name
different use-rule
----------------
delta_semantic != 0

The semantic defect is read against a fixed language game: it witnesses a mismatch of use-rules, not a mismatch of spelling.

Example III.5.5 (Defect Encoding). With a fixed coefficient ring \(k\) and selected witness family, the safe encoding of the cycle defect is the ideal generated by the selected cycle witness coordinates:

\[ \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{cycle}}(W) \;=\; \bigl\langle\, x_c \;\big|\; c \in \mathrm{CycleWitness}_U(W) \,\bigr\rangle \;\subset\; \mathcal{O}_X^U(W). \]

Then \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{cycle}}(W) = 0\), or \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{cycle}}(W) \subset p\), reads that every selected cycle witness vanishes along the section or point. Only under a fixed no-cancellation discipline may the representative \(\delta_{\mathrm{cycle}}(W) = \sum_{c \in \mathrm{CycleWitness}_U(W)} x_c\) be used, because the sum of witnesses may cancel in positive characteristic or with signed coefficients; lawfulness is therefore defined by \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{cycle}} = 0\) or \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{cycle}} \subset p\), not by \(\delta_{\mathrm{cycle}} = 0\). The substitution defect is encoded the same way, as \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{substitution}}(W) = \langle\, x_m \mid m \in \mathrm{SubstitutionMismatch}_U(W) \,\rangle\). A defect section is not the law predicate itself; it is the selected witnesses encoded as ring elements or ideals.

Principle III.5.6 (No-Cancellation Discipline). Reading witness vanishing in a coordinate algebra requires a no-cancellation discipline on the coefficient system and the encoding. The safe basic forms are Boolean evaluation \(\mathrm{ev}_s(x_v) \in \lbrace 0,1 \rbrace\), monomial witness ideals \(\mathcal{I}_L(W) = \langle\, x_v \mid v \in \mathrm{Viol}_L(W) \,\rangle\), and product witnesses such as \(c_{ABC} = e_{AB}\, e_{BC}\, e_{CA}\). For example: 

I_cycle = < c_ABC >
I_auth  = < effect_capture * (1 - has_authority) >
I_sub   = < substitutes_Impl_Base * contract_mismatch >

Under this discipline, a zero witness ideal keeps the meaning "the selected violation witnesses vanished" and additive cancellation is never misread as lawfulness.

Principle III.5.7 (Equation Relativity). A law equation is relativized to the Atom vocabulary \(V\), the law universe \(U\), the coverage topology \(J\), the selected witness family, the selected signature axes, the UseContext \(\Gamma\), the UseRuleSet \(R\), and the resolution \(\rho\). Consequently \(\delta_L = 0\) within the selected reading asserts nothing about readings that were not selected.

Idempotent collapse and the Stanley-Reisner regime

Two structural results make the Boolean-flavored witness algebra fully computable: idempotent coordinates collapse to a product of copies of \(k\), and square-free witness regimes identify the obstruction ideal with a Stanley-Reisner ideal.

Lemma III.5.6A

Idempotent Coordinate Collapse

Lean: proved
Statement
For finitely many selected coordinates over a field \(k\), let \(A = k[x_v \mid v \in E] \,/\, \langle x_v^2 - x_v \mid v \in E \rangle\). Then \(A\) is a finite product of copies of \(k\), and \(\mathrm{Tor}_i^A(M,N) = 0\) for \(i > 0\), \(I/I^2 = 0\) for every ideal \(I \subset A\), and \(\Omega_{A/k} = 0\).

Definition III.5.6B (Square-Free Witness Regime). On a context \(W\), fix a finite set of selected primitive witness coordinates \(E_W = \lbrace e_1, \dots, e_n \rbrace\) and keep the deformation side of the coordinate algebra as \(k[e_1, \dots, e_n]\). The context \(W\) is in a square-free witness regime when the law universe \(U\) selects a family of forbidden witness supports \(\mathrm{Forb}_U(W) \subset \mathrm{FinSubsets}(E_W)\) and assigns to each forbidden support \(S\) the square-free monomial \(x_S = \prod_{e \in S} e\). The obstruction ideal is then

\[ \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) \;=\; \bigl\langle\, x_S \;\big|\; S \in \mathrm{Forb}_U(W) \,\bigr\rangle \;\subset\; k[E_W]. \]
Theorem III.5.6C

Stanley-Reisner Obstruction Theorem

Lean: defined / proved
Statement
If \(W\) is in a square-free witness regime and \(\mathrm{Forb}_U(W)\) is a family of inclusion-minimal forbidden supports, the simplicial complex \(\Delta_U(W) = \lbrace T \subset E_W \mid \text{no } S \in \mathrm{Forb}_U(W) \text{ satisfies } S \subset T \rbrace\) is defined, and \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) = I_{\Delta_U(W)}\): the obstruction ideal is the Stanley-Reisner ideal of \(\Delta_U(W)\). The lawful chart \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(W) = V(I_{\Delta_U(W)})\) is read as a coordinate subspace arrangement whose subspaces avoid the forbidden supports. If \(\mathrm{Forb}_U(W)\) is not minimal, reducing to inclusion-minimal supports yields the same radical obstruction ideal.

Corollary III.5.6D (Obstruction Invariants). In a square-free witness regime the following are read as obstruction invariants: minimal forbidden supports are minimal law obstruction patterns; faces of \(\Delta_U(W)\) are simultaneously admissible witness supports; links and restrictions of \(\Delta_U(W)\) are context refinements and boundary-local readings; and the minimal monomial generators of \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W)\) are the irreducible selected obstruction witnesses.

Example III.5.6E (Three-Witness Stanley-Reisner Chart). Let \(E_W = \lbrace x, y, z \rbrace\) and \(\mathrm{Forb}_U(W) = \lbrace \lbrace x,y \rbrace \rbrace\). Then \(\Delta_U(W) = \lbrace T \subset \lbrace x,y,z \rbrace \mid \lbrace x,y \rbrace \not\subset T \rbrace\) and \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) = \langle x y \rangle \subset k[x,y,z]\). Similarly, \(\mathrm{Forb}_V(W) = \lbrace \lbrace x,z \rbrace \rbrace\) gives \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^V(W) = \langle x z \rangle\). This chart is reused in the monomial law-conflict computations of Part V. In Lean, the chart is fixed as FiniteExamples.StanleyReisnerChart with obstructionIdeal_eq_span_xy and the face and non-face witnesses proved.

Law condition types

Principle III.5.8 (Law Condition Types). The general form of a law condition is a subfunctor of the functor of points \(h_X : \mathrm{CommAlg}_k \to \mathrm{Set}\) of the architecture scheme, \(L \subset h_X\); when isomorphism data must be retained — decomposition, refactor equivalence, semantic identification — the law condition is read as a substack \(L \subset \mathcal{X}\). The core of Part III reads closed equational laws as zero sets of ideals, and only those. The typology is:

Closed equational law
Read as the vanishing of a selected witness ideal \(\mathcal{I}_L\).
Open / inequational law
Nonzero capability, enabled provider, available path: a \(D(f)\)-type open condition.
Constructible law
A Boolean combination of closed and open conditions.
Descent law
Local lawful sections must glue along a cover with gluing data.
Temporal / trace law
Relativized to runtime trace sheaves, state transitions, automata, and temporal predicates.
Higher / stacky law
Decomposition, semantic identification, refactor equivalence: groupoid- or stack-valued data.

The slogan of the canonical text is read accordingly:

closed law is equation.
general law is a geometric condition.
obstruction ideal is the closed defect envelope
of selected witnesses.

\(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\) collects the part of the selected witness family that is readable as a closed equational envelope. Open, constructible, descent, temporal, and stacky laws are handled as open subobjects, locally closed subobjects, descent conditions, trace conditions, or stack conditions once the required extra structure is fixed. This classification is a claim boundary: it fixes exactly where closed defects may be treated as ideals and refuses to force other law types into ideals unnaturally. How the closed equational vocabulary generates the coefficients and restrictions of obstruction is fixed as a theorem in the Law Equation Realization below.

Obstruction ideal sheaf

Definition III.6.1 (Local Obstruction Ideal). For a context \(W\), let \(\mathrm{Def}_U(W) = \lbrace \mathcal{I}_L(W) \mid L \in U \text{ and } L \text{ is selected on } W \rbrace\) be the law witness ideals selected by the law universe. The local obstruction ideal is their sum:

\[ \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) \;=\; \sum_{L \,\in\, U \text{ selected on } W} \mathcal{I}_L(W) \;\subset\; \mathcal{O}_X^U(W). \]

Definition III.6.2 (Obstruction Ideal Sheaf). When \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\) is compatible with restriction and satisfies the sheaf condition on covers, it is called the obstruction ideal sheaf: \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U\), an ideal sheaf of the structure sheaf, with \(\mathrm{res}_i\bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W)\bigr) \subset \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W')\) for each context morphism \(i : W' \to W\). Law failure thus restricts coherently across local contexts. When an unconditional construction is preferred, one defines \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\) as the sheaf image of the map \(\bigoplus_L \mathcal{I}_L \to \mathcal{O}_X^U\) from the direct sum of selected local witness ideals; then \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U\) is an ideal sheaf by definition, and restriction compatibility is the functoriality of the sheaf image. The local-sum notation is used exactly where it agrees with this sheaf-image construction.

Principle III.6.3 (Obstruction Is Ideal-Theoretic). An obstruction is not a failure label. The algebro-geometric condition of lawfulness is that \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\) is zero, or pulls back to zero along a section.

Lawful locus

Definition III.7.1 (Lawful Locus). For the architecture geometry \(X\) and the obstruction ideal sheaf \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\), the lawful locus is the zero set

\[ \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \;=\; V\bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\bigr), \]

so that \(x \in \mathrm{Flat}_U(X)\) exactly when all selected law witness ideals vanish at \(x\); that is, \(\delta(x) = 0\) for all \(\delta \in \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\).

Definition III.7.2 (Lawful Section). An architecture section \(s : T \to X\) is lawful for \(U\) when it factors through the lawful locus; equivalently, when \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0\):

\[ \mathrm{Lawful}_U(s) \;\iff\; s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0. \]

Lean status LawfulLocus.lawfulLocus as PrimeSpectrum.zeroLocus, LawfulSectionData.lawful_iff_pulledObstructionIdeal_eq_bot, lawful_iff_factorsThroughLawfulLocus, and the one-directional prime-map theorems sectionPrimeMap_mem_lawfulLocus_of_lawful in Formal/AG/LawAlgebra/LawfulLocus.lean — defined, with a proved bridge. The converse from set-theoretic image containment to ideal vanishing is not claimed, because of the radical gap.

Principle III.7.3 (Lawful Locus Is Not Total Correctness). \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)\) is a zero set relativized to the selected law universe \(U\), coverage topology \(J\), witness family, and signature axes: \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)\) is lawful within \(U\). The flatness here is AAT-flatness — factorization through the lawful locus — and is distinct from the flatness of morphisms in ordinary algebraic geometry. It is not total correctness over all possible law universes. AAT defines the lawfulness that can be spoken inside a fixed geometry.

Architecture Nullstellensatz and certificate depth

Theorem candidate III.7.2A

Architecture Nullstellensatz

Lean: statement only
Statement
Fix the affine chart \(X_W = \mathrm{Spec}\, k[E_W]\) with \(k\) algebraically closed. Let \(E_{\mathrm{bool}} \subset E_W\) carry Boolean evaluation and set \(B_W = \langle e^2 - e \mid e \in E_{\mathrm{bool}} \rangle\), and let \(I_U(W) = \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) \subset k[E_W]\) be the closed equational obstruction ideal. Emptiness of the selected Boolean affine lawful points is \(V(I_U(W) + B_W)(k) = \varnothing\), which by the Hilbert Nullstellensatz is equivalent to \(1 \in \sqrt{I_U(W) + B_W}\). If \(I_U(W) + B_W\) is radical, this is equivalent to \(1 \in I_U(W) + B_W\), and a presentation \(1 = \sum_i a_i f_i\) over the chosen generators is an unlawfulness certificate, of minimal display degree \(\mathrm{NSdeg}_U(W)\).

Definition III.7.2B (Nullstellensatz Depth). For the chosen generators \(F_U(W) = \mathrm{generators}(I_U(W)) \cup \mathrm{generators}(B_W)\) and a presentation \(1 = \sum_{f \in F_U(W)} a_f f\), define the display degree \(\deg_F = \max_f \deg(a_f f)\). When \(V(I_U(W) + B_W)(k) = \varnothing\), the Nullstellensatz depth is the minimum over all such presentations:

\[ \mathrm{NSdepth}_U(W) \;:=\; \min \deg_F\Bigl(1 = \textstyle\sum a_f f\Bigr). \]

Proposition III.7.2C (Nullstellensatz Depth Monotonicity). Fix the chosen generators and suppose \(U \subset U'\) with \(I_U(W) \subset I_{U'}(W)\), where \(F_{U'}(W)\) is obtained from \(F_U(W)\) by adding generators. If both selected Boolean lawful point sets are empty, then \(\mathrm{NSdepth}_{U'}(W) \le \mathrm{NSdepth}_U(W)\): enlarging the law universe can only shorten the shortest unlawfulness certificate.

Example III.7.2D (Unit Certificate). If \(I_U(W) = \langle x \rangle\) and \(B_W = \langle x - 1 \rangle\), then \(1 = x - (x - 1)\) and \(\mathrm{NSdepth}_U(W) = 1\). In Lean the concrete certificate is fixed by NSdepthExample.one_eq_x_sub_x_minus_one and NSdepthExample.nsdepth_eq_one, as proved examples.

Affine AAT charts

Definition III.8.1 (Affine AAT Chart). For a context \(W\), the affine AAT chart is

\[ \mathrm{AffAAT}(W,U) \;=\; \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}\bigl(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W),\, D_W^U\bigr), \]

the spectrum of the architecture coordinate ring on \(W\), where \(D_W^U\) is the decoration: typed coordinate labels, selected law readings, signature axis readings, the obstruction ideal, and AAT interpretation maps. \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}\) is not a new spectrum. It is the ordinary affine scheme \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) with decoration attached: \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(A,D) := (\mathrm{Spec}\,A,\ \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Spec}\,A},\ D)\), using the ordinary topological space, prime ideals, and structure sheaf.

Definition III.8.2 (R-Valued Local Configuration Functor). For a context \(W\) and law universe \(U\), the local architecture configuration functor is \(h_W^U : \mathrm{CommAlg}_k \to \mathrm{Set}\), where \(h_W^U(R)\) is the set of \(R\)-valued \(W\)-local architecture configurations: assignments of \(R\)-values to the typed coordinates readable on \(W\) that satisfy the structural relations. At this stage the selected law witness ideals are not required to vanish; the lawful locus is cut out afterwards by \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\).

Raw Affine Chart Representability

Suppose \(W\) has finitely presented coordinate and structural relation families and restriction preserves typed coordinates and structural relations. Hence \(\mathrm{AffAAT}_{\mathrm{raw}}(W,U) = \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(A_{\mathrm{raw},W}^U, D_{\mathrm{raw},W}^U)\) is the affine scheme representing the moduli functor of typed local architecture configurations, carrying AAT labels and the selected reading.

Read off the universal property of the presentation: the free commutative algebra quotiented by the structural relation ideal. A configuration is exactly an algebra map killing \(J_{\mathrm{struct}}(W)\).

Assumption III.8.4 (Sheafified Chart Presentation). To use the sheafified section ring \(A_W^U := \mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) as the coordinate algebra of the same local configuration functor, the presentation-stability of Condition III.4.5 is fixed: the canonical map \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{raw}}^U(W) \to \mathcal{O}_X^U(W)\) preserves the selected finite presentation for \(h_W^U\), or induces an isomorphism on the represented local functor. Only under this assumption does one read \(h_W^U(R) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{k\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}}(A_W^U, R)\) and call \(\mathrm{AffAAT}(W,U) = \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(A_W^U, D_W^U)\) the sheafified affine AAT chart. Raw representability and the condition under which the sheafified chart preserves it are read separately.

Definition III.8.5 (Local Lawful Chart). The local lawful chart on \(W\) is

\[ \mathrm{Flat}_U(W) \;=\; V\bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W)\bigr) \;=\; \bigl\lbrace\, p \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W), D_W^U) \;\big|\; \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W) \subset p \,\bigr\rbrace, \]

the locus on the affine chart where every selected law witness ideal vanishes.

Example III.8.6 (Boundary Chart). On a boundary context \(W_{\mathrm{boundary}}\) the coordinates \(x_{\mathrm{core}}, x_{\mathrm{feature}}, x_{\mathrm{overlap}}, x_{\mathrm{authority}}, x_{\mathrm{effect}}, x_{\mathrm{semantic}}, x_{\mathrm{runtime}}\) appear. If \(U\) requires boundary consistency, authority/effect compatibility, and semantic/runtime compatibility, the local obstruction ideal takes the shape \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W_{\mathrm{boundary}}) = \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{boundary}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{authority}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{effect}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{semantic\ runtime}}\), and the local lawful chart is \(V(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{boundary}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{authority}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{effect}} + \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{semantic\ runtime}})\).

Ringed AAT topos and architecture scheme

Definition III.9.1 (Ringed AAT Topos). The sheaf-theoretic foundation of the architecture scheme is the ringed AAT topos \(T_X^U = \bigl(\mathrm{AATSh}(A,U,J),\ \mathcal{O}_X^U\bigr)\), with \(\mathrm{AATSh}(A,U,J) = \mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{ArchCtx}(A), J_U)\). It is Part II's site-and-sheaf structure equipped with Part III's sheaf of commutative rings, and it is the base floor on which affine charts, the lawful locus, and derived intersections are placed.

Definition III.9.2 (Chart Compatibility). A family of affine AAT charts \(\mathrm{AffAAT}(W_i,U) = \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W_i), D_{W_i}^U)\) is chart-compatible when at least the following hold:

Open immersion
Chart transition maps are open immersions of the underlying affine spectra.
Overlap representability
Overlaps are represented by affine AAT charts, typically localizations or the chart of \(W_i \times_W W_j\).
Restriction compatibility
\(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W_i) \to \mathcal{O}_X^U(W_i \times_W W_j)\) agrees with the restriction of coordinate rings.
Ideal compatibility
\(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\) restricts to the obstruction ideal on overlaps.
Decoration compatibility
\(D_{W_i}^U\) restricts to the selected AAT decoration on overlaps, including Atom labels, law readings, signature axes, obstruction ideals, and interpretation maps.
Cocycle condition
Triple-overlap chart transitions agree.
Locally ringed condition
After forgetting AAT labels, the resulting structure sheaf is locally ringed.

Definition III.9.3 (Architecture Scheme). An architecture scheme is a decorated locally ringed architecture geometry locally presented by chart-compatible affine AAT charts:

\[ X = \bigl(\,|X|,\ \mathcal{O}_X^U,\ D_X\,\bigr), \qquad D_X = \bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U,\ \mathrm{At}_X,\ \mathrm{Sig}_U,\ \mathrm{LawRead}_U,\ \mathrm{Flat}_U,\ \lambda_X\bigr). \]

Each point \(x \in |X|\) has a context \(W\) and a neighborhood \(N_x \cong \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W), D_W^U)\), with chart overlaps satisfying Definition III.9.2. Under the forgetful reading that drops the AAT decoration, an ordinary locally ringed space with a scheme atlas remains. The underlying space \(|X|\) is not the space of the source itself; it is the underlying space of the architecture geometry relativized to the Atom vocabulary, law universe, and coverage topology. ArchitectureScheme is the strong object: decorated \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}\) charts glued by open immersions and the cocycle condition on the scheme side, preserving Atom labels, law readings, obstruction ideals, and signature axis readings on the decoration side.

Lean status Scheme.RingedAATTopos, RingedAATTopos.forgetfulLocallyRingedSpace, Scheme.ChartCompatibility, Scheme.ArchitectureScheme with ArchitectureScheme.singleAffineSpec and its compatibility theorems in Formal/AG/LawAlgebra/Scheme.lean — defined, with the forgetful bridge and single-affine compatibility proved.

Principle III.9.4 (Scheme Relativity). An architecture scheme is relativized to the source \(S\), the Atom vocabulary \(V\), the law universe \(U\), the coverage topology \(J\), the coefficient ring \(k\), and the chart atlas. The same source generally yields different architecture schemes when any of these change: \(X_S^{V,U,J,k} \ne X_S^{W,U,J,k}\), \(X_S^{V,U,J,k} \ne X_S^{V,U',J,k}\), \(X_S^{V,U,J,k} \ne X_S^{V,U,J',k}\) in general.

Restriction of rings and gluing of schemes

Definition III.10.1 (Ring Restriction). A context morphism \(i : W' \to W\) gives a ring homomorphism \(\mathrm{res}_i : \mathcal{O}_X^U(W) \to \mathcal{O}_X^U(W')\), restricting coordinates on \(W\) to \(W'\). Under projection, a forgotten coordinate does not become zero; it merely ceases to be read in the restricted ring: 

forgotten coordinate != zero coordinate

Definition III.10.2 (Ideal Restriction). The obstruction ideal sheaf is compatible with restriction: \(\mathrm{res}_i\bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W)\bigr) \subset \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U(W')\). The reading of law failure restricts along context restriction. A projection may hide a defect from view, and that is all it does: 

unread defect != zero defect
unmeasured obstruction != vanished obstruction

Definition III.10.3 (Scheme Gluing). For a coverage family \(\lbrace W_i \to W \rbrace_{i \in I}\), a family of affine AAT charts \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W_i), D_{W_i}^U)\) that is compatible on overlaps \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W_i \times_W W_j), D_{W_i \times_W W_j}^U)\) glues — in the same sense as standard scheme gluing — to a locally ringed architecture geometry \(X_{\mathrm{glue}}\) in general. The glued object need not coincide with \(\mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{AAT}}(\mathcal{O}_X^U(W), D_W^U)\): in ordinary algebraic geometry, too, a scheme glued from affine charts need not be the Spec of its global section ring. Returning to a single affine chart on \(W\) requires explicit additional hypotheses: 

the glued underlying scheme is affine.
the canonical comparison X_glue -> Spec Gamma(X_glue, O_X) is an isomorphism.
O_X^U(W) identifies with Gamma(X_glue, O_X).
the glued decoration D_glue identifies with D_W^U.

Calling the result an ArchitectureScheme gluing requires not just section compatibility but the full chart compatibility of Definition III.9.2: open immersions, joint coverage, cocycle, and decoration compatibility. The sheaf descent of Part II reappears in Part III as the gluing of ringed geometry, and affine representability is an additional consequence of gluing, never an unconditional one.

Principle III.10.4 (Local Lawful Sections Need Not Glue). Independently selected local lawful sections do not by themselves yield a global lawful section; overlap compatibility and descent are required: 

forall i, exists s_i : W_i -> Flat_U(X)
does not automatically imply
exists s : W -> Flat_U(X)
with s|W_i = s_i

This local-global gap is the entrance to obstruction cohomology in Part IV.

Lawfulness-Ideal Correspondence

Theorem III.11.1

Lawfulness-Ideal Correspondence

Lean: proved under explicit assumptions
Statement
Assume obstruction soundness, obstruction completeness, axis exactness, witness coverage, a \(U\)-adequate cover when a cover is used, sheaf descent for \(\mathrm{Ob}_U\), and ring restriction compatibility. Then for an architecture section \(s : T \to X\), the following are equivalent: \(\mathrm{Lawful}_U(s)\); \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0\); \(s\) factors through \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U)\); \(\omega_U(s) = 0\); and the required \(\mathrm{Sig}_U\) axes vanish.
Proof idea
Each law \(L\) holds on \(s\) exactly when \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_L = 0\); vanishing of all selected witness ideals equals vanishing of their sum \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U = 0\); ideal pullback vanishing equals factorization through the zero locus; soundness and completeness identify section-wise witness vanishing with zero obstruction valuation; axis exactness and witness coverage identify it with required-signature zero.

Correspondence.LawfulnessIdealCorrespondencePackage, lawful_iff_pulledObstructionIdeal_eq_bot, pulledObstructionIdeal_eq_bot_iff_factorsThroughLawfulLocus, factorsThroughLawfulLocus_iff_omegaU_zero, omegaU_zero_iff_requiredSignatureAxesZero, Correspondence.lawfulnessIdealCorrespondence in Formal/AG/LawAlgebra/Correspondence.lean.
Assumptions / boundary
The correspondence assumptions are explicit structure fields, not background axioms. The package also carries the one-directional bridges lawful_of_selectedWitnessIdeals_le_ker and lawful_of_generatedLawWitnessIdeals_le_ker. This is a certified bounded inference, not an unconditional universal claim.

This is a defect representation theorem: when the selected witness ideals represent law failure soundly and completely, lawfulness is exactly the section-wise vanishing of the obstruction ideal — 

lawfulness
  <-> obstruction ideal vanishing
  <-> factorization through lawful locus
  <-> zero obstruction valuation
  <-> required signature zero

Principle III.11.2 (Exactness Is Assumption). The correspondence is not an unconditional universal claim. It is relativized to the selected law universe, witness family, signature axes, coverage, descent, and exactness. Without soundness, a zero defect may fail to imply lawfulness; without completeness, a law failure may fail to produce a selected defect; without coverage, local vanishing may miss a required witness; without descent, local lawful data may fail to glue. This relativity is not a weakness of AAT: it is what makes explicit in which geometry, for which laws, and under which coverage the theory speaks.

Law equation realization

Theorem III.11.1 gives the correspondence between lawfulness and ideal vanishing. The realization theory strengthens it by one level: the witness-ideal vocabulary does not merely express the correspondence — it generates the coefficients, restrictions, and interpretations of obstruction.

Definition III.11.3 (Law Equation Realization). A law equation realization presents each law as a family of equations rather than a predicate. It consists of an observable presheaf assigning a commutative ring \(\mathcal{O}(W)\) to each context and a ring homomorphism \(\mathrm{res}_i : \mathcal{O}(W) \to \mathcal{O}(W')\) to each context morphism, preserving identities and composition; violation coordinates \(v_{L,a}(W) \in \mathcal{O}(W)\) for each law \(L\) and Atom \(a\) — the Atom-indexed realization of Definition III.5.1; restriction compatibility \(\mathrm{res}_i(v_{L,a}(W)) = v_{L,a}(W')\); the witness ideal \(\mathcal{I}_L(W) = \langle v_{L,a}(W) \mid a \rangle\) realizing Definition III.5.2; and the obstruction ideal \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}(W) = \sum_{L\ \mathrm{required}} \mathcal{I}_L(W)\) realizing Definition III.6.1. In addition, a family of displayed local readings is fixed: a base context \(W_{\mathrm{base}}\) and, for each index \(i\), a local context \(W_i \to W_{\mathrm{base}}\), an architecture object \(A_i\), a nonempty finite law support of required laws, and a defect observable \(d_i \in \mathcal{O}(W_i)\). Displayed names the components explicitly presented in the input data (synonymous with selected). The family is subject to one chart-local tie: 

law-defect tie:
  if L belongs to the law support of i,
  and L holds on A_i,
  then d_i in I_L(W_i).

The tie mentions one law, one chart, one displayed reading. Overlap equations, coefficient restrictions, and vanishing conclusions appear nowhere in the input. Satisfaction of the displayed required laws means: for each \(i\), every law in the law support of \(i\) holds on \(A_i\) — and since the support is nonempty and consists of required laws, this satisfaction is never vacuous.

Theorem III.11.4 — Certified bounded inference

Generated Obstruction Quotient and Restriction Evaluator

Statement
Fix a law equation realization. Then: ideal restriction — the ideal generated by \(\mathrm{res}_i(\mathcal{I}_L(W))\) lies in \(\mathcal{I}_L(W')\), and likewise for \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}\); the restriction conditions of Definitions III.6.2 and III.10.2 follow from the construction rather than being assumed. Generated coefficient — the quotient \(Q(W) = \mathcal{O}(W) / \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}(W)\) inherits restriction by the universal property and \(W \mapsto Q(W)\) is a presheaf of abelian groups, with the functor laws proved. Generated interpretation — the interpretation of a displayed reading is the quotient class \(\mathrm{interpret}(i) = [d_i] \in Q(W_i)\); interpretation is constructed, not free data. Vanishing — if the displayed required laws are satisfied, then \([d_i] = 0\) for every \(i\). Faithfulness — \([d_i] = 0\) iff \(d_i \in \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}(W_i)\); vanishing is ideal membership itself, not definitional collapse. Nondegeneracy — if \(d_i \notin \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}(W_i)\) then \([d_i] \ne 0\). Restriction evaluator — if the displayed required laws are satisfied, then on any common refinement \(Z\) (maps \(g_i : Z \to W_i\), \(g_j : Z \to W_j\) commuting with the maps to \(W_{\mathrm{base}}\)), \(\mathrm{res}_{g_i}([d_i]) = \mathrm{res}_{g_j}([d_j])\).
Proof idea
Ideal restriction is a computation of images from the restriction compatibility of violation coordinates. The functor laws follow from the universal property of the quotient. Vanishing is three steps: pick \(L\) in the nonempty law support; the law-defect tie gives \(d_i \in \mathcal{I}_L\); \(L\) is required, so \(\mathcal{I}_L \subset \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}\) and the class dies in the quotient — no overlap equations or descent are consumed. Faithfulness is general quotient-ring theory. The evaluator follows because both sides are restrictions of zero classes. What matters is that this proof exists at all: in a vocabulary where interpretation is free data, the agreement is underivable in principle (Principle III.11.6).
Assumptions
Exactly the realization data of Definition III.11.3, including the chart-local law-defect tie and nonempty required law supports. The evaluator is a conclusion of the theorem, not an input hypothesis.
Boundary
A certified bounded inference relative to the fixed realization. On the Lean side, the AG Part III index row for Theorem III.11.1 records the bridge Correspondence.displayedRequiredLawsHoldOn_defect_mem_localObstructionIdeal from displayed required laws to generated obstruction ideal membership; no separate III.11.4 index row is claimed here.

Corollary III.11.5 (Defect Class Detector). Under a law equation realization, if \([d_i] \ne 0\), then no law in the law support of \(i\) holds on \(A_i\). A nonzero class is a certificate of failure of the displayed required laws. The converse is not claimed: what is equivalent to vanishing is not the holding of the law but the ideal membership of the defect (faithfulness).

Principle III.11.6 (Predicate Vocabulary Does Not Generate). In a vocabulary where laws are opaque predicates and interpretation is free data, there is no uniform construction of a restriction evaluator for the totality of reading data: 

predicate does not determine the equation.
no coefficient, no cohomology.

This absence is not the claim that an individual reading can never have an evaluator; it is the absence of a uniform construction. The witness-ideal discipline of the fifth section and the realization of Definition III.11.3 are the vocabulary that fills this absence.

Counterexamples and non-conclusions

Counterexample (cancellation). Over a coefficient system without the no-cancellation discipline, a sum of witness coordinates can vanish while the individual witnesses do not — in characteristic 2, two present cycle witnesses sum to zero. This is why lawfulness is defined by \(s^{*}\mathcal{I}_L = 0\), not by \(s^{*}(\delta_L) = 0\) (Definition III.5.3, Principle III.5.6).

Counterexample (gluing). Local lawful sections on each \(W_i\) do not force a global lawful section on \(W\) (Principle III.10.4), and a scheme glued from affine charts need not be the Spec of its global section ring (Definition III.10.3). Both failures are genuine geometric phenomena, not bookkeeping defects.

Non-conclusion (projection). A coordinate forgotten by projection is not a zero coordinate, an unread defect is not a zero defect, and an unmeasured obstruction is not a vanished obstruction (Definitions III.10.1 and III.10.2). Restriction limits what is read; it proves nothing about what is unread.

Non-conclusion (totality). \(\mathrm{Flat}_U(X)\) is lawfulness within the selected \(U\), \(J\), witness family, and signature axes (Principle III.7.3). Zero on a selected axis is not total zero, and no claim is made about unselected law universes or unselected readings (Example III.3.3, Principle III.5.7).

Conclusion of Part III

Part III added a sheaf of commutative rings to the architecture geometry of Part II, defined the obstruction ideal sheaf as the sum of selected law witness ideals, and defined the lawful locus as its zero set:

\[ X \longmapsto (X, \mathcal{O}_X^U), \qquad \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U \subset \mathcal{O}_X^U, \qquad \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V\bigl(\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{Ob}}^U\bigr). \]

AAT thereby acquires the tower

ArchitectureGeometry
  -> Ringed AAT Topos
  -> Affine AAT Charts
  -> ArchitectureScheme
  -> LawfulLocus

and its core reads:

law is equation.
obstruction is ideal.
lawfulness is vanishing.
the equation generates coefficient and restriction.
architecture scheme is chart-compatible ringed geometry.

The remaining question is whether local lawful sections glue to a global lawful section. That question is Part IV: obstruction cohomology.
