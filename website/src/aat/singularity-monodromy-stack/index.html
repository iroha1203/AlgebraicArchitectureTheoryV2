Part VI

Singularity, Monodromy, and Stacks

Part V read law conflict as derived non-transversality: \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_i^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_U, \mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_V) \) for \( i > 0 \). Part VI asks where that derived obstruction concentrates, how operation history leaves residue, and when no canonical decomposition exists. How to read the claims

Every claim of Part VI is stated relative to a fixed atom vocabulary, law universe, coverage topology, coefficient structure, and selected reading. Three claim grades are kept distinct throughout.

Formal theorem
A mathematical proposition proved from the axioms, definitions, and stated assumptions.
Certified bounded inference
A relative inference holding under explicitly stated coverage, exactness, effectiveness, or witness-completeness assumptions. It is never an unconditional absolute claim. The main theorems of this chapter — the singularity criterion, the transport descent criterion, square monodromy nonfillability, the operation-invariant Galois correspondence — carry this grade.
Analytic reading
A way of reading a constructed geometric object through graphs, signatures, periods, or metrics; here, the normal cone reading and the architectural monodromy index.

Theorem labels on this page follow the canonical text. A label carries a Lean status badge only when the AG Lean index has a corresponding ledger row; a label without a badge is a mathematical statement in the canonical text and is not read as a current Lean proved claim. One statement of this chapter, the Kuranishi local model, is a theorem candidate in the canonical text and is marked as such.

1. From Part V to Part VI

Part V defined the derived intersection of lawful loci and read law conflict as derived non-transversality. For law universes \( U \) and \( V \) with obstruction ideals \( \mathcal{I}_U \) and \( \mathcal{I}_V \), the conflict classes are Tor sheaves of the structure sheaf quotients:

\[ \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) \;=\; \mathrm{Tor}_i^{\mathcal{O}_X}\!\big(\mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_U,\; \mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_V\big) \qquad \text{for } i > 0, \]

with the first-order conflict singled out as \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) = \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_U, \mathcal{O}_X/\mathcal{I}_V) \), computed on the derived intersection \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) \cap^{\mathbf{R}} \mathrm{Flat}_V(X) \). Nonvanishing of a selected class, \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_i(U,V) \neq 0 \) for some selected \( i > 0 \), shows a latent incompatibility in realizing \( U \) and \( V \) simultaneously. Part V also read repair as controlled movement through lawful loci, bad repair as obstruction transfer, and refactoring as derived-geometric deformation.

Three questions remain, and they organize this chapter:

Where is this obstruction located?
Which operation paths preserve or move it?
Can local decompositions be glued into a global one?

Part VI answers with three structures:

Singularity
The location where derived obstruction concentrates — the place where repair geometry is obstructed.
Monodromy
Path-dependent debt invisible to endpoint comparison — how operation history leaves residue on obstruction, semantic, and effect sheaves.
Stack
The geometry that keeps refactor equivalence and decomposition non-uniqueness, culminating in the decomposition gerbe and its descent failure.

With these, AAT speaks mathematically about hard-to-repair loci, operations that leave residue although the final states look the same, and architectures for which no unique correct partition exists.

2. Architecture stratum

Before locating obstruction, the geometry is cut into the pieces on which location makes sense.

Definition VI.2.1 (Architecture stratum). A stratum of an architecture geometry \( X \) is a part \( S \subset X \) cut out by a selected architecture role, boundary, operation pattern, semantic region, or runtime region. A stratum is a part of the architecture geometry relativized to atoms, laws, obstructions, signatures, and coverage.

Representative strata:

component stratum
boundary stratum
service stratum
shared state stratum
authority hub stratum
semantic boundary stratum
runtime interaction stratum
adapter stratum
legacy stratum
experimental feature stratum

Principle VI.2.2 (Stratum relativity). Stratification is relativized to the atom vocabulary \( V \), the law universe \( U \), the coverage topology \( J \), the selected signature axes, and the coefficient structure. The same source can look singular under one law universe and smooth under another: 

singular for U
  does not imply
singular for all law universes.

This relativity is the standing non-conclusion of the whole chapter: no stratum is called singular absolutely, only singular for a selected reading.

3. Cotangent and tangent complex

Definition VI.3.1 (Cotangent complex). For an architecture stratum \( S \subset X \) and a law universe \( U \), the cotangent complex is written \( L_{S/U} \). Here \( S \) is given as a derived stratum inside the \( U \)-law coordinate geometry, with a fixed inclusion or structure map \( S \to X_U \), and \( L_{S/U} \) is the cotangent complex relative to this \( U \)-law base. It reads how the stratum is constrained by the law universe: 

L_{S/U}
  =
  infinitesimal constraint complex of S relative to U

Definition VI.3.2 (Tangent complex). The tangent complex is \( T_{S/U} = \mathbf{R}\mathrm{Hom}(L_{S/U}, \mathcal{O}_S^U) \), where \( \mathcal{O}_S^U \) is the law coordinate structure sheaf on \( S \). It reads the deformation, repair, and refactor directions relativized to \( U \): 

H^0(T_{S/U}):
  first-order lawful refactor directions.

H^1(T_{S/U}):
  blocked refactor / lifting obstruction.

The cohomological grading follows the convention that \( H^1(T_{S/U}) \) contains the first obstruction classes.

Principle VI.3.3 (Tangent is not operation list). The tangent complex is not a list of operations. Concrete operations — rename, move, extract, split, merge — can represent tangent directions, but the tangent complex reads how those operations interact with law constraints: 

operation is syntax of change.
tangent direction is geometry of change.

4. Smoothness

Definition VI.4.1 (U-smooth stratum). A stratum \( S \) is \( U \)-smooth when every first-order lawful deformation in the selected deformation test class \( \mathrm{DefTest}_U(S) \) has the selected lifting and filling property along the specified nilpotent thickenings and base-change tests, and the selected obstruction class vanishes. With the lifting / filling predicate made explicit, the primary definition is: 

LiftFill_U(eta):
  eta has the selected lifting and filling property.

forall eta in DefTest_U(S),
  LiftFill_U(eta)
  and
  ob_U(eta) = 0

Proving smoothness from obstruction vanishing alone requires an effective obstruction theory on the selected deformation regime: 

ob_U(eta) = 0 -> LiftFill_U(eta)   (effectiveness)
LiftFill_U(eta) -> ob_U(eta) = 0   (soundness of the obstruction reading)

In a regime carrying both, the condition \( \forall \eta,\ \mathrm{ob}_U(\eta) = 0 \) is read as equivalent to the primary definition. A standard sufficient criterion: if

\[ \mathrm{Ext}^1_{\mathcal{O}_T}\!\big(\mathbf{L}\xi^{*} L_{S/U},\; M_T\big) = 0 \]

for all selected local bases \( \xi : T \to S \) and all selected deformation modules \( M_T \), then \( S \) is \( U \)-smooth. When the tangent cohomology computes the obstruction space in the fixed coefficient regime, \( H^1(T_{S/U}) = 0 \) is likewise a sufficient criterion.

Principle VI.4.2 (Smooth does not mean small). Smoothness is not size. A large component or service is smooth when its lawful deformations extend smoothly — large but smooth is admissible. A small boundary concentrating higher obstruction is singular — small but singular is dangerous. Size metrics do not enter the definition on either side.

5. Architecture singularity

Definition VI.5.1 (U-singular stratum). A stratum \( S \) is \( U \)-singular when the selected deformation test class contains a first-order lawful deformation whose selected obstruction class is nonzero: 

exists eta in DefTest_U(S),
  ob_U(eta) != 0

Assuming further that the obstruction reading is sound for lifting failure in the selected regime — \( \mathrm{ob}_U(\eta) \neq 0 \rightarrow \neg\,\mathrm{LiftFill}_U(\eta) \) — an actual selected lifting failure exists near \( S \), and in this sense \( U \)-singular negates \( U \)-smooth. The definition itself is the existential statement; its equivalence with not \( U \)-smooth is relativized to the exactness of the selected obstruction theory. The phenomenon: 

first-order refactor exists
but
higher-order lawful deformation is blocked.

The following are singularity criteria or analytic readings once an appropriate regime is fixed — not the primary definition: 

H^1(T_{S/U}) != 0 as an obstruction space.
Ext^1_{O_T}(L xi^* L_{S/U}, M_T) != 0 for some selected test.
tangent rank jumps.
normal cone has a selected nonzero obstruction direction.
local lifting fails for a specified deformation.
derived law conflict concentrates on S.

A non-conclusion guards the reading: \( H^1(T_{S/U}) \neq 0 \) means the room where obstruction lives is nontrivial — 

nonzero obstruction space
  !=
every deformation is obstructed.

Definition VI.5.2 (Normal cone reading). For the lawful locus \( \mathrm{Flat}_U(X) = V(\mathcal{I}_U) \) and a point or stratum \( S \), the normal cone \( C_{S/\mathrm{Flat}_U} \) reads in which directions \( S \) departs from the lawful locus. A repair direction is structurally effective only if it does more than improve a metric: 

repair direction v is structural
  only if
image(v) points toward vanishing of I_U in the normal cone.

Principle VI.5.3 (Singularity is repair difficulty). A singularity is not a place that looks complicated. It is a place where lawful deformation jams and repair directions are blocked by higher obstruction: 

singular stratum
  =
place where repair geometry is obstructed.

6. Singularity criterion

Theorem VI.6.1 — Certified bounded inference

Architecture Singularity Criterion

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

Let \( S \) be a stratum — component, boundary, service, shared state, authority hub, semantic boundary, runtime interaction. Assume: 

cotangent complex L_{S/U} is defined
tangent complex T_{S/U} is defined
selected deformation modules are fixed
law universe U is fixed

If for some selected first-order deformation \( \eta \)

\[ \mathrm{ob}_U(\eta) \neq 0 \;\text{ in }\; H^1(T_{S/U}), \]

then \( S \) is \( U \)-singular. Moreover, a non-smooth cotangent complex, a tangent rank jump, a nontrivial normal cone, or \( H^1(T_{S/U}) \neq 0 \) are read as singularity warnings: they show that a selected obstruction class can appear.

Proof idea
Nonvanishing of \( H^1(T_{S/U}) \) means the obstruction space for lifting first-order lawful deformations to higher order is nontrivial; a witness \( \eta \) with \( \mathrm{ob}_U(\eta) \neq 0 \) instantiates Definition VI.5.1 directly. When the cotangent complex is not smooth, a smooth lifting may fail to exist, so \( S \) is a singularity candidate where repair and refactor jam.
Theorem VI.6.2 — Certified bounded inference

Square-Zero Lifting Obstruction

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

Let \( S \to X \) be a \( U \)-lawful stratum with cotangent complex \( L_{S/U} \). Take a test object \( \xi : T \to S \), an \( \mathcal{O}_T \)-module \( M_T \), and a square-zero extension

\[ 0 \to M_T \to \mathcal{O}_{T'} \to \mathcal{O}_T \to 0. \]

The obstruction to lifting \( \xi \) to \( T' \) is a class in the Ext group of the pullback cotangent complex,

\[ \mathrm{ob}_T(\xi) \;\in\; \mathrm{Ext}^1_{\mathcal{O}_T}\!\big(\mathbf{L}\xi^{*} L_{S/U},\; M_T\big), \]

and

ob_T(xi) != 0
  =>
xi does not lift to T'.

If \( \mathrm{ob}_T(\xi) = 0 \) and the selected obstruction theory is effective, then lifts exist and their isomorphism classes form a torsor under \( \mathrm{Ext}^0_{\mathcal{O}_T}(\mathbf{L}\xi^{*} L_{S/U}, M_T) \), with automorphisms controlled by \( \mathrm{Ext}^{-1}_{\mathcal{O}_T}(\mathbf{L}\xi^{*} L_{S/U}, M_T) \). Consequently, if for some base \( T \), module \( M_T \), and first-order direction \( \xi \) the class \( \mathrm{ob}_T(\xi) \) is nonzero, then \( S \) carries a singular obstruction in its \( U \)-reading.

Definition VI.6.3 (Kuranishi map). Let \( S \) be a \( U \)-lawful stratum and fix a selected local base \( p : P \to S \), where \( P \) is a geometric point \( \mathrm{Spec}\,k \) or a selected formal stratum. Read the tangent and obstruction spaces through the pullback cotangent complex:

\[ \mathrm{Tan}_{S,p} := \mathrm{Ext}^0_{\mathcal{O}_P}\!\big(\mathbf{L}p^{*} L_{S/U},\, \mathcal{O}_P\big), \qquad \mathrm{Obs}_{S,p} := \mathrm{Ext}^1_{\mathcal{O}_P}\!\big(\mathbf{L}p^{*} L_{S/U},\, \mathcal{O}_P\big). \]

Fix a selected complete local deformation space \( \widehat{\mathrm{Def}}_{S,p} \). The map returning the higher lifting obstruction on the formal deformation germ,

\[ \kappa_{S,p} : \widehat{\mathrm{Def}}_{S,p} \to \mathrm{Obs}_{S,p}, \]

is the Kuranishi map, and \( \mathrm{Tan}_{S,p} \) is read as the tangent space of \( \widehat{\mathrm{Def}}_{S,p} \).

Theorem candidate VI.6.4

Kuranishi Local Model

Lean: statement-only

Suppose the deformation-obstruction theory on a formal neighbourhood of \( p \) is controlled by \( L_{S/U} \) and effective, and the complete local Kuranishi data \( (\widehat{\mathrm{Def}}_{S,p}, \mathrm{Obs}_{S,p}, \kappa_{S,p}) \) is defined. Then the lawful deformation germ near \( p \) is read as the zero fibre

\[ \kappa_{S,p}^{-1}(0) \subset \widehat{\mathrm{Def}}_{S,p}. \]

In particular, \( \kappa_{S,p}(v) \neq 0 \) means the direction \( v \) does not lift as a selected lawful deformation. When the repair transfer pairing of Part V,

\[ T_{\mathrm{rep}} \times \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \to \mathrm{TransRes}_{U,V}, \]

is in place, the first- or second-order component of \( \kappa_{S,p} \) is read as a selected transfer residue. This is a theorem candidate in the canonical text: the statement fixes the intended definitional and proof design.

Corollary VI.6.5

Singular Boundary

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

For a boundary stratum \( B \), if \( H^1(T_{B/U}) \neq 0 \), then \( B \) is a \( U \)-singular boundary. A small feature repair crossing such a boundary can generate higher semantic, authority, effect, and runtime obstructions.

7. God object reinterpreted

Definition VI.7.1 (God object). For a law universe \( U \), a stratum \( S \) is a God object when 

GodObject_U(S)
  iff
S is a singular stratum
where multiple law loci meet non-transversely.

The definition never references the size of the object: 

large object
  is not necessarily God object.

small singular boundary
  may be worse than a large smooth component.

Principle VI.7.2 (Size is not singularity). Size, line count, method count, and dependency count can be representations of singularity, but they are not singularity itself: 

metric is a reading.
singularity is derived geometry.

Defining the God object by size alone loses the structure of AAT. The counterexample is built into the definition: a large object whose lawful deformations extend smoothly is large but smooth and is not a God object; a small boundary where two law loci meet with \( \mathrm{LawConflict}_1(U,V) \neq 0 \) concentrated on it satisfies the definition despite every size metric being small.

9. Operation homotopy

Definition VI.9.1 (Refactor-equivalent endpoint). Two architecture states \( A \) and \( B \) are refactor equivalent, written \( A \sim_{\mathrm{ref}} B \), when they agree under the refactor equivalence preserving the selected invariants and essence. An operation path \( \gamma : A \to B \) whose endpoints agree under refactor equivalence is treated as an endpoint-equivalent path.

Definition VI.9.2 (Operation loop). An operation loop is an operation path whose endpoint returns to its start under refactor equivalence: \( \gamma : A \to A' \) with \( A' \sim_{\mathrm{ref}} A \). The set of homotopy classes of operation loops is the architecture fundamental group,

\[ \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X, U;\ \text{chosen homotopy data}), \]

representing the loop structure of operation history. It is built in stages: 

operation category:
  architecture operations and composable operation paths.

operation groupoid:
  reversible or equivalence-preserving operation paths are inverted.

homotopy category / fundamental groupoid:
  selected operation homotopy under refactor equivalence is quotiented.

pi_1^AAT(X,U; chosen homotopy data):
  chosen base architecture at a component of the fundamental groupoid.

Only once this construction is fixed can one ask whether an operation loop acts on a sheaf.

Definition VI.9.3 (Homotopy generator family). A family of relations generating the selected operation homotopy on \( \mathrm{Op}_U(X) \) is a homotopy generator family, written \( H_U(X) \). Representative generators: 

independent square:
  exchange of two independent operations.

same contract replacement:
  replacement preserving the same contract.

repair filler:
  filling between a repair path and a direct path.

identity insertion / deletion:
  inserting and removing identity operations.

associativity reassociation:
  reassociation of composition brackets.

Which generators are adopted is relativized to the law universe, the operation family, the refactor equivalence, and the coefficient transport.

Definition VI.9.4 (Presentation two-complex). Taking the 1-skeleton of the operation graph and attaching each generator of \( H_U(X) \) as a 2-cell yields the presentation two-complex \( K_H(X,U) \): edges are selected operations, 2-cells are selected operation homotopy relations.

Definition VI.9.5 (Presented architecture fundamental group). Fix a base architecture state \( A \). The edge-path group of \( K_H(X,U) \) is the architecture fundamental group relativized to \( H \):

\[ \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H,A) \;=\; \pi_1\big(K_H(X,U),\, A\big), \]

abbreviated \( \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H) \) when the base point is clear. This definition does not treat operation homotopy as an unspecified quotient: it is made explicit, relativized to the generator family \( H \). In the edge-path group, edges are formally invertible; a reversed edge does not assert that a corresponding inverse operation exists as an architecture operation.

Principle VI.9.6 (Homotopy presentation boundary). \( \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H) \) is the fundamental group of the selected operation graph and homotopy generator family. It contains no unselected operations, no unselected semantic equivalences, and no unconstructed paths: 

different H
  -> different pi_1^AAT reading.

10. Monodromy

Definition VI.10.1 (Monodromy action). For an operation loop \( \gamma \in \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H,A) \), assume a monodromy representation is given:

\[ \rho_{U,H,A} :\ \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H,A) \;\to\; \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Ob}_U) \times \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Sem}_U) \times \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Eff}_U). \]

Then \( \gamma \) induces automorphisms of the architecture sheaves, with \( \mathrm{Mon}_\gamma := \rho_{U,H,A}(\gamma) \): 

Mon_gamma(Ob_U)  : Ob_U  -> Ob_U
Mon_gamma(Sem_U) : Sem_U -> Sem_U
Mon_gamma(Eff_U) : Eff_U -> Eff_U

This action is called monodromy. Monodromy is not a structure the AAT geometry carries automatically: it is defined when operation loops come equipped with a representation acting on the obstruction, semantic, and effect sheaves. Absent such a representation, the theory stays silent about loop actions — a boundary, not a defect.

Definition VI.10.2 (Finite Gauss-Manin system). Fix a finite operation groupoid \( \mathrm{OpGpd}(X,U) \). Assign to each object \( A \) a coefficient object \( H_A = H^n(A, \mathrm{Ob}_U) \) or its selected finite-dimensional reading. When each operation path \( \alpha : A \to B \) carries a transport map \( \mathrm{GM}_\alpha : H_A \to H_B \) satisfying 

GM_id = id
GM_{beta alpha} = GM_beta GM_alpha

the functor \( \mathrm{GM} : \mathrm{OpGpd}(X,U) \to \mathrm{Vect}_k \) is a finite Gauss-Manin system. For an operation loop \( \gamma : A \to A \), the automorphism \( \rho_U^{\mathrm{GM}}(\gamma) := \mathrm{GM}_\gamma \in \mathrm{Aut}(H_A) \) is the Gauss-Manin monodromy.

Definition VI.10.3 (Monodromy debt). An operation loop \( \gamma \) has monodromy debt when \( \mathrm{Mon}_\gamma(\mathrm{Ob}_U) \neq \mathrm{id} \). This is path-dependent architecture debt invisible to endpoint comparison: 

same final architecture presentation
but
different obstruction sheaf state

Definition VI.10.4 (Measured square monodromy). Take a selected 2-cell \( \sigma \) of \( K_H(X,U) \) with boundary loop \( \gamma_\sigma \). When coefficient transport is defined along \( \gamma_\sigma \) and a selected axis \( x \) has a comparison reading, the square-level monodromy defect is

\[ \mu_x(\sigma) \;=\; d_x\big(\mathrm{Transport}(\gamma_\sigma),\ \mathrm{id}\big), \]

where \( d_x \) is the distance or equality-defect reading on the selected axis. The value measures how identically the 2-cell transports the selected coefficient reading.

Theorem VI.10.5 — Certified bounded inference

Transport Descent Criterion

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

Fix \( K_H(X,U) \), a base architecture \( A \), a coefficient transport functor, and a selected detecting axis family \( X_{\mathrm{axis}} \), with edge transport given on the operation graph. The edge transport descends to the presented group \( \pi_1^{\mathrm{AAT}}(X,U,H,A) \) if and only if the boundary transport of every homotopy generator 2-cell is detected as the identity: 

for all sigma in H_U(X),
for all x in X_axis,
  mu_x(sigma) = 0

iff

edge transport descends to
  rho_{U,H,A} : pi_1^AAT(X,U,H,A) -> Aut(Coeff_U)

Here \( X_{\mathrm{axis}} \) is a selected detecting family reflecting transport equality.

Proof idea
The universal property of the presented group. Transport on the free path groupoid is determined by edges, and the 2-cells of \( H \) are the relations. If every relation's boundary transport is the identity, transport factors through the quotient group; conversely, factoring through the quotient sends each relation to the unit, so every \( \mu_x(\sigma) \) vanishes.

Definition VI.10.6 (Architectural Monodromy Index). Fix a finite measured square family \( \Sigma = \{\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n\} \) and positive weights \( w_i \). The Architectural Monodromy Index is the weighted sum

\[ \mathrm{AMI}_x(\Sigma) \;=\; \sum_i w_i \, \mu_x(\sigma_i). \]

When several axes are bundled, the axis aggregation policy is part of the measurement profile. The AMI is an analytic reading: it reads constructed transport data and issues no claim about unselected squares or axes.

Theorem VI.10.7 — Certified bounded inference

Square Monodromy Nonfillability

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

Assume:

K_H(X,U) is fixed.
sigma is a selected 2-cell or selected square boundary.
coefficient transport along boundary sigma is defined.
selected axis x detects transport equality.
mu_x(sigma) != 0.

Then, under the fixed \( K_H(X,U) \), transport, and axis exactness, the boundary loop of \( \sigma \) cannot be read as a filling with identity transport on the selected axis \( x \): 

mu_x(sigma) != 0
  =>
selected x-axis filling is refuted.

The refutation is axis-relative: the theorem is silent about fillings on axes outside the selected detecting family.

11. Monodromy debt theorem

Theorem VI.11.1

Monodromy Debt

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

For an operation loop \( \gamma : A \to A \), assume:

gamma is endpoint-equivalent under refactor equivalence
Mon_gamma(Ob_U) is defined

same endpoint
  does not imply
same architecture continuation.
Proof idea
Endpoint comparison sees only the start and end of the path; monodromy sees the automorphism the path induces on the sheaves — the action on \( \mathrm{Ob}_U \), \( \mathrm{Sem}_U \), \( \mathrm{Eff}_U \). If \( \mathrm{Mon}_\gamma(\mathrm{Ob}_U) \neq \mathrm{id} \), the obstruction sheaf reading has changed across the loop; the presentation returns refactor-equivalent, but the architecture continuation is not the same.
Example
A migration executed and rolled back through a contract-changing path: the endpoints agree under refactor equivalence, yet the transported obstruction reading differs — the loop is a debt carrier that no endpoint diff detects.

15. Decomposition stack

Definition VI.15.1 (Decomposition stack). For an architecture geometry \( X \), the stack of admissible decompositions is \( \mathrm{Dec}_U(X) \): for a local context \( W \), the value \( \mathrm{Dec}_U(X)(W) \) is the groupoid of decompositions admissible on \( W \). Examples of decompositions:

16. No canonical decomposition

Definition VI.16.1 (Gerbe obstruction). Consider a cover \( \mathcal{U} = \{ W_i \to W \} \) on which local decompositions exist: each \( \mathrm{Dec}_U(X)(W_i) \) is nonempty, but the cocycle condition on overlaps and triple overlaps may break. In that case a gerbe obstruction class is defined,

\[ [\mathrm{gerbe}_U(X)] \;\in\; H^2\big(X,\ \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Dec}_U)\big), \]

where \( \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Dec}_U) \) is the automorphism sheaf of the decomposition groupoid. This \( H^2 \) is in general not abelian sheaf cohomology: it is a non-abelian gerbe obstruction relativized to the decomposition groupoid. When the gerbe is banded by an abelian sheaf \( A \), the class lands in ordinary abelian sheaf cohomology, \( [\mathrm{gerbe}_U(X)] \in H^2(X, A) \); otherwise it is treated as a non-abelian cohomology / stack obstruction class rather than an element of a cohomology group.

Theorem VI.16.2

No Canonical Decomposition

Lean: packaged (assumption-relative)

Assume:

local decompositions exist
Aut(Dec_U) is defined
gerbe obstruction class is defined

If \( [\mathrm{gerbe}_U(X)] \neq 0 \), then \( X \) admits no global canonical decomposition relativized to \( U \): 

local decompositions exist
but
no global canonical decomposition.
Proof idea
If the local decompositions came from a global canonical decomposition, the overlap isomorphisms and the triple overlap cocycle condition would cohere, forcing the gerbe obstruction to vanish: global canonical decomposition implies \( [\mathrm{gerbe}_U(X)] = 0 \). The hypothesis says the class is nonzero, so the local decompositions do not glue into a global canonical one.

not ignorance.

In that situation, searching for the one true partition is itself the wrong move. What is called for is reading the decomposition stack and the gerbe obstruction.

Chapter summary

Part VI formalized how the derived obstruction of Part V appears inside architecture geometry. Singularity locates where obstruction concentrates; monodromy shows how operation history leaves residue; stacks keep refactor equivalence and decomposition non-uniqueness. The three main theorems: 

Singularity Criterion:
  H^1(T_{S/U}) != 0
  or cotangent complex is not smooth
  implies S is U-singular.

Monodromy Debt:
  Mon_gamma(Ob_U) != id
  implies endpoint-equivalent loop has hidden architecture debt.

No Canonical Decomposition:
  [gerbe_U(X)] != 0
  implies no global canonical decomposition.

With these, AAT handles hard-to-repair loci, history-dependent debt, and non-unique decomposition as structures of architecture geometry. The next question is how to represent, measure, and analyze these structures — through representations, periods, metrics, and analytic shadows — which leads into Part VII.
