The evaluator catalog

Ten measurements, one question each.

A LawPolicy selects evaluators from a fixed registry. Each evaluator answers one structural question over the declared evidence, has explicit conditions under which it stays silent, and anchors to a specific theorem in the AAT text. This page is the catalog.

How to read this catalog

Every entry follows the same shape: the engineer's question the evaluator answers, what a nonzero result names concretely, and when the evaluator is silent by design. The theory anchor is a one-way link — it explains the measurement for readers who want the underlying statement, but no entry requires it. Verdicts always land in the five-valued vocabulary; "silent" below means not_computed or unmeasured with a typed boundary, never an error.

Evaluators marked analytic reading produce numbers to inspect, not verdicts to gate on — the packet keeps the two strictly apart.

Gluing and coherence

  • ag.cech-obstruction — the headline measurementDo the conventions each context declares glue into one global convention across the selected cover, or is there a mismatch loop no local fix can resolve? Nonzero names the exact overlap edges and evidence atoms carrying the obstruction (classSupport), plus the shape of the cover's nerve. When the cover has no cycles and no triple overlaps and every restriction check passes, it can conclude COVER_SHAPE_EXCLUDES_GLUING_OBSTRUCTION — the cover itself rules the obstruction out. This is the headline evaluator of the one-cent drift, which now runs it alongside the SAGA evaluators below. Anchor: Part IV, §12 (Čech H¹ over the selected cover, F₂ coefficients).
  • ag.coherence-obstruction — one level upWhere pairwise agreements exist, do they cohere on triple overlaps? Runs only when the selected cover supplies a finite triple-overlap 2-skeleton (and at most 12 contexts); silent without one. Existence-level reading of H² coherence. Anchor: Part IV, §10.

Sections and restrictions

  • ag.restriction-compatibilityDoes what a context declares survive restriction to its sub-contexts — is each declared value still the declared value after you narrow the view? Checks every restriction edge of the finite poset; silent when the cover has no restriction edges. Anchor: Part II (presheaf restriction on the finite poset site).
  • ag.section-factorizationCan a selected witness assignment actually be realized as a section — does the assignment factor through the declared structure? Silent when the profile's witness-assignment atom is absent from the evidence. Anchor: Part II.
  • ag.boundary-residueWhen a feature extension sits on top of a core, does work at the feature boundary leave residue that the boundary accounting cannot absorb? Requires the cover to classify patches as core / feature / boundary; silent without that classification. Anchor: Part IV, §§8–9 and §13 (boundary pairing and Stokes accounting).

Conflict and repair targets

  • ag.law-conflict-torTwo selected laws constrain the same variables — do they merely overlap, or do they genuinely conflict in a way that no assignment satisfies both? Nonzero carries the conflict's support. Requires generators for exactly the common ambient law pair; fails closed on anything else. Anchor: Part V (Tor₁ as the derived measure of law conflict).
  • ag.square-free-repairWhich minimal combinations does the declared law surface forbid, which of those supports are observed in the selected ArchMap, and what hitting sets does that declaration enumerate? An observed support yields measured_nonzero; when no declared generator has an observed support atom, the row is measured_zero with a silence_by_design statement. The hitting-set list remains declaration-derived: it is a combinatorial repair-target surface (REPAIR_TARGETS_IDENTIFIED), not a list computed from observed combinations and not a claim about which cut is semantically best. Anchor: Part III, §5 (square-free monomial reading; Alexander duality for minimal hitting sets).

Analytic readings — numbers, not verdicts

  • ag.sheaf-laplacianWhere does disagreement concentrate, even when nothing is strictly obstructed? Computes the finite sheaf-Laplacian spectrum and a Hodge-style decomposition as an analytic reading. The assumption ledger marks "harmonic debt minimality" as theorem-candidate — the packet carries that status rather than hiding it. Anchor: Part VIII, §8.
  • ag.period-stokes-auditDo the supplied finite period values balance the books — does the boundary accounting identity hold on the declared model, under one fixed coefficient reading? An audit of supplied accounting values, not a discovery pass. Anchor: Part IV §13 and Part VII §5.

Repair descent

  • ag.saga-descentGiven a declared repair plan, does the repaired assignment actually glue within the selected finite complex? REPAIR_GLUES_WITHIN_SELECTED_COMPLEX when it does; MEASURED_NONGLUING_RESIDUAL with concrete support when it does not. With the full supplied-slot set validated (triple overlaps, F₂ coefficient, true-sheaf certificate, gluing data), the vocabulary sharpens to the residual class itself: MEASURED_NONGLUING_RESIDUAL_CLASS names a nonzero class in Z1/B1, and supplied comparison data can transport it (saga-comparison:h1-transfer). Runs on a validated archsig-repair-plan/v0.5.3 (see Contracts); without a plan it stays not_computed with a silence_by_design boundary. It measures the plan you declared — it does not synthesize repairs or claim the plan is minimal. Anchor: Part X (semantic repair descent; the Lean-proved core of the descent theorems).
  • ag.saga-groundedAre the displayed laws actually holding on the selected charts, by the declared raw-value check? The grounded law surface supplies a skeleton, per-chart defect observables, and a holdsCriterion; the evaluator reports DISPLAYED_LAWS_HOLD_ON_SELECTED_CHARTS or MEASURED_LAW_DEFECT_AT_CHART with the raw witness atoms, and emits the law-grounded conclusions packet. In the one-cent drift this is the trap made measurable: every chart's own money law holds while the residual class is nonzero. Anchor: Part X (grounded conclusions; detector soundness).
  • ag.harmonic-debtHow expensive is the essential part of the defect? An analytic reading (never a structural verdict) over declared cellular cochains: the harmonic component of the observed deviation is the part no local transfer removes, and with a supplied cost model (analytic.costModel, Lipschitz constant) it yields essentialRepairLowerBound. Without the cost model the lower bound stays silence_by_design. In the demo the head state's bound is the quarter-cent rounding residue; the repaired state books it explicitly and the bound drops to zero. Anchor: Part VIII, §8 (harmonic debt; repair lower bounds).

The foundation row

Alongside the selected evaluators, every run emits an ag.foundation row: whether the declared evidence and profile actually support AG measurement at all (AG_MEASUREMENT_FOUNDATION_READY_UNDER_PROFILE). When foundation checks fail, evaluator rows depend on violated assumptions and the gate sees violated_assumption_dependency — the run degrades loudly, not silently.

Selection is per-repository: a policy that selects only ag.cech-obstruction is complete and normal (the shipped demo does exactly that). Add evaluators when your evidence and your review questions justify them — the LawPolicy page covers how selection is declared and justified.
