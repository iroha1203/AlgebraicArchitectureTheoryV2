Artifacts

ArchSig reads a finite-poset-site ArchMap with a selected LawPolicy measurement profile, then emits a measurement packet, summary, insight report, viewer data, and run manifest.

  • archmap/v0.5.0Source-grounded atoms, contexts, covers, declared sources, and extraction doctrine.
  • law-policy/v0.5.0The selected evaluator manifests, laws, measurement profile, policies, basis refs, and non-conclusions.
  • archsig-measurement-packet.jsonStructural verdicts, computed invariants, analytic readings, assumptions, and non-conclusions.
  • archsig-analysis-summary.jsonThe compact first reading surface for Skills and human reviewers.
  • archsig-run-manifest.jsonGenerated artifact list, input digests, validation summary, and run metadata.

Read summary first

The summary is designed to keep the review focused. It answers what was measured before the reader opens detailed measurement rows.

  1. verdictThe measured conclusion for the supplied ArchMap and LawPolicy.
  2. qualityMeasurementCounts for nonzero axes, hotspots, recurrent pressure, holes, holonomy loops, workflow risk, transfer bridges, and coverage gaps.
  3. distanceInsightsThe engineer-facing distance reading: structural center, change-sensitive areas, policy obstruction state, blocked evidence, recommended refs, and comparison-needed claims.
  4. distanceDiagnosisThe measurement-state reading for Part IV distance families, including moved axes, blocked results, family summaries, and primary distance refs.
  5. trendDiagnosisWhere pressure concentrates and which cross-axis insights appear.
  6. richDominantFindingsCompact packet refs for spectrum hotspots, architectural holes, nonzero holonomy, and structural readings.
  7. sourceRefsThe bridge from summary to source evidence and selected measurement rows.
  8. actionQueueThe prioritized review queue with detail refs, including generated, spectrum, homotopy, and structural refs.
  9. measurementBasisInput refs, validation results, coverage gaps, measured boundaries, and selected policy refs.

Open measurement detail

Open archsig-measurement-packet.json and the insight report when a finding needs source refs, assumptions, missing support, or a selected measurement row.

  • structuralVerdictShows measured, nonzero, unknown, unmeasured, and not-computed structural rows.
  • sourceRefsPoint the agent back to files, symbols, docs, tests, traces, or other source evidence.
  • missingEvidence and coverage gapsKeep unknown evidence visible instead of treating absence as zero.
  • nonConclusionsState what the measurement output is not claiming.

Measurement families

Families are different views over the same ArchMap plus LawPolicy analysis. Some rows use exact packet field names; grouped rows collect related readings that currently appear across packet sections rather than as one single top-level field.

Repair / operation readingsRepair candidates, operation deltas, transferred obstructions, side-effect axes, and readiness.

Measurement basis

Every reading is relative to the supplied evidence and selected policy. Before acting on a finding, check what was measured.

  • Observed source stateWhich ArchMap was read, which source refs were included, and which observations were source-backed.
  • Selected policyWhich LawPolicy selected the evaluator pack, evaluator ids, basis refs, scope, and severity.
  • Validation stateWhether ArchMap, LawPolicy, and analysis validation passed, warned, or failed.
  • Coverage boundaryWhich gaps block a zero reading or require more evidence before repair planning.

Reading statuses

  • actionableThe bounded reading has enough evidence for review action.
  • needsReviewHuman or source review is required before implementation decisions.
  • blockedByCoverageGapMissing evidence must stay visible as an unknown remainder.
  • nonConclusionArchSig explicitly refuses a conclusion on that surface.

Measurement scope

Read every result as relative to the supplied ArchMap, selected LawPolicy, validation state, and coverage boundary. If the evidence or policy changes, the reading can change too.
