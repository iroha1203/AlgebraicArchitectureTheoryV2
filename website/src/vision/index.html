Research Vision

Redesign software engineering: make software evolution computable.

Software engineering studies software that never stops changing. Design, implementation, issues, pull requests, review, CI, operations, and AI-generated change all act on an architecture and reshape what it can become next. This research program builds software architecture as a geometry, and on that geometry, a dynamics of software evolution.

The goal

This program treats the state of an architecture, locality, gluing, obstruction, and repair as mathematical objects, and makes the trajectory of change — branching, merging, feedback, and governance — the subject of theorems and computation.

The vision continues the lineage of software evolution research that M. M. Lehman opened: software that keeps changing inside its operating environment. It connects that object to algebraic geometry, formalization, finite measurement, and development dynamics, toward a new computational foundation for software engineering.

The Rising Sea of software engineering

Grothendieck, instead of prying open hard problems one at a time, raised the level of abstraction until problems dissolved naturally — la mer qui monte, the Rising Sea. This program applies the same method to software engineering.

Tests, contracts, review, and CI handle local objects precisely. But the problems where every part passes and the whole still fails live in the overlaps between parts. So the program moves up from tracking code and modules directly to the level of atoms, laws, sites, sheaves, covers, and cohomology. At that level, "locally correct but globally broken" becomes an ordinary computation of gluing and obstruction classes.

The Rising Sea keeps engineering problems at the center of the theory. E2E failures, implicit interface assumptions, shared state, and broken temporal ordering acquire coordinates. Objects with coordinates can be turned into theorems, measured, and handed to tooling.

Why algebraic geometry

In software architecture, several laws act at once; locally valid constructions must glue into a global whole; and the system deforms while its breaks are repaired. Algebraic geometry treats exactly this ensemble as one mathematics.

This choice replaces the invention of a new metaphor or bespoke metric for every design problem with the use of existing mathematical structure. Interaction of laws becomes operations on ideals; globalizing local repairs becomes descent; what blocks it becomes a cohomology class. The higher the water rises, the more problems that looked separate on the ground appear as the same geometric phenomenon, with tools already built.

Atoms carry purity and portability

AAT axiomatizes the atom as a primitive architectural fact. The atom axioms provide existence and finite composition of architectural facts; laws impose equations and zero conditions on top. In this order, the definitions and theorems of AAT stand as pure mathematics, free of parsers, ASTs, repository layouts, or any particular analyzer.

Observation reads an existing atom family in a selected reading. ArchMap records finite, source-grounded atom evidence; LawPolicy records which laws are selected; ArchSig computes the mathematical measurement from both. Because existence, observation, policy selection, and computation are separate responsibilities, every conclusion can be traced to the stage of evidence it came from.

Atoms are also a common carrier across programming languages and frameworks. Facts drawn from Rust, Java, TypeScript, different ADLs, or different repository structures become, once mapped into the same atom vocabulary, subject to the same laws, the same obstructions, and the same theorems. A polyglot system is one architecture geometry.

Probabilistic reading, deterministic measurement

Supplying semantic atoms takes the ability to read how source is actually used. AI agents carry this local semantic reading, and the authoring skills for ArchMap and LawPolicy fix the selected targets, vocabulary, evidence, and procedure into artifacts — turning AI reading into traceable input.

ArchSig is the deterministic computational core that receives that input: the same input yields the same measurement, and Rust portability delivers it across operating systems and processor architectures. Chaining probabilistic generation into deterministic verification combines the reading power of AI with the reproducibility of mathematical measurement.

The research program

The program advances one chain of work.

  • AAT — architecture as geometry Builds software architecture as an algebraic geometry rising from atoms and laws: sites, sheaves, law algebra, lawful locus, obstruction cohomology, repair, deformation, and derived structure.
  • Lean — claims made checkable Fixes definitions, assumptions, theorems, comparison data, and finite witnesses as machine-checked declarations, keeping the mathematical text and the formal text in correspondence.
  • ArchMap / ArchSig — geometry meets finite measurement Records selected architecture evidence as finite artifacts and computes lawful locus, obstruction, coverage, and witnesses as reverifiable measurement, visualized in ArchView.
  • SFT — geometry set in motion Builds the dynamics of software evolution on AAT geometry: development traces as time, forces from developers, organizations, and AI, policy as governance, and measurement along trajectories.

In one sentence: AAT geometry makes software architecture a computable object, and SFT dynamics makes the trajectories and reachable futures of software evolution computable.

AI-driven development

AI agents generate many candidate changes in little time, expanding the speed, branching, and search range of software evolution. This program places that generative power inside architecture geometry and development dynamics.

AAT supplies the architecture objects, laws, obstructions, and repairs that a proposal acts on. SFT places proposals as forces on the development system and studies their trajectories and mergeability. ArchSig and FieldSig measure the selected evidence; review and CI act as control based on what is observed. Together they connect AI generation to the understanding and governance of software evolution.

One loop from practice to mathematics and back

The program connects questions born in engineering practice vertically to mathematics, formalization, measurement, and evolution theory.

Globalizing a local repair, interacting laws, merging parallel change, selecting among AI proposals, stabilizing over the long term: questions from practice produce definitions, theorems, certificates, and datasets. The results return to observation and experiment in practice and produce the next theorem candidates and evolution models. This loop is the engine of the research.
